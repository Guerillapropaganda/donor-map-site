### Strongest finding: Prime Healthcare HHS-OIG CIA #2

This is the cleanest direct conflict-of-interest receipt on the donor list, and it was not visible in the Phase 5b top-15 because the contribution amount ($20,000) sits below the max donor threshold.

- **$37.5M False Claims Act settlement, 2021-07-19.** Prime Healthcare, Dr. Prem Reddy (Chairman/President/CEO), and cardiologist Dr. Siva Arunasalam jointly resolved Anti-Kickback Statute and Stark Law allegations. Prime paid $33.725M; Reddy paid **$1.775M personally**; Arunasalam paid $2M. ([DOJ press release](https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/prime-healthcare-services-and-two-doctors-agree-pay-375-million-settle-allegations-kickbacks))
- **HHS-OIG Corporate Integrity Agreement #2.** Joint Prime/Reddy CIA executed June 2021, **estimated completion June 2026**. Required Prime to maintain a compliance program and engage an Independent Review Organization to audit physician-affiliate compensation. ([HHS-OIG CIA record](https://oig.hhs.gov/compliance/corporate-integrity-agreements/browse-cias/prime-healthcare-services-inc/))
- **The CIA was operative for Becerra's entire HHS tenure** (March 19, 2021 to January 20, 2025). It approaches its estimated completion date concurrent with the 2026 election cycle.
- **Prior matter (CIA #1):** Prime previously paid $65M to settle FCA claims of Medicare upcoding and unnecessary admissions across 14 California hospitals (2018, ~2018–2023 CIA). Reddy paid $3.25M personally; whistleblower received $17.225M.
- **Reddy donation pattern:** historically Republican; shifted heavily Democratic from 2021 forward, including $41,300 to DNC Services Corp on 2023-12-07 and approximately $123,000 to Democratic state parties on a single day 2024-06-14.

Editorial framing the data supports: a $20,000 contribution from Prime Healthcare (whose CIA was overseen by HHS-OIG during Becerra's entire tenure) to Becerra's gubernatorial campaign warrants the strongest scrutiny on this list. HHS-OIG operates with independence from the Secretary's office, and no public evidence of Becerra personally intervening in Prime's CIA was identified. ### Second-strongest finding: Cantu / DHR Health and Stark Law

The Cantu Cal-Access designation as "Developer" omits the operative HHS-policy fact: Cantu is co-founder and Board of Managers member at **DHR Health** (Doctors Hospital at Renaissance, Edinburg TX), one of the largest physician-owned hospital systems in the US.

- **ACA §6001 (2010)** amended the Stark Law to ban *new* physician-owned hospitals from billing Medicare and prohibit grandfathered hospitals from expanding without CMS approval. DHR (founded 1997/1999, grandfathered) was directly affected.
- **United States ex rel. v. Doctors Hospital at Renaissance et al., 4:11-cv-02565 (S.D. Texas, 2011–2022).** Federal qui tam alleged Cantu used below-market Lone Star Bank loans to physicians as leverage to force patient referrals to DHR, in violation of Anti-Kickback Statute and Stark Law. February 2022: court **dismissed FCA claims against Cantu and the Bank with prejudice**, finding allegations were based on publicly disclosed information and relators were not the original source. **Cantu was not found liable.** ([federal court opinion via GovInfo](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/USCOURTS-txsd-4_11-cv-02565/pdf/USCOURTS-txsd-4_11-cv-02565-0.pdf))
- **DHR Brownsville expansion fight (2022).** Federation of American Hospitals and American Hospital Association jointly opposed DHR's CMS request to add a Cameron County campus, arguing it violated ACA §6001's spirit.
- **Cantu's Democratic donor history.** Bundled $600,000+ for Hillary Clinton 2008 from Rio Grande Valley physicians; explicitly motivated, per his own characterization, as "protection money" against legislation restricting physician-owned hospitals.
