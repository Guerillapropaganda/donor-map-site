# Steadfast LA Foundation Structural Research Document

Current as of May 6, 2026. This document prioritizes primary and official-source records: Steadfast LA official pages, City of Los Angeles Mayor’s Office releases, Los Angeles Recreation and Parks board materials, City Clerk files, Bureau of Contract Administration rebuild lists, IRS bulk exempt-organization files, and LA Ethics Commission contribution data. Where a requested record could not be located in the permitted source set, the finding is marked **NO DATA**.

## Executive Summary

Steadfast LA is publicly described by its own site as a civic nonprofit founded by Rick Caruso and led by private-sector participants to accelerate Los Angeles wildfire rebuilding; its site separately describes “Steadfast LA Foundation” as an affiliated 501(c)(3) charitable organization for donations supporting modular housing, small-business grants, and the Palisades Recreation Center ([Steadfast LA](https://steadfastla.com)). The strongest IRS primary-source match located is a separate row for “STEADFAST LA,” EIN 33-3239336, in the IRS exempt-organization business master file, showing subsection code 04 rather than subsection code 03; that indicates the verified IRS row is not itself a 501(c)(3) record ([IRS EO BMF, eo3.csv](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-soi/eo3.csv)). No IRS Form 990, IRS determination letter, or ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer profile was located for a distinct “Steadfast LA Foundation” entity as of this pass.

The City relationship is documented through Mayor Bass’s official releases, a City Clerk file, a Bureau of Contract Administration rebuild list, and a Recreation and Parks board report. The most concrete city-facing project record is the January 15, 2026 Board of Recreation and Parks report for the Palisades Recreation Center donation project, which identifies PPRC Development Project, LLC as donor, cites Steadfast LA as the related recovery organization through Nick Geller’s Steadfast LA email, lists SWA Group and Gensler as designers, and values the project at approximately $40 million ([Recreation and Parks Board Report 26-017](https://recreation.parks.lacity.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/commissioner/2026/jan15/26-017.pdf)).

The LA mayoral-campaign cross-map found no exact-name LA Ethics Commission contribution records for the named Steadfast LA board/coalition participants to the six checked 2026 LA mayoral candidate and IE committees. Employer/corporate-affiliation matching did identify contributions by employees or people whose disclosed employers match Steadfast-affiliated companies such as Gensler, Disney, Amazon, Netflix, JPMorgan, CBRE, Latham & Watkins, and Parsons; these are not equivalent to board-member personal donations and should be treated as corporate-cluster signals only ([LA Ethics Campaign Contributions dataset](https://data.lacity.org/City-Ethics/Campaign-Contributions/m6g2-gc6c)).
