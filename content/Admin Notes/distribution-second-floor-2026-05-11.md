The hotel sale to San Manuel Investment Authority was announced **May 4, 2026** (1 week old when this dossier was written). The IEHP DOJ case was filed **Sept 2025** (8 months old). Hernandez's plea was **March 2026** (2 months old). The Bianco ballot seizure warrants were unsealed **March-April 2026** (2 months old). The window where someone else publishes this structural piece is open right now.

## Why the donor map is the right home

The donor map's whole editorial purpose is **structural assembly**. Beat reporters publish individual stories on individual events. Donor maps connect those stories through the money flows underneath them. The Second Floor beat is structurally identical in format to `/airbnb-bass` (the Wesson family pipeline at LA City Hall), `/mahan` (Silicon Valley AI investors via IE PACs), and `/the-hedge` (anti-wealth-tax billionaire coalition). ## Risk surface

**Defamation surface is low.** Every named fact traces to a primary source. The beat does not assert any quid-pro-quo; it documents money flows + asks open questions about the subjects' positions. The structural pattern is documentary.

**Political-pressure surface is moderate.** Bianco is a 2026 gubernatorial candidate with $5.26M in his committee. Pacheco is a former DA with active legal practice. Essayli is a sitting US Attorney. These are not low-leverage figures. Pitch tier order should be: investigative reporters with subpoena-proofing experience first (BuzzFeed, CalMatters), then local outlets, then national amplifiers.

**Legal-correspondence surface is moderate.** Expect cease-and-desist letters from at least one named party. Standard donor-map response: every fact is sourced, no quid-pro-quo asserted, all named subjects given an "open questions" paragraph to respond. Memory rule about defamation-prone language already applied at draft stage.
