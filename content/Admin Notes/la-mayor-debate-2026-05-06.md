# LA Mayor Debate Recap — 2026-05-06

**Setup:** NBC4 / Telemundo 52, Skirball Cultural Center, 5pm. One-hour format. Moderators: Colleen Williams + Conan Nolan (NBC4) + Enrique Chiabra (Telemundo 52). Five topics: wildfires, immigration, homelessness, public safety, quality of life.

**Qualified candidates** (5%+ in two NBC News-standard polls):
- Karen Bass (D, incumbent mayor)
- Nithya Raman (D, councilmember CD4)
- Spencer Pratt (R, reality TV / Palisades fire victim)

**Excluded:** Adam Miller and 11 other candidates. Miller's campaign publicly criticized the criteria.

40% undecided 27 days from June 2 primary. Race wide open below Bass.

## The dominant moment — pre-debate, not on stage

**2026-05-05** (debate eve), Bass interview:
- *"I feel like he's exploiting the grief of people in the Palisades, and I think that's reprehensible."*
- Claimed she had *"never heard of"* Pratt before this race.

**Pratt response (Fox News + emotional ad with his mother released debate day):**
- *"I'm not sure if Karen Bass forgot she let my house burn down and my parents house burn down and I had actual neighbors burn alive across the street from my childhood home."*
- *"The only grief is my grief, my community's grief that I initially started this fight on behalf of."*
- *"The most insane, psycho, diabolical thing I've heard in a minute."*

**Read:** Bass made a tactical error. Pratt's grievance is documented (his home + parents' home + neighbor deaths). "Exploiting grief" + "never heard of him" reframed the race around fire response and made Bass look defensive. The exchange dominated coverage.

## On-stage clashes

| Topic | Quote / detail |
|---|---|
| Wildfires | Pratt called Bass *"an incredible liar"* over wildfire characterizations. Moderators issued "no name-calling" instruction. |
| Immigration | Yes/no on non-citizen voting in local elections: Pratt no, Bass distinguished green-card holders from undocumented residents (*"when you say non-citizens, it doesn't mean they're here illegal"*), Raman aligned with Bass. |
| Homelessness | Raman attacked Inside Safe at **$85K-$100K per motel room per year**. Pledged to cut street homelessness in half by the 2028 Olympics. Bass countered Raman pushed Inside Safe into her own district. Bass on Raman's encampment position: *"It is completely unacceptable."* |
| LAPD | Raman opposed the 2023 LAPD pay contract. Said insufficient public safety results for the spend. |
| Quality of life | Limited pickup in coverage so far. |

1. **Inside Safe cost map.** Raman put $85-$100K/motel room/year on the record. Question: who collects that money? Motel chains, hotel operators, service contractors. Cross-reference recipient list against Bass's donor base. **NEW BEAT CANDIDATE — see emerging-beat-candidates.md C-009.**

2. **Steadfast LA / Caruso-Bass rebuild alliance.** The grief exchange made fire-response the dominant frame. Neither Bass nor Pratt is talking about who profits from the rebuild. **Existing beat candidate, awaiting Gemini Deep Research return.**

3. **LAPD 2023 contract donor map.** Raman opened the door. LAPPL is on Bass's endorsement list. Worth structural exploration if a contradiction-rich angle emerges.
