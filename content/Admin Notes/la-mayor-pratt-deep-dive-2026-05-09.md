## Corporate donors (regulatory significance)

LA City Charter Section 470 caps individual contributions to mayoral candidates at $1,800 (primary) but **prohibits corporate contributions entirely**. Two records appear on the donor list as corporations:

- **KOMOS TEQUILA** — $1,800 — New York, NY — listed as corporate name (no person, no occupation)
- **DELTA SPECIAL OPERATIONS CORP** — $1,800 — Santa Rosa Valley, CA — listed as corporate name (no person, no occupation)

Whether either is technically a sole-proprietorship LLC or a true corporation is open. If they are corporations, the contributions may violate LA City contribution rules. **This is a Tier 3 / David lane** — the legal characterization belongs to the FPPC, not to us. But the structural finding is that two records on Pratt's donor list are written as corporate names rather than individual contributors.

## UPDATE — APPARENT LEGAL VIOLATIONS (Tier 1 finding)

### Violation 1 — Komos, Inc. corporate in-kind contribution

**Contributor name in LA Ethics filing:** "KOMOS TEQUILA"
**Date filed:** 2026-02-01
**Amount:** $1,800 in-kind ("BEVERAGES FOR EVENT")
**Legal entity:** **Komos, Inc.** — confirmed via the brand's own website footer at komos.com ("© 2025 Komos, inc.") and terms of use ("KOMOS, INC" throughout).
**Jurisdiction:** New York (per Komos terms-of-use governing-law clause)

**Founder:** Mike Meldman (widely-reported 2021 launch; Casamigos co-founder, Discovery Land Company)

**Apparent violation:** LA City Charter §470 / LAMC §49.7.13 prohibit contributions to LA city candidates by "corporations, including foreign corporations, business entities, partnerships, joint ventures, syndicates, and associations." **Komos, Inc. is a corporation. The in-kind contribution to Pratt's campaign appears to violate this prohibition.**

**Compounding fact:** Mike Meldman — Komos's founder — also personally contributed $1,885 to Pratt's campaign on 2026-04-14 (filed under "Michael Meldman, Real Estate Developer, Discovery Land Company"). **Combined Meldman/Komos contributions to Pratt's campaign: $3,685 across two channels** (the corporate vehicle + personal max-out). Whether the Komos in-kind aggregates with Meldman's personal under FPPC contribution-attribution rules is open. Either way, the underlying corporate-contribution prohibition appears violated.

**Reporting issue (separate):** The donor was reported in the LA Ethics filing as "KOMOS TEQUILA" rather than its accurate legal name "Komos, Inc." Whether this rises to a separate reporting-accuracy violation is open.
