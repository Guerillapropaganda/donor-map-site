# The $35M Anti-Wealth-Tax Coalition — Why It Matters For The Donor Map

## What surfaced 2026-05-06

A Bay Area / LA billionaire coalition has put **$35 million** into a PAC called **Building a Better California** to defeat California's 2026 Billionaire Tax Act (Initiative 25-0024) and pass three counter-initiatives designed to kill or weaken it.

## Why this matters

The coalition isn't just opposing one ballot initiative. **Two named members of this coalition (Larsen and Resnick) are simultaneously the largest hedge donors in our existing donor-map work.**

- **Christian Larsen** funds Porter ($117,600), Mahan ($500K), Hilton ($39K) — three CA Gov candidates across two parties. AND drops $2.5M into killing the wealth tax.
- **Stewart Resnick** funds Becerra ($94K cumulative through 5/4), Villaraigosa ($72,800), Mahan ($25K), Swalwell (~$78K before withdrawal) — four CA Gov candidates. AND drops $2-2.5M into killing the wealth tax.

This is the structural shape the donor map exists to surface:

> **The same billionaires writing max-out checks to multiple gubernatorial candidates are simultaneously funding a $35M coalition to kill the tax that would tax their billions.**

The candidates aren't enemies. They're hedges. The wealth tax is the actual thing the donors care about. The candidates exist to determine which version of "billionaires keep their money" gets enacted.

## The wealth tax in plain terms

**California Billionaire Tax Act (Initiative 25-0024)** — proposed by SEIU-United Healthcare Workers West. One-time 5% excise tax on California residents with net worth over $1 billion (phase-out between $1B and $1.1B). Estimated ~$100B in revenue, earmarked for healthcare, education, and food assistance. Currently in signature-collection phase (25% as of March 2026 per Ballotpedia).

The Resnicks at $5.4B and Larsen (likely $1B+ in XRP holdings) are personally subject to the tax. Their $2-2.5M each is straight defensive capital.
