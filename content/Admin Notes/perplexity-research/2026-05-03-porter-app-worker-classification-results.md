# Katie Porter 2026 App-Based Worker Classification Audit

## Scope and source rules

This audit reviews Katie Porter's 2026 California gubernatorial record on app-based worker classification, AB5, Proposition 22, gig-worker labor rules, and related platform-company labor practices. The source set is limited to Tier 1 primary records and Tier 2 named-publication California political press. Where no Tier 1 or Tier 2 source was located, the relevant row is marked `NO DATA`.

## Method note

Cal-Access findings are from the California Secretary of State database export reviewed May 3, 2026, with the relevant receipt row extracted from `RCPT_CD` and committee-cover rows checked in `CVR_CAMPAIGN_DISCLOSURE_CD` ([Cal-Access database export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)). Official campaign and named-publication searches checked Porter's campaign site, KQED, CalMatters, Politico California, the Los Angeles Times, the San Francisco Chronicle, the Sacramento Bee, and LAist for AB5, Proposition 22, app-based workers, gig workers, independent contractors, Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, and Instacart.

## Bottom line

The primary record verifies the conflict lead: Uber Innovation PAC gave $150,000 to `Friends of Katie Porter for Governor 2026` on March 20, 2026 ([Cal-Access database export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)). The policy-side record is not yet matched to that contribution: permitted sources show Porter voting Yea on the 2021 PRO Act and signing a broader 2020 NLRB retaliation letter that footnoted Instacart worker protests, but no Tier 1 or Tier 2 source located in this pass states her 2026 position on AB5, Proposition 22, or whether app-based workers should be classified as employees or independent contractors ([House Clerk roll-call XML](https://clerk.house.gov/evs/2021/roll070.xml), [Jayapal House letter to NLRB](https://jayapal.house.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/6.24.20-Jayapal-Pocan-NLRB-10j.pdf)).
