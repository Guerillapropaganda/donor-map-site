# CPCA Advocates: Organizational Profile

**Prepared:** May 2, 2026 | **Context:** Sponsor of EMC Research gubernatorial poll fielded April 21–26, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

California Primary Care Association (CPCA) Advocates — formally registered under the IRS name **Californiahealth Plus Advocates** — is the 501(c)(4) advocacy affiliate of the California Primary Care Association (CPCA), a 501(c)(3) membership body representing community health centers (CHCs) across California. Founded in 2016 under the name CaliforniaHealth+ Advocates and rebranded in 2023, CPCA Advocates operates a companion state PAC created in 2021. In April 2026 it commissioned an EMC Research gubernatorial primary poll that showed former HHS Secretary Xavier Becerra — whom it had endorsed in November 2025 — tied with Steve Hilton at 19%. The organization is simultaneously a funder of the independent expenditure committee "Working Families for Healthy Communities Supporting Becerra for Governor 2026."

CPCA Advocates' About Us page states: "CPCA Advocates believes that every Californian is entitled to comprehensive healthcare coverage and access to high quality care" — language that is aspirational but does not explicitly endorse or oppose single-payer legislation ([CPCA Advocates About Us](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/ABOUT/CPCAAdvocates/ABOUT_/About_Us.aspx)).

### 4.2 Medi-Cal Managed Care Reimbursement Reform

CPCA's position on PPS (Prospective Payment System) modernization is active and documented:

- **APM (Alternative Payment Model):** CPCA maintains a detailed FAQ page explaining the FQHC Alternative Payment Model (APM), California's capitation-based alternative to fee-for-service PPS. CPCA has been deeply engaged with DHCS on APM implementation since its 2015 pilot authorization under SB 147 ([CPCA APM FAQs](https://www.cpca.org/CPCA/HEALTH_CENTER_RESOURCES/APM_FAQs.aspx?WebsiteKey=4190cf54-8e68-4858-9a19-e7253d7d94e1)).

- **PPS Modernization:** CPCA Advocates' regulatory priorities page explicitly lists "PPS Modernization" as an ongoing regulatory advocacy effort ([CPCA Advocates Regulatory page](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/ADVOCACY/Regulatory.aspx)).

- **Proposition 35 (2024):** CPCA and CPCA Advocates co-sponsored Proposition 35, the "Protect Access to Health Care Act of 2024," which permanently authorized a Managed Care Organization (MCO) tax and directed proceeds to Medi-Cal provider rates. CPCA Advocates' Patient Access to Care Fund contributed $1,729,053 to the Yes on Prop 35 campaign ([California Secretary of State — Prop 35 contributions](https://www.sos.ca.gov/campaign-lobbying/helpful-resources/measure-contributions/2024-ballot-measure-contribution-totals/proposition-35-provides-permanent-funding-medi-cal-health-care-services-initiative-statu)). Prop 35 passed with 67% of the vote in November 2024.

- **2025 Budget Advocacy:** CPCA Advocates' budget priorities include opposition to PPS rate cuts for undocumented patients (seeking a one-year delay) and opposition to dental cuts ([CPCA Advocates Budget page](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/ADVOCACY/Budget.aspx)).

- **AB 1113 Opposition (2025):** CPCA Advocates formally opposed AB 1113 (González), which would have required FQHCs to maintain a 90% "mission spend ratio." CPCA Advocates stated the bill "will create harmfully broad restrictions" ([Assembly Health Committee Analysis via TrackBill](https://trackbill.com/s3/bills/CA/2025/AB/1113/analyses/assembly-health.pdf); [CPCA Advocates Newsroom](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/ABOUT/MEDIA/CPCAAdvocates/MEDIA/News_Articles.aspx)).

### 4.3 FQHC Section 330 Grant Structure

CPCA actively provides resources to members on HRSA Health Center Program (Section 330) funding, including offering general Letters of Support for member grant proposals: "CPCA is pleased to provide a general Letter of Support to members for grant proposals related to Health Center Program (Section 330) funding. The Board of Directors has adopted the policy of limiting these Letters of Support to community clinics and health centers or consortia that are members of CPCA" ([CPCA Letters of Support page](https://www.cpca.org/CPCA/HEALTH_CENTER_RESOURCES/Letters_of_Support.aspx)).

CPCA Advocates works in "close partnership with the National Association of Community Health Centers (NACHC) to track and coordinate responses to federal legislation and budget actions. Key federal issues for community health centers include health center funding and workforce" ([CPCA Advocates Federal Advocacy](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/Advocacy/FEDERAL_ADVOCACY/CPCAAdvocates/ADVOCACY/Federal_Advocacy.aspx?hkey=6917c461-4daf-495b-9f6d-d9213c891f08)).

In May 2025, after the Trump administration reclassified Section 330 grants as a "Federal public benefit" (restricting immigrant access), CPCA Advocates was active in opposing these restrictions and protecting Medicaid/Medi-Cal access — running a "Stand United for Medicaid" campaign ([CPCA Advocates Medicaid Resources](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/Advocacy/GRASSROOTS_ADVOCACY/Medicaid_Action_Resources/CPCAAdvocates/Member_Resources/Medicaid_and_Medi-Cal_Resources.aspx?hkey=5bece400-45c2-467b-81d0-7c4136857d69)).

### 4.4 Positions During Becerra HHS Tenure (March 2021 – January 2025)

Xavier Becerra served as U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services from March 2021 to January 2025. Key HHS priorities during that period relevant to CPCA's mission included: expanded HRSA Section 330 funding, COVID-19 relief for FQHCs, telehealth flexibilities, Medicaid expansion, and the American Rescue Plan's FQHC funding provisions.

**Alignment with HHS/Becerra priorities:**

- **Section 330 / HRSA funding:** CPCA Advocates consistently advocated for increased and sustained HRSA Health Center Program funding — directly aligned with Becerra-era HHS priorities of expanding community health center capacity.
- **Telehealth:** CPCA Advocates' federal priorities explicitly listed "Support federal efforts to maintain post-PHE telehealth flexibilities in Medicare" — aligned with Becerra HHS's COVID-era telehealth expansion policies ([CPCA Advocates Advocacy Priorities](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/Advocacy/CPCAAdvocates/ADVOCACY/Advocacy_Priorities.aspx?hkey=79b6e861-2983-411b-9377-b693c0cda83e)).
- **Medicaid/Medi-Cal expansion:** Both CPCA and Becerra HHS strongly supported Medicaid expansion and coverage for low-income populations.
- **COVID relief:** CPCA worked with HRSA under Becerra's tenure on COVID Response Loan Fund and relief for FQHCs.
- **HRSA grant support to CPCA:** HHS TAGGS records list "CALIFORNIA PRIMARY CARE ASSOCIATION" as a recipient of HRSA award U8654986, associated with Section 330 Health Center Program support objectives ([HHS TAGGS](https://taggs.hhs.gov/Detail/CFDADetail?arg_CFDA_NUM=93527)).

**No identified opposition** to Becerra-era HHS priorities in CPCA's public advocacy record during this period.

**CPCA Advocates endorsed Becerra for California Governor** on November 10, 2025 ([CPCA Advocates Newsroom](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/ABOUT/MEDIA/CPCAAdvocates/MEDIA/News_Articles.aspx); confirmed on [CPCA Advocates Endorsements page](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/ADVOCATES_PAC/Endorsements/CPCAAdvocates/PAC/Endorsements.aspx?hkey=aab1f3fe-8207-49ca-8987-efa189a77879)). Becerra also appears on the [Becerra for Governor endorsement page](https://www.xavierbecerra2026.com/endorsements/) listing "CA Primary Care Association Advocates."
