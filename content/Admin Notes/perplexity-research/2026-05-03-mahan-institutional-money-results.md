# Matt Mahan Institutional-Money Audit

## Scope and method

This audit reviews Matt Mahan’s 2026 California gubernatorial fundraising structure using primary California campaign-finance records and named-publication political reporting. The core primary dataset is the California Secretary of State Cal-Access database export reviewed on May 3, 2026, with committee records deduplicated by committee, transaction ID, and latest filing ID to avoid double-counting amended filings ([Cal-Access DB export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)). The FPPC Top 10 Contributors page is used as an additional official snapshot, with its own published committee totals and committee IDs ([FPPC June 2026 Top Contributors](https://www.fppc.ca.gov/search-filings/top-10-contributors-list/june-2026-primary-election/)).

The main correction from this pass is that the “12 donors at $2M+ each” framing is not stable when amended Cal-Access filings are deduplicated. Several large donor totals double if both an original and amended Form 460 row are counted, but the latest-filing transaction-level view and the FPPC Top 10 page support lower current totals for many donors ([Cal-Access DB export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip); [FPPC June 2026 Top Contributors](https://www.fppc.ca.gov/search-filings/top-10-contributors-list/june-2026-primary-election/)).

## Bottom line

The verified structure is not “no voters,” but it is heavily institutional. Mahan has a real candidate committee with more than 1,500 unique donors and measurable small-dollar activity, yet the dominant money is the independent-expenditure layer: Back to Basics and Deliver account for about 69% of the verified candidate-plus-IE total in the May 3 Cal-Access extraction ([Cal-Access DB export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)). The cleanest editorial framing is that Mahan’s campaign combines a normal state candidate committee with an unusually concentrated, Silicon Valley-led super-PAC apparatus, and that several very large donor totals circulating in raw tallies should be corrected downward unless amended Cal-Access rows have been explicitly deduplicated ([Cal-Access DB export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip); [CalMatters](https://calmatters.org/politics/2026/04/california-governor-race-financials/)).
