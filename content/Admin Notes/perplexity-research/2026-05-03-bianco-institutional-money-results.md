# Chad Bianco 2026 Institutional-Money Audit

Scope: Chad Bianco's 2026 California gubernatorial campaign, with focus on independent expenditures, development and law-enforcement donor networks, campaign vendors, Form 700 availability, Oath Keepers history, ballot-seizure litigation, and donor-base coverage. Source rule: Tier 1 means primary government, court, SEC, IRS, FPPC, FEC, or Cal-Access records. Tier 2 means named-publication California political press allowed by the prompt.

Method note: Cal-Access findings below are from the California Secretary of State raw database export reviewed May 3, 2026. The relevant committee is `Bianco for Governor 2026`, filer ID `1479095`, and row-level identifiers are included where possible ([Cal-Access DB export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip), Tier 1, reviewed May 3, 2026). NO DATA means no Tier 1 or Tier 2 source was located in this pass.

## Plain-English bottom line

The strongest verified institutional-money finding is not a large pro-Bianco IE operation. It is a direct-candidate-committee donor structure anchored in Riverside and Southern California development interests, fuel and real-estate entities, and law-enforcement committees. The cleanest story line is the M&D Development plus Downs Energy aggregation question, because it is supported both by Cal-Access row-level receipts and by San Francisco Chronicle reporting on shared addresses, management links, and the possible FPPC single-source issue ([Cal-Access DB export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip), Tier 1, reviewed May 3, 2026; [San Francisco Chronicle](https://www.sfchronicle.com/politics/article/contributions-bianco-violate-state-campaign-rules-22160676.php), Tier 2, March 31, 2026). The second strongest story line is law enforcement: Cal-Access shows at least $143,720.50 in deduplicated direct law-enforcement committee giving to Bianco, and CalMatters separately framed his donor base as officers, police chiefs, business owners, and PORAC support ([Cal-Access DB export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip), Tier 1, reviewed May 3, 2026; [CalMatters](https://calmatters.org/politics/2026/04/california-governor-gop-candidates/), Tier 2, April 2, 2026). The IE layer remains a negative finding in this pass: no pro-Bianco or anti-Hilton Form 496 late-expenditure operation was identified in the searched Cal-Access records ([Cal-Access DB export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip), Tier 1, reviewed May 3, 2026).
