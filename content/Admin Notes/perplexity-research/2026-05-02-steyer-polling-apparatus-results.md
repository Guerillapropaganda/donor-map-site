# Tom Steyer 2026 California Gubernatorial Campaign: Polling Apparatus Investigation

**Prepared:** May 2026 | **Scope:** Primary-source investigation of internal polling vendors, payment mechanisms, and associated infrastructure for Steyer for Governor 2026 (FPPC ID 1485077)

---

## Executive Summary

Tom Steyer's 2026 California gubernatorial campaign retains **Global Strategy Group (GSG)**, a major New York-based Democratic polling firm, as its primary pollster. GSG's founding partner and president, **Jefrey Pollock**, is named in Steyer's campaign team announcement as the campaign's pollster ([Politico California Playbook](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-playbook/2025/11/19/maybe-this-time-tom-steyer-will-break-through-00658618)). The arrangement is confirmed in the Secretary of State's official CAL-ACCESS bulk export: FPPC committee **1485077**, "Steyer for Governor 2026," filed Form 460 records listing **Global Strategy Group, LLC** payments coded **POL / Polling** totaling **$865,142.27** across filing IDs **3114184** and **3138973** in the official `EXPN_CD.TSV` records ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

The premise that zero polling payments appear in Cal-Access EXPN_CD is therefore **incorrect in the official bulk records**: Global Strategy Group LLC is recorded directly in the campaign's expenditure filings under the `POL` expenditure code and "Polling" descriptions, with additional polling/focus-group support vendors also listed in the same Form 460 records ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

The five alternative possibilities are evaluated below and substantially ruled out with primary-source evidence, with narrow residual gaps noted.

## Section 1: Primary Pollster — Global Strategy Group (Jefrey Pollock)

Pollock has twice been named "Pollster of the Year" by the American Association of Political Consultants.

### 1.3 Direct FPPC Payment Record

Global Strategy Group LLC appears as a payee in the Secretary of State's CAL-ACCESS `EXPN_CD.TSV` file for **Steyer for Governor 2026 / FPPC 1485077**, and the relevant expenditure rows are explicitly coded as **POL** with "Polling" or "Polling - See Below if Itemized" descriptions ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)). The official rows total **$865,142.27** to Global Strategy Group, LLC across two Form 460 filings: **$155,742.27** in filing ID **3114184** filed February 2, 2026, and **$709,400.00** in filing ID **3138973** filed April 23, 2026 ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

The same official filings show additional polling, focus-group, and research-support expenditures: Crossroads Consulting ($78,975.26, research services), Blue Rose Research ($56,000), Sago ($20,898), Nelson Recruiting ($14,464), National Research and Polling Group ($8,215.98), Castillo & Associates ($8,000), Information Alchemy ($7,900), Discuss.io ($7,749), TargetSmart Communications ($7,000.09), and smaller focus-group travel/research support costs ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

### 1.4 How Payment Is Structured

The payment flows directly through the campaign committee (FPPC 1485077), not through any intermediary. The official CAL-ACCESS bulk export includes Form 460 filing IDs **3114184** and **3138973** for Steyer for Governor 2026, and those filings place GSG and other polling vendors on the candidate committee's own expenditure ledger ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

**There is no evidence that Steyer routes GSG payments through Bearstar Strategies, NextGen America, or any family office.** The payment appears on the campaign ledger directly.

## Section 3: Evaluation of the Five Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: Bearstar Strategies Running Internal Polling/Message Research Billed as Media or Consulting

**RULED OUT by primary-source evidence.**

Bearstar Strategies Inc does **not** appear anywhere in the Steyer for Governor 2026 payee records per [Transparency USA](https://www.transparencyusa.org/ca/committee/steyer-for-governor-2026-1485077-cao/payees). Bearstar's own disclosed payments on [its Transparency USA payee page](https://www.transparencyusa.org/ca/payee/bearstar-strategies-inc) show a total of $3,053,810 received from California committees — none of which include Steyer for Governor 2026.

Bearstar's principal work in the 2026 cycle is for **Eleni Kounalakis for Governor 2026** ($170,000) and ballot measure campaigns. Kounalakis's campaign additionally separately hired **David Binder Research** for polling ($110,000 from Kounalakis across committees per Transparency USA). Bearstar is a general and media consulting firm (Gavin Newsom's longtime firm) led by Ace Smith (CEO), Juan Rodriguez (CFO/Director), and Sean Clegg (Secretary) per [CA SOS registration (BizProfile, filed 1997, active)](https://www.bizprofile.net/ca/san-francisco/bearstar-strategies-inc). While capable of commissioning polling through subcontractors, it has no verified relationship with the Steyer 2026 campaign.

### Hypothesis 2: Non-CA Polling Vendor Paid Through Steyer-Connected Federal Infrastructure

**SUBSTANTIALLY RULED OUT.**

- **NextGen Climate Action Committee (FEC C00547349):** [OpenSecrets 2026 expenditure page](https://www.opensecrets.org/political-action-committees-pacs/nextgen-climate-action/C00547349/expenditures/2026) shows **no disbursements recorded for 2026**. 2023–2024 cycle top vendors were Flytedesk ($1.5M), NextGen Climate Action ($1.2M), Solidarity Strategies, and Scale to Win — no polling firms.
- **NextGen America (501(c)(4)):** [ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/461957345) shows the most recent Form 990 (FY Dec. 2024) lists **no polling or survey vendors** among disclosed expenses. NextGen America's publicly released polling uses **Impact Research** as a partner firm, not GSG.
- **Tom Steyer PAC (FEC C00745869):** This hybrid PAC was essentially dormant in the 2023–2024 cycle with only $105,430 raised and $118,784 spent per [OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org/political-action-committees-pacs/tom-steyer-pac/C00745869/summary/2024). No evidence of polling expenditures. [UNVERIFIED: 2025–2026 Tom Steyer PAC disbursements; FEC API blocked during this investigation.]
- **Tom Steyer 2020 (FEC C00711614):** The presidential committee is inactive (suspended in Feb. 2020). Its $8,869,408 in "Strategy & Research" expenditures went to **Merrill Strategy Group** ($9,997,182 total per OpenSecrets) and **Beacon Research** (confirmed in the OpenSecrets filing), not GSG. This 2020 polling infrastructure is **not** being repurposed for 2026 per available evidence.

The direct FPPC payment record for GSG makes routing through federal infrastructure unnecessary and no evidence supports it.

### Hypothesis 3: Family Office/Direct Personal Polling

**RULED OUT by primary-source evidence.**

California law requires campaign expenditures to flow through the candidate committee. The disclosed Steyer campaign committee (FPPC 1485077) shows GSG is paid directly through the committee on the campaign ledger. No evidence of any Steyer family office entity (e.g., Orcas Island, Fahr LLC, or related Steyer personal vehicles) paying for polling outside of the FPPC-registered committee structure was found.

**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED, but not for polling specifically.**

**DEFINITIVELY RULED OUT.**

At least three rounds of internal polling by Global Strategy Group are documented: January, February–March, and March–April 2026. The campaign committee directly pays GSG. Steyer's campaign team at launch named Jefrey Pollock (GSG) as the pollster.

## Section 7: The Apparent "Zero Polling Payments" Discrepancy

The user premise that FPPC 1485077 shows "zero recorded payments to political polling firms" in Cal-Access through May 1, 2026 warrants explanation:

1. **Direct web access vs. bulk export:** The live Cal-Access web interface is difficult to automate, but the Secretary of State's official bulk export is accessible and contains the relevant `EXPN_CD.TSV` expenditure rows ([California Secretary of State raw data page](https://www.sos.ca.gov/campaign-lobbying/helpful-resources/raw-data-campaign-finance-and-lobbying-activity)).
2. **Filing-period issue:** The first GSG payment total, $155,742.27, appears in Form 460 filing ID 3114184; the larger set of GSG polling payments appears later in Form 460 filing ID 3138973 filed April 23, 2026, which could be missed if the review used an older export or only earlier filing IDs ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).
3. **Payee-name and pipeline issue:** The relevant payee is "Global Strategy Group, LLC," not "GSG" or a California-local polling vendor, so a keyword screen limited to common California pollsters or "research" language could miss it even though the official rows are coded `POL` and described as polling ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

In short, GSG **is** in the public campaign finance record — it is not hidden, rerouted, or otherwise obscured. It is a material but still relatively small line item compared with Steyer's nine-figure campaign spending, and the official bulk records show both a named primary pollster and a broader polling/focus-group vendor ecosystem.

## Conclusion

Steyer's 2026 polling apparatus is **straightforward, not obscured, and fully consistent with standard high-budget California gubernatorial practice.** The campaign retains **Jefrey Pollock of Global Strategy Group** as its pollster — a decision announced publicly at campaign launch in November 2025 and confirmed by direct FPPC expenditure records totaling **$865,142.27** to Global Strategy Group LLC in the official CAL-ACCESS export through the April 23, 2026 Form 460 filing ([Politico California Playbook](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-playbook/2025/11/19/maybe-this-time-tom-steyer-will-break-through-00658618); [California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)). At least three rounds of internal polling have been conducted, and the campaign's filings also show a wider polling/focus-group support stack.

No evidence supports routing of polling payments through Bearstar Strategies, NextGen America, the 2020 presidential committee, or any personal family office vehicle. Bearstar is specifically aligned with a rival (pro-Swalwell) camp. The apparent absence of polling payments in any prior Cal-Access review most likely reflects filing-period coverage, payee-name matching, or review of the live interface rather than the official bulk `EXPN_CD.TSV` records.

The broader consulting apparatus is Fight Agency (media/creative), Debra Schommer Media Group (TV buying), Evolve Media LLC (digital), with operational continuity from the 2020 campaign via Heather Hargreaves (campaign manager) and The Merrill Strategy Group (direct mail).
