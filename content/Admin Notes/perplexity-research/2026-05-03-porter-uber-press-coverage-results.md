# Porter Uber Money: Press Coverage Accountability Audit

## Executive summary

The permitted source set supports the asymmetry hypothesis in its narrowest form: no named-publication California political press article was located that asked Katie Porter or Friends of Katie Porter about Uber Innovation PAC’s $150,000 contribution, and no 2026 voter guide, debate, or candidate-interview source located here put Porter on the record about AB5, Proposition 22, app-based worker classification, or gig-worker rights. The underlying contribution is verified in Cal-Access as a March 20, 2026 $150,000 contribution from Uber Innovation PAC to Friends of Katie Porter for Governor 2026, FPPC filer 1487598, Form 460 filing 3137978, line 9, transaction INC13 ([Cal-Access bulk export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

Comparator coverage is uneven, not uniformly robust. Becerra-Chevron and Villaraigosa-fossil-fuel donor conflicts received repeated named-publication coverage, including direct campaign-response material for Becerra and Villaraigosa, while Mahan-Armstrong/Coinbase appears in donor-base coverage but not as a directly framed accountability question to Mahan ([Politico California Climate](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-climate/2026/04/29/big-oil-california-governor-race-becerra-00899309), [LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2025-05-26/oil-villaraigosa-fossil-fuel-governors-race), [LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2026-04-27/google-co-founder-sergey-brin-confronted-gavin-newsom-then-launched-political-war)). The resulting count is: Porter-Uber accountability articles: 0; Becerra-Chevron articles mentioning the conflict: at least 5; Mahan-Armstrong/Coinbase donor-base articles: at least 3, direct accountability asks located: 0; Villaraigosa fossil-fuel donor/pledge articles: at least 4.

## Scope and method

This audit uses Tier 1 primary records and Tier 2 named California political press only. Searches covered Politico California Playbook and California Climate, CalMatters, LA Times, SF Chronicle, KQED, Sacramento Bee, and LAist through May 3, 2026. Cal-Access conclusions use the May 3, 2026 bulk export and are cited to the canonical state export URL ([Cal-Access bulk export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

## Bottom line

The record supports a narrow accountability-gap finding: as of the May 3, 2026 source review, Porter’s $150,000 Uber Innovation PAC contribution is primary-record verified, but no permitted California political press source was located asking Porter about the contribution or asking her for a 2026 position on AB5, Prop 22, app-based worker classification, or gig-worker rights ([Cal-Access bulk export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip), [CalMatters voter guide](https://calmatters.org/california-voter-guide-2026/governor/?module=next_button)). That silence is sharper because fossil-fuel donor conflicts involving Becerra and Villaraigosa have received repeated coverage and campaign-response treatment ([Politico California Climate](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-climate/2026/04/29/big-oil-california-governor-race-becerra-00899309), [LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2025-05-26/oil-villaraigosa-fossil-fuel-governors-race)). The Mahan-Armstrong comparator is only a partial contrast: the press has named Brian Armstrong/Coinbase in broader Mahan billionaire-donor coverage, but this audit did not locate a direct accountability question to Mahan about Armstrong’s $500,000 pro-Mahan IE contribution ([LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2026-04-27/google-co-founder-sergey-brin-confronted-gavin-newsom-then-launched-political-war), [Cal-Access bulk export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).
