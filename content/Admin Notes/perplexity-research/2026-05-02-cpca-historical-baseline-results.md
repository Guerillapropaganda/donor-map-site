# CPCA Advocates Historical Baseline: Is the 2026 Becerra Support Normal?

**Research Date:** May 2, 2026  
**Scope:** CaliforniaHealth+ Advocates / CPCA Advocates political activity, 2018–2026  
**Central Question:** Is the four-channel 2026 support for Xavier Becerra's gubernatorial campaign normal or unprecedented compared with prior California election cycles?

---

## Executive Summary

The short answer is **unprecedented at every level**. CPCA Advocates — a 501(c)(4) social welfare affiliate of the California Primary Care Association (CPCA), rebranded from CaliforniaHealth+ Advocates in January 2024 — has never previously endorsed a gubernatorial candidate, never previously commissioned a public political poll, and never previously seeded an independent expenditure (IE) committee. In prior cycles (2018, 2020, 2022, and 2024), the organization either lacked a PAC entirely or confined its electoral activity to Assembly-level candidate endorsements and modest direct PAC contributions. The 2026 cycle represents a qualitative and quantitative step-change across all four channels simultaneously, occurring against a backdrop of revenue that nearly tripled in two fiscal years (FY2023–FY2025) and an IE committee co-seeded alongside the California State Council of Laborers and led by a firm closely associated with Governor Gavin Newsom.

## 1. Organization Background and Branding History

CaliforniaHealth+ Advocates is a 501(c)(4) social welfare organization incorporated as an affiliate of the [California Primary Care Association](https://www.cpca.org/cpca/Advocates/ABOUT_/Values/About_CaliforniaHealth__Advocates.aspx) (CPCA), the statewide membership body for federally qualified health centers (FQHCs). Its EIN is 81-1326638 and its tax-exempt status dates to July 2016. The organization formally rebranded to **CPCA Advocates** effective **January 23, 2024** ([CPCA Advocates newsroom](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/ABOUT/MEDIA/CPCAAdvocates/MEDIA/News_Articles.aspx)), with the organization's Facebook page noting the prior name explicitly. The website is [cpcaadvocates.org](https://cpcaadvocates.org).

A separate political action committee — the **CaliforniaHealth+ Advocates Political Action Committee** (later CA Primary Care Association Advocates PAC) — was registered with the California Secretary of State on **March 23, 2021**, assigned committee ID **1437185** ([CPCA Advocates PAC page](https://cpcaadvocates.org/CPCAAdvocates/ADVOCATES_PAC/CPCAAdvocates/PAC/Advocates_PAC.aspx)). No PAC existed in the 2018 or 2020 cycles, limiting the organization to nonpartisan civic engagement and issue advocacy in those years.

## 7. Major Political Spending Campaigns

### 2024 Prop 35 (Largest Single Spending Event in Org History)

Proposition 35 asked California voters to make permanent the managed care organization (MCO) tax that funds Medi-Cal. Community health centers rely heavily on Medi-Cal reimbursement, making this existentially important for FQHCs. The CPCA Advocates committee (1465399) raised **$1,729,053** for Yes on Prop 35 per the [California Secretary of State](https://www.sos.ca.gov/campaign-lobbying/helpful-resources/measure-contributions/2024-ballot-measure-contribution-totals/proposition-35-provides-permanent-funding-medi-cal-health-care-services-initiative-statu). The parent CPCA separately contributed $2 million to the main Yes on Prop 35 committee. Prop 35 passed in November 2024 with approximately 68% of the vote ([Ballotpedia: Proposition 35 (2024)](https://ballotpedia.org/California_Proposition_35,_Managed_Care_Organization_Tax_Authorization_Initiative_(2024))).

The $1.729 million in Prop 35 spending substantially exceeds total direct candidate PAC contributions across all prior cycles combined ($18,700 + $57,400 + $50,000 = ~$126,100 through 2024). However, ballot measure spending is structurally different from candidate support: it is one-time, issue-specific, and directly tied to FQHC financial survival rather than partisan candidate promotion.

### 2026 Becerra Support: Scale in Context

The 2026 Becerra support involves multiple financial tracks, only some of which are quantified as of May 2026:
- **Direct PAC contribution**: $7,500 (confirmed, Cal-Access)
- **IE committee seed money**: Amount not yet publicly disclosed (committee 1490885, formed April 30, 2026)
- **Commissioned poll**: Cost not disclosed; EMC Research statewide gubernatorial polls typically cost $25,000–$75,000
- **Staff and organizational time**: Unquantified

Even if total Becerra-related spending matches the Prop 35 committee's $1.729 million, it would represent the first time the organization directed comparable resources toward a candidate rather than a ballot measure.

## 9. 2026 Becerra Support: Context and Political Alignment

### Why Becerra?

Xavier Becerra served as California Attorney General from 2017 to 2021, then as U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services from 2021 to January 2025 — overseeing federal Medicaid policy, the ACA, and community health center funding during the COVID-19 pandemic. His track record aligns directly with FQHC interests. The [EMC Research poll commissioned by CPCA Advocates](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-playbook/2026/04/29/the-pile-on-that-wasnt-00897956) showed Becerra tied with Steve Hilton at 19% among likely voters, rising to 23% when pushed — framing the polling effort as reinforcing a narrative of viability rather than gauging undecided opinion.

### Bearstar Strategies and the Newsom Connection

Bearstar Strategies, which leads media strategy for IE committee 1490885, is associated with Newsom's political network. Newsom has not formally endorsed in the governor's race but his allies' involvement in the Becerra IE committee signals tacit alignment. The co-seed funder — California State Council of Laborers — is a major labor organization with longstanding Democratic Party ties ([Politico California Playbook](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-playbook/2026/04/29/the-pile-on-that-wasnt-00897956)).

### Revenue Trajectory as Enabling Condition

The organization's revenue nearly tripled from FY2022 ($788,381) to FY2025 ($4,268,892). This expansion — coinciding with the Prop 35 victory that secured permanent Medi-Cal funding — created both the financial capacity and the political capital to escalate electoral ambition. The 2026 four-channel Becerra campaign is, in part, a consequence of the organization having built a much larger resource base than existed in any prior cycle.

## 10. Conclusion: Normal or Unprecedented?

**The 2026 four-channel support for Xavier Becerra is unprecedented at every dimension of comparison with prior cycles.**

| Dimension | Prior Cycles (2018–2024) | 2026 Becerra |
|-----------|--------------------------|--------------|
| Highest office endorsed | State Assembly (confirmed 2024); no statewide candidate | **Governor** (first ever) |
| Commissioned poll | None documented in any prior cycle | **EMC Research poll** (April 2026) — first ever |
| IE committee for candidate | None (Prop 35 ballot measure only in 2024) | **Working Families for Healthy Communities** (1490885) — first ever candidate IE |
| Direct PAC contribution, statewide | None | **$7,500 to Becerra for Governor** (first to any statewide office) |
| Channels simultaneously deployed | Max 2 (endorsement + direct contribution) | **All 4** simultaneously |
| Org revenue underpinning the cycle | $788K–$1.49M (FY2022–FY2023) | **$4.27M** (FY2025) — 5× prior baseline |

In 2018 and 2020, no PAC existed and the organization functioned as a civic engagement vehicle. In 2022, the new PAC made modest Assembly-level contributions. In 2024, the organization endorsed Assembly candidates, made direct contributions, and spent $1.73 million on Prop 35 — but all candidate support remained at the legislative level. In 2026, the organization crossed into a qualitatively different arena: gubernatorial politics, public polling as narrative tool, and candidate IE committee infrastructure previously reserved for major labor unions and party-aligned super PACs.

The structural shift in revenues (FY2024–FY2025 doubling) and the political success of Prop 35 (2024) appear to have accelerated the organization's electoral ambitions well beyond historical norms. Whether this represents a durable elevation of the organization's political profile or a one-cycle intensification tied to Becerra's specific policy alignment with FQHC interests cannot be determined from available evidence. What is clear is that **nothing in the 2018, 2020, 2022, or 2024 record establishes a precedent for the scale, scope, or office level of the 2026 Becerra campaign engagement**.
