## Bottom line

The current primary-source record supports a narrow but important exposure finding: Wonderful Nut Orchards is directly named in a 2025 State Water Board SWP/CVP transfer order as part of Westside Mutual Water Company, farming in both Westlands and Kern County Water Agency areas, and having previously banked CVP water in Kern Water Bank ([State Water Board 2025 temporary-change order](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/waterrights/water_issues/programs/petitions/docs/2025/14443tt250527-order.pdf)). The broader SGMA exposure is real at the basin level, especially Kern, Tulare Lake, Tule, Kaweah, and Westside, but no permitted source located in this pass ties a specific 2025-2026 enforcement action directly to a named Wonderful parcel or Resnick landholding beyond the State Water Board's Wonderful/Westside/Kern Water Bank transfer order ([State Water Board Kern County Subbasin page](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/sgma/groundwater_basins/kern-county-subbasin.html), [State Water Board Tulare Lake Subbasin page](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/sgma/groundwater_basins/tulare_lake_subbasin.html), [State Water Board Tule Subbasin page](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/sgma/groundwater_basins/tule-subbasin.html), [State Water Board Kaweah Subbasin page](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/sgma/groundwater_basins/kaweah-subbasin.html)). Politically, Cal-Access verifies Resnick/Wonderful money across Villaraigosa, Mahan, Swalwell, Becerra, Newsom's ballot committee, and multiple agricultural/business PACs, while Villaraigosa's located 2026 water position is a general "all of the above" statement rather than a documented SGMA, Westlands, or Resnick-specific pledge ([Cal-Access database export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip), [Los Angeles Times](https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2026-04-01/anti-regulation-democrats-top-takeaways-from-governors-race-forum-in-fresno)).
