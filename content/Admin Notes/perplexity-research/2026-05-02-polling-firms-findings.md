# Polling-industry findings summary: what to publish

The full Perplexity report is at `2026-05-02-polling-firms-funders-results.md` (669 lines, 47-poll inventory plus 11+ firm profiles). This file surfaces the four findings that are beat-page-publishable, in order of editorial strength.

## Finding 1: David Binder Research is paid by both sides of the Steyer race

**The receipts:**
- **$30,000** from anti-Steyer industry IE committee 1489677 (filing 3138008, EXPN_DSCR: "Survey/Oppose/Tom Steyer/Governor/Statewide"). This is opposition message-testing, not horserace polling. Subvendors: KGS Research $9,875, Political Data Inc. $4,200.
- **$50,000** from Eric Swalwell for Governor committee 1488732 (filing 3139007 Schedule G). Pro-Swalwell polling.
- **$undisclosed** for the California Democratic Party VOTER Index Tracker I (April 7, 2026, n=800 LV).
- **Public horserace polls** released February through April 2026 covering the same race they are being paid to oppose one candidate in and support another in.

**The funders behind 1489677** (the anti-Steyer pool): PG&E ($8M via passthrough committee 1490270), California Realtors ($5M), JOBSPAC / Cal Chamber ($5M), California Building Industry Association ($1M), IBEW Local 1245 ($50K), CCPOA ($25K). Total visible pool: approximately $22M.

**DBR's prior relationship with that funder stack:** DBR's number-six lifetime California payer is **"Action for Safety and Justice"** at $1.38M, a CCPOA-allied independent expenditure vehicle. The CCPOA orbit is a documented prior client. PG&E, the Realtors, JOBSPAC, BIA, and IBEW Local 1245 are not in DBR's top-15 lifetime payers; the CCPOA-allied IE is.

**The frame that writes itself:** The same firm releasing public polls on the Steyer race is paid by an industry coalition to test attack messages against Steyer, paid by Swalwell's campaign to support Swalwell, and paid by the California Democratic Party to track the whole field. Three paying clients, three different angles on the same race, public horserace polls on top. No FPPC complaint located.

**Beat-page working title:** *"The pollster who works for everybody."*

## Finding 2: Gudelunas Strategies polled the race for the same coalition that paid DBR

**The receipts:** Two polls, April 14–18 and April 23–27, 2026. The April 23–27 wave showed:

| Poll (same field dates) | Becerra | Steyer | Hilton |
|------------------------|--------:|-------:|-------:|
| **Gudelunas (anti-Steyer IE sponsor)** | **24%** | 15% | 23% |
| CBS / YouGov | 13% | 15% | 16% |
| Emerson / ICP (Apr 14–15) | 10% | 14% | 17% |
| EMC | 21% | 17% | 20% |
| Independent Voter Project | 23% | 14% | 20% |
| Evitarus / CA Dem | 13% | 13% | 16% |
| Kreate | 10% | 16% | 18% |

Gudelunas had Becerra **11 points above CBS/YouGov on identical field dates**, and **14 points above Emerson** from days earlier. The polling consensus put Becerra in the 13 to 23 percent range; Gudelunas was at the very top.

**Sponsor identification:** Sacramento Bee (April 21, 2026) identifies the Gudelunas sponsor as "an anti-Steyer independent expenditure that is backing Becerra." The funders match the Hook A coalition: PG&E, Realtors, BIA, JOBSPAC / Cal Chamber, IBEW Local 1245, CCPOA. The cmte-ID-to-Gudelunas-payment trace is not yet confirmed in Cal-Access EXPN_CD; that is an open primary-source verification (could be 1489677, could be a sister vehicle).

**The pattern:** Polls timed to release **immediately after Swalwell's exit (April 12) and Yee's withdrawal-and-Steyer-endorsement (April 20)** show Becerra surging. The narrative effect is to consolidate non-Steyer Democratic voters behind Becerra, who is the donor coalition's preferred alternative because his policy platform is less aggressive toward PG&E and corporate property taxes than Steyer's. This is what donor-coalition-favorable polling divergence looks like operationally.

**Beat-page working title:** *"The poll that justifies the donation that pays for the poll."* This is the same daisy-chain frame the class-traitor beat already proved, applied to a different layer of the same machine.

## Finding 3: The polling field has a structural opacity problem

Of 47 cataloged 2026 CA Gov polls released through May 1, 2026:

- **Approximately 27 percent (about 12 polls)** come from Democratic-aligned firms with **no disclosed sponsor**: Tulchin, FM3, Lake Research, Tavern Research (×2), Global Strategy Group (×2), RBI Strategies, PPP, Impact Research, EMC (2 of 3), DBR standalone.
- **About 22 percent are media-funded**: Emerson / Inside California Politics-Nexstar (8), Politico/UC Berkeley TrueDot (2), CBS/YouGov, Capitol Weekly.
- **About 20 percent are public-interest or academic**: PPIC (3), Berkeley IGS (3), USC/CSU/Cal Poly, Independent Voter Project (2).
- **About 9 percent are CA Democratic Party VOTER Index** (Evitarus + DBR).
- The rest are firm-self-funded (Kreate, CivicLens, Bold Decision) or single industry / candidate / Republican-internal commissions.

**The aggregator hygiene problem:** Poll aggregators that average undisclosed-sponsor polls into the consensus are weighting opaque advocacy work as if it were neutral. When a Democratic-aligned firm releases a "public" poll without naming who paid for it, the number flows into 538-successor / Silver Bulletin / Decision Desk HQ averages with the same weight as a PPIC or Berkeley IGS public poll.

**Beat-page working title:** *"One in four California polls won't say who paid for them."*

## Finding 4: PG&E pays both major Democratic California pollsters

DBR is on the anti-Steyer IE that PG&E funded with $8M.

Tulchin Research's own bio page lists corporate clients including **"Abbott Laboratories, AT&T, General Electric, Google, National Semiconductor, Pacific Gas and Electric, Tesla and Toyota."** PG&E is a direct Tulchin corporate client per the firm's own bio. Tulchin is not a vendor on filing 3138008; the relationship is through Tulchin's commercial/issue-research practice, not the political committee work.

This is a less explosive finding than Findings 1 and 2 (no specific 2026 race expenditure documented), but it is structurally important: the two largest Democratic California polling firms both have paid relationships with the largest funder of the anti-Steyer coalition. **Sidebar fact for the beat, not the headline.**

## Finding 5 (caveat): What the report could not verify

The report flags primary-source gaps that need to close before some claims publish:
1. The cmte-ID-to-Gudelunas link (Section 3.5). Sac Bee names the sponsor coalition; Cal-Access EXPN_CD has not yet been traced to confirm whether the polls were paid by 1489677 specifically or by a sister IE. If 1489677, it strengthens Finding 2 to a documented Cal-Access edge. If not, the claim becomes "an anti-Steyer IE [sister to the one that paid DBR]" which is weaker.
2. AAPOR Transparency Initiative member list could not be retrieved for most firms. PPIC and Emerson are confirmed Charter Members; DBR, FM3, EMC are confirmed non-members; the rest are unknown.
3. CA SOS BizFile entity numbers for all 11 firms were not Tier-1 confirmed (robots-disallow blocked direct query).
4. FEC committee-name resolution for the federal-side payer profiles is incomplete due to API rate-limiting.

None of these gaps blocks Findings 1, 2, or 3 from publishing. They block claim-precision in the prose ("an anti-Steyer IE" vs "anti-Steyer IE 1489677") on Finding 2.
