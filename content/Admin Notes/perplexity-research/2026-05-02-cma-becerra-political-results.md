# CMA / CALPAC Political Activity Supporting Xavier Becerra: 2026 California Gubernatorial Race

**Report Date:** May 2, 2026  
**Scope:** California Medical Association (CMA) and its affiliated political action committee (CALPAC) activity supporting Xavier Becerra in the 2026 California gubernatorial race.  
**Sources:** Cal-Access / FPPC records (via Transparency USA and FPPC public filings), CMA official newsroom (cmadocs.org), Politico California Playbook, KQED / LAist reporting, social media official accounts.

---

## Executive Summary

As of May 2, 2026, the California Medical Association has issued a **formal endorsement** of Xavier Becerra (April 29, 2026), published an **official press release** with a leadership quote from CMA President Dr. René Bravo, and amplified the endorsement across social media channels. The endorsement followed a **private meeting** between Becerra and CMA physicians at which Becerra explicitly distanced himself from single-payer healthcare — CMA's longstanding opposition position.

On **financial activity**, no direct monetary contributions from CALPAC (FPPC ID 742617) to Becerra committee 1480025 appear in publicly accessible Transparency USA records as of the report date. CALPAC's 2026 election cycle expenditure disclosures do not list Becerra's committee or the Working Families for Healthy Communities IE committee among payees. No CMA/CALPAC-commissioned polls on the 2026 governor race have been identified; the one publicly disclosed internal poll supporting Becerra's candidacy was commissioned by CPCA Advocates (a community health center advocacy group unaffiliated with CMA), not by CMA or CALPAC.

No coalition letters, formal position papers, or legislative testimony referencing Becerra's candidacy have been identified.

---

## 1. Formal Endorsement

**Finding: CONFIRMED**

The California Medical Association formally endorsed Xavier Becerra for Governor of California on **April 29, 2026**, per an official press release published on the CMA newsroom ([cmadocs.org](https://www.cmadocs.org/newsroom/news/view/ArticleId/51180/CMA-Endorses-Xavier-Becerra-for-Governor)) and amplified on [CMA's Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CMAdocs/posts/cma-representing-more-than-50000-physicians-and-medical-students-across-the-stat/1359569119551118/), [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/p/DXuLmWXlD_u/), and [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cmadocs_icymi-yesterday-the-california-medical-activity-7455685159376531456-xhJt) accounts.

The endorsement was voted on by CMA's board on the evening of Tuesday, April 29, per [Politico California Playbook](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-playbook/2026/04/29/the-pile-on-that-wasnt-00897956). The official CMA press release was dated **May 1, 2026** on the cmadocs.org newsroom page (consistent with the Politico April 29 board vote timeline and a May 1 public release date per [KQED](https://www.kqed.org/news/12082059/xavier-becerra-backpedals-on-single-payer-as-he-woos-powerful-doctors-lobby)).

No coalition letters, formal policy position papers, or legislative testimony referencing Becerra's candidacy or explicitly endorsing his single-payer (or modified) healthcare position have been identified in available public records, CMA's newsroom, or media reporting.

CMA's organizational activity related to single-payer and federal healthcare cuts in the 2025–2026 period is documented separately from the gubernatorial endorsement:

- CMA has advocated for reversal of federal Medicaid/Medi-Cal funding cuts — a position shared by Becerra and aligned with his stated campaign priority of combating H.R.1's projected $9.5 billion annual impact on California safety-net programs ([KQED](https://www.kqed.org/news/12082059/xavier-becerra-backpedals-on-single-payer-as-he-woos-powerful-doctors-lobby)).

No joint letter, testimony, or formal policy document co-signed by CMA/CALPAC and Becerra's campaign or specifically referencing his candidacy has been located.

## 7. Context: CMA/Becerra Relationship and Single-Payer Dynamics

The endorsement occurred against a specific healthcare policy backdrop documented by [KQED (April Dembosky, May 1, 2026)](https://www.kqed.org/news/12082059/xavier-becerra-backpedals-on-single-payer-as-he-woos-powerful-doctors-lobby) and [LAist](https://laist.com/news/politics/xavier-becerra-backpedals-on-single-payer-as-he-woos-powerful-doctors-lobby):

- Becerra had **campaigned on single-payer** through at least late March 2026; a March 23, 2026 social media post stated: "I proudly helped write it, pass it, and defend it in court. Now I'm ready to go further as Governor and deliver single-payer health care for our state."
- Prior to the CMA endorsement, Becerra held a **private meeting** with CMA physicians. In that meeting, per Dr. Bravo's disclosure to KQED, Becerra stated that at this point he was not supportive of single-payer and framed the priority as protecting Medi-Cal from H.R.1 cuts.
- Following the endorsement, Becerra's campaign spokesperson Jonathan Underland stated to KQED: "A single payer system is the right goal — but it's not possible under this administration, and right now we have a crisis on our hands."
- CMA "has long opposed the single-payer system, arguing it is not economically feasible and would erode physician autonomy" ([KQED](https://www.kqed.org/news/12082059/xavier-becerra-backpedals-on-single-payer-as-he-woos-powerful-doctors-lobby)).
- State Assemblymember Ash Kalra (D-San José), a backer of recent single-payer bills, endorsed Tom Steyer "because of his clear commitment" to single-payer, not Becerra ([KQED](https://www.kqed.org/news/12082059/xavier-becerra-backpedals-on-single-payer-as-he-woos-powerful-doctors-lobby)).
