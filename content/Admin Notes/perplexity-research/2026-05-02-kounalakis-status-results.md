# Eleni Kounalakis — 2026 California Gubernatorial Race Status
**As of May 2, 2026**

---

## Summary

Eleni Kounalakis entered the 2026 California gubernatorial race on **April 24, 2023**, becoming the first prominent Democrat to formally launch a campaign to succeed Gov. Gavin Newsom, who is term-limited ([LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2023-04-24/lt-gov-eleni-kounalakis-2026-governors-race), [Politico California](https://www.politico.com/news/2023/04/24/eleni-kounalakis-campaign-california-governor-2026-00093381)). The Secretary of State's official CAL-ACCESS bulk export records a 2026 Governor Form 501 for Kounalakis with committee ID **1460033**, report date **April 24, 2023**, and execution date **April 24, 2023** ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)). She **withdrew on August 8, 2025**, citing the opportunity to make a greater impact as State Treasurer and acknowledging weak mid-2025 fundraising momentum ([CalMatters](https://calmatters.org/politics/2025/08/california-governors-race-kounalakis/), [Sacramento Bee](https://www.sacbee.com/news/politics-government/capitol-alert/article311633547.html)). As of the date of this note, Kounalakis is no longer a gubernatorial candidate; she is instead an active, certified candidate for **California State Treasurer** in the June 2, 2026 primary, where the SOS certified candidate list identifies her as "Lieutenant Governor of California" and a Democratic candidate for Treasurer ([California Secretary of State certified candidate list](https://elections.cdn.sos.ca.gov/statewide-elections/2026-primary/cert-list-candidates.pdf)).

In the treasurer race Kounalakis holds a commanding fundraising advantage, with approximately **$8.4 million cash on hand** as of April 18, 2026 — the highest in the field by a wide margin — and roughly **$4.4 million in total contributions** logged under her treasurer committee, "Eleni Kounalakis for Treasurer 2026" ([Sacramento Bee voter guide](https://www.sacbee.com/news/politics-government/election/voter-guide/article315278245.html), [Transparency USA treasurer race](https://www.transparencyusa.org/ca/race/california-treasurer)). Her campaign has secured high-profile endorsements from Gov. Newsom, Speaker Emerita Nancy Pelosi, incumbent Treasurer Fiona Ma, State Controller Malia Cohen, Hillary Clinton, and Sen. Barbara Boxer. The McClatchy editorial board (Sacramento Bee) issued a formal endorsement in her favor, calling her "especially well-suited to manage the portfolio of a state treasurer" ([Sacramento Bee endorsement](https://www.sacbee.com/opinion/article315562889.html)). The field also includes Democratic state Sen. Anna Caballero and Board of Equalization member Tony Vazquez, plus three candidates from smaller parties; the Sacramento Bee and LA Times both frame the race as effectively a two-way contest between Kounalakis and Caballero ([Sacramento Bee endorsement](https://www.sacbee.com/opinion/article315562889.html), [LA Times voter guide](https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2026-05-01/2026-california-election-state-treasurer-kounalakis-caballero-vazquez-hawks-serpa-voter-guide)).

## Bulleted Timeline

- **April 24, 2023** — Kounalakis formally opens gubernatorial campaign committee ("Eleni Kounalakis for Governor 2026," FPPC ID 1460033), becoming the first major Democrat to enter the 2026 governor's race ([LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2023-04-24/lt-gov-eleni-kounalakis-2026-governors-race), [Politico California](https://www.politico.com/news/2023/04/24/eleni-kounalakis-campaign-california-governor-2026-00093381); [California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).

- **March 13, 2025** — Politico California first reports that Kounalakis is quietly exploring a run for state treasurer as a fallback, while publicly remaining in the governor's race ([Politico California](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-playbook/2025/03/13/scoop-elenis-backup-plan-00227932)).

- **July 30, 2025** — Former Vice President Kamala Harris announces she will not run for governor, further disrupting the field and accelerating Kounalakis's reassessment ([Politico California](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-playbook/2025/07/31/the-governors-race-truly-begins-00486381)).

- **August 1, 2025** — Reporting shows Kounalakis raised only ~$107,000 in the first six months of 2025, leaving $4.6 million cash on hand, while other candidates pulled well ahead in fundraising ([Politico California](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/california-playbook/2025/08/01/cash-flows-to-porter-and-dries-up-for-kounalakis-00488166)).

- **August 8, 2025** — Kounalakis posts a public statement announcing she is **withdrawing from the 2026 governor's race** and will instead seek the California State Treasurer seat. An Emerson College poll released the same day showed her at 3% in the gubernatorial field ([CalMatters](https://calmatters.org/politics/2025/08/california-governors-race-kounalakis/), [Sacramento Bee](https://www.sacbee.com/news/politics-government/capitol-alert/article311633547.html), [LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2025-08-08/lt-gov-eleni-kounalakis-drops-out-of-governors-race-pivots-to-run-for-treasurer), [Politico California](https://www.politico.com/news/2025/08/08/eleni-kounalakis-drops-out-of-california-governors-race-00499422), [KQED](https://www.kqed.org/forum/2010101910873/california-governors-race-shifts-as-harris-kounalakis-say-theyre-not-running)). Quote: *"At this moment, I believe I can make the greatest impact by focusing on California's financial future."*

- **August–December 2025** — Transfers the bulk of her gubernatorial campaign funds to her treasurer committee, "Eleni Kounalakis for Treasurer 2026" ([Politico California](https://www.politico.com/news/2025/08/08/eleni-kounalakis-drops-out-of-california-governors-race-00499422)).

- **March 26, 2026** — California Secretary of State Shirley N. Weber certifies the official candidate list for the June 2, 2026 primary. Kounalakis is listed as "ELENI KOUNALAKIS / Democratic / Lieutenant Governor of California" for the Treasurer office ([California Secretary of State certified candidate list](https://elections.cdn.sos.ca.gov/statewide-elections/2026-primary/cert-list-candidates.pdf)).

- **April 19, 2026** — Sacramento Bee voter guide reports Kounalakis has $8.4 million cash on hand as of April 18, leading the treasurer field; notes major donors include developers, organized labor, David Dolby, the CA YIMBY Victory Fund, Anheuser-Busch, and the California Teachers Association ([Sacramento Bee voter guide](https://www.sacbee.com/news/politics-government/election/voter-guide/article315278245.html)).

- **April 28–30, 2026** — McClatchy editorial board (Sacramento Bee, Fresno Bee) endorses Kounalakis for treasurer ([Sacramento Bee endorsement](https://www.sacbee.com/opinion/article315562889.html)).

- **May 1, 2026** — LA Times publishes comprehensive treasurer voter guide with Kounalakis as a leading candidate; primary is June 2, 2026 ([LA Times voter guide](https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2026-05-01/2026-california-election-state-treasurer-kounalakis-caballero-vazquez-hawks-serpa-voter-guide)).

## Notes on Source Gaps

- **ltg.ca.gov:** The official Lieutenant Governor newsroom ([ltg.ca.gov/press-releases/](https://ltg.ca.gov/press-releases/)) does not appear to contain a press release specifically announcing Kounalakis's gubernatorial withdrawal or treasurer entry as of May 2, 2026. The announcement was made via social media (referenced by multiple outlets) rather than an official office press release.
- **CAL-ACCESS / FPPC treasurer committee ID:** The live cal-access.sos.ca.gov domain blocks automated retrieval, but the Secretary of State's official bulk CAL-ACCESS export identifies "KOUNALAKIS FOR TREASURER 2026; ELENI" as active recipient committee **1483008** and separately records Form 501 committee ID **1460720** for Kounalakis's 2026 Treasurer candidacy ([California Secretary of State raw CAL-ACCESS export](https://campaignfinance.cdn.sos.ca.gov/dbwebexport.zip)).
- **Treasurer race polling:** No named-publication-sourced poll with specific percentage figures for the treasurer race was located. The Berkeley IGS March 2026 poll covered ballot measures, not the treasurer race. The April 2026 CADEM tracking survey covered the governor's race only. Kounalakis's competitive position is characterized by editorial and news sources but without a published poll number.
