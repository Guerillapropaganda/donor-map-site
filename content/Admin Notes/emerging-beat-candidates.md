## C-001 — The Farallon apostate beat (Steyer pre-2012 record as standalone)
**Surfaced:** 2026-05-02 from `Downloads/2026-05-02-steyer-financial-history-stress-test.pplx.md`

**Thesis:** Tom Steyer made his fortune financing exactly the assets he now campaigns against. Farallon under his name financed Indonesian coal (Bumi/Berau/Adaro), Whitehaven Coal in Australia, Indiabulls coal-fired power, Meiya Power, plus held Peabody/Arch/Massey on the public side. 5.5% of Corrections Corporation of America ($89M, the largest single private-prison-operator stake of the era). UST tobacco. $234M Caesars + $399M Harrah's. Trump Entertainment Resorts. Bloomberg confirmed he remained the second-largest investor in Farallon after his 2012 management exit. NYT rated his 2020 debate claim "I withdrew my investments from fossil fuels" as "somewhat overstated."

**Receipts:** Reuters May 13 2014 ("From black to green"), NYT July 4 2014 ("Prominent environmentalist helped fund coal projects"), Politico Oct 24 2016 (CCA $89M stake), Sacramento Bee Oct 12 2016 (UST tobacco), Sacramento Bee Nov 3 2016 (Trump Entertainment Resorts $558K), Forbes Oct 23 2012 + Bloomberg Oct 22 2012 (exit terms, second-largest investor), NYT Jan 23 2020 fact-check, LA Times April 6 2026 (CCA→CoreCivic→ICE Otay Mesa connection now operative). SEC 13F filings cited inline in the Perplexity report.

**Why interesting:** Right now this material is folded INTO the Class Traitor beat as the "his own record" subsection. That gives readers the metabolized version. But the standalone version — "the billionaire who built his fortune funding the worlds the impeachment campaign was supposed to tear down" — is its own punchier beat. The class-traitor frame applied through time, not through politics. Could pair as a follow-up after Class Traitor lands.

**Earliest plausible slot:** Post-launch, after the May 6 → May 27 anchor sequence. June or later.

## C-002 — CAR's 2026 split-roll defense across all CA gov candidates
**Surfaced:** 2026-05-02 from `Downloads/2026-05-02-car-anti-steyer-tax-mechanism.pplx.md`

**Thesis:** California Association of Realtors' $5M anti-Steyer is not an anti-housing position — CAR overlaps with Steyer on supply-side housing (CAR supported AB 610 / AB 1007 / AB 1276 streamlining; Steyer wants to build 1M homes). The CAR money is a defense of commercial Prop 13. CAR's RAA page explicitly lists "Split Roll" as a 2026 threat. The right beat is the cross-candidate version: who in the CA gov race is CAR with, who are they against, and on which tax mechanism specifically.

**Receipts:** CAR RAA page (https://www.car.org/advocacy/PACSnRAF/RAA), CAR's October 2025 legislative program (supports/opposes table), CAR's March 6 2026 gubernatorial forum press release (named participants: Becerra, Hilton, Mahan, Porter, Swalwell, Villaraigosa), Steyer's tax-loopholes page (April 11 2026), KQED + LAist April 14 2026 (Steyer's 2027 special election proposal).

**Why interesting:** Process journalism on industry money mechanism, not candidate framing. Pair material with Bearstar Octopus (May 27) — both are about the structural side of the race rather than the candidate side. Or pair with Race Map (June 1) as a tax-mechanism overlay. Has the additional virtue that it explains things accurately: the housing-vs-CAR framing is wrong; the commercial-Prop-13 framing is right.

**Earliest plausible slot:** Pair with Bearstar Octopus May 27, OR feed into the June 1 Race Map as a layer.

## C-003 — The Becerra "Voters Need to Know" red-box page (process journalism)
**Surfaced:** 2026-05-02 from `Downloads/2026-05-02-gudelunas-1489677-spending-update.pplx.md` referencing LA Times May 1 2026

**Thesis:** Becerra's campaign posted a "Voters Need to Know" page literally lined by a red box — a textbook example of red-boxing (the practice of campaigns publishing their preferred attack themes in conspicuous formats so allied IEs can run them without crossing into legal coordination). The LA Times article framed it as a coordination-adjacent tactic. Steyer spokesperson Anthony York's quote: "Xavier Becerra is signaling to his allies with Big Oil and the utility monopolies how they should spend their millions distorting Tom Steyer's record."

**Receipts:** LA Times May 1 2026 ("Coded messages, red boxing, other tactics in California race for governor"). The actual Becerra page itself (URL not yet captured — David's URL pass for Chevron beat already touches the page; can pull for this beat).

**Why interesting:** Process journalism explains *how* the $20M anti-Steyer operation is being directed without obvious coordination. The mechanic itself — red-boxing as a legal-but-coordination-adjacent tactic — is a story most California voters have never heard. Pair material with Bearstar Octopus (May 27) since both are about the operational side. Could also feed the Cop-Coddler beat (May 20) if Becerra's red-box page is invoked there as a related signaling pattern.

**Earliest plausible slot:** Bearstar Octopus May 27 (natural fit) or as material woven into Cop-Coddler May 20 / Race Map June 1.

## C-004 — Williamson / The Collaborative federal indictment
**Surfaced:** 2026-05-02 from `Downloads/2026-05-02-bearstar-polaris-client-map.pplx.md`

**Thesis:** Dana Williamson, former Newsom chief of staff and architect of "The Collaborative" (the Newsom-orbit consulting hub that includes Bearstar Strategies' Sean Clegg, Jim DeBoo, and lobbyist Greg Campbell), was indicted November 2025 on 23 federal counts (bank and tax fraud, conspiracy) for billing Becerra's dormant state campaign account for bogus consulting services and claiming luxury goods as business expenses. DeBoo, Clegg, and other Bearstar figures have NOT been charged. But KCRA-TV reported May 1 that Bearstar's pro-Becerra IE launch was notable given "some of the same consultants now connected with Bearstar were previously linked with a group tied with that case." Consumer Watchdog separately reported FBI surveillance notices going to "many associates and clients" of The Collaborative.

**Receipts:** [LA Times Nov 13, 2025](https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2025-11-13/federal-fraud-case-newsom-political-orbit) (Williamson indictment), [KSFO/KCRA May 1, 2026](https://www.ksfo.com/2026/05/01/controversial-political-consulting-firm-backing-xavier-becerra/) (Bearstar pro-Becerra IE), [Consumer Watchdog Jan 13, 2026](https://consumerwatchdog.org/accountability/uber-needs-to-disclose-to-shareholders-its-executives-conflict-of-interest-on-contingency-fee-ballot-measure-ties-to-dana-williamson-scandal/) (Collaborative FBI surveillance context).

**Why interesting:** This is the structural-corruption story under the IE-money story. The Class Traitor beat surfaces the $20M anti-Steyer machine. The Bearstar Octopus beat surfaces the consulting infrastructure. THIS beat surfaces what happens when that infrastructure intersects federal criminal investigations. Three layers of the same political-economy story. The "The Collaborative" name itself is editorially powerful.

**Earliest plausible slot:** Could fold into Bearstar Octopus (May 27) as a third act, or stand alone as a follow-up beat in early June post-primary. Standalone version requires the Williamson indictment outcome to settle (trial calendar TBD).

## C-005 — Bianco: The Sheriff Who Seized the Ballots
**Surfaced:** 2026-05-02 from `Downloads/2026-05-02-bianco-donor-law-enforcement.pplx.md`

**Thesis:** Chad Bianco — Riverside County Sheriff, Republican gubernatorial candidate polling 12-14%, qualified for the May 5 CNN debate — sits at the intersection of three structural stories: (1) his department seized 650,000 ballots from the 2025 Prop 50 redistricting referendum under a warrant obtained from a judge Bianco had endorsed in 2022 and who had publicly praised him; (2) his confirmed 2014 Oath Keepers membership (LAist + NPR primary-source reporting via Anonymous's 2021 Epik leak and DDoSecrets); (3) his Constitutional Sheriffs and Peace Officers Association affiliation. Plus a clean FPPC-aggregation question on Downs Energy + M&D Development's combined $156,800 from sibling-managed entities. The donor money tracks: Riverside County developer (Highland Fairview, $39,200), county road contractor (RJ Noble, $35,000), medical waste contractor (Mediwaste, $20,000) — all entities operating under his sheriff's jurisdiction or county contracts.

**Receipts:** [SF Chronicle Mar 31, 2026](https://www.sfchronicle.com/politics/article/contributions-bianco-violate-state-campaign-rules-22160676.php) (Downs/M&D aggregation), [LAist Oct 5, 2021](https://laist.com/news/criminal-justice/riverside-sheriff-chad-bianco-once-was-an-oath-keeper-defends-the-extremist-group) + [NPR Oct 6, 2021](https://www.npr.org/2021/10/06/1043651361/oath-keepers-california-sheriff-chad-bianco-january-6-us-capitol) (Oath Keepers), [CalMatters Mar 26, 2026](https://calmatters.org/politics/2026/03/chad-bianco-ballots-seized-riverside/) + [CalMatters Apr 21, 2026](https://calmatters.org/investigation/2026/04/chad-bianco-emails/) (ballot seizure + CSPOA emails). Cal-Access RCPT_CD primary extraction May 2 confirms all donor amounts.

**Why interesting:** Republican-side equivalent to the Class Traitor / Three Becerras pattern: candidate operating downstream of a documented donor-jurisdiction conflict, with a politically-loaded prior affiliation (Oath Keepers), running for higher office while still wielding the local enforcement authority. Constitutional Sheriffs / militia-adjacent material gives the beat a national-political-violence dimension that Steyer/Becerra material lacks. Pairs with Race Map (June 1) as the GOP-side story. Could stand alone at a later date or feed Race Map.

**Earliest plausible slot:** Race Map June 1 as the GOP exemplar, or standalone post-primary if Bianco does well in the June 2 vote and warrants deeper coverage.

## C-006 — Villaraigosa: The Pledge He Broke
**Surfaced:** 2026-05-02 from `Downloads/2026-05-02-villaraigosa-donor-income.pplx.md`

**Thesis:** In 2018, Antonio Villaraigosa signed a public pledge not to accept contributions from oil companies or named fossil-fuel executives. In 2026, he is the principal Democratic beneficiary of fossil-fuel money in the gubernatorial race: $72,800 from California Resources Corporation (the state's largest oil and gas producer), $78,400 from Chevron, $78,400 from Marathon Petroleum, $78,400 from Berry Corporation. He has not re-signed the pledge. He simultaneously opposes single-payer healthcare (the only major Democrat to do so) while taking $78,400 from AltaMed (a system that benefits from the existing multi-payer arrangement Villaraigosa defends), $78,400 from COPE Healthcare Consulting, $15,000 from Fresenius Medical Care (German-owned dialysis giant, world's largest, has actively litigated against California healthcare regulation). Plus the post-mayor income trail: Mercury Public Affairs (clients included Hungary, Qatar, Turkey), Actum LLC (Hungary's government), $381,820 paid to him as Newsom's "infrastructure czar" by a California Forward nonprofit funded by AT&T, SoCalGas, Disney, and Southern California Edison — entities with regulatory interests before the state. And Reed Hastings, who put $7M into the 2018 CCSA-IE supporting Villaraigosa, has dropped him for 2026 — Hastings's money went to Mahan instead.

**Receipts:** [LA Times May 26, 2025](https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2025-05-26/oil-villaraigosa-fossil-fuel-governors-race) (broken pledge), [LA Times Nov 24, 2017](https://www.latimes.com/politics/la-pol-ca-antonio-villaraigosa-tax-returns-20171124-htmlstory.html) (post-mayor income through 2016), [LA Times Oct 7, 2021](https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2021-10-07/former-elected-leaders-nunez-boxer-villaraigosa-mercury-public-affairs) (Mercury exit), [CalMatters Jun 17, 2025](https://calmatters.org/politics/2025/06/california-newsom-villaraigosa-infrastructure-project/) (infrastructure czar / California Forward funding), [LA Times Nov 8, 2025](https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2025-11-08/democrats-running-for-governor-talk-about-healthcare) (single-payer opposition). Cal-Access RCPT_CD primary extraction May 2 confirms all donor amounts.

**Why interesting:** Three editorial frames available: (1) "The Pledge He Broke" — pure receipts story, the 2018 promise vs. the 2026 ledger; (2) "Selling Influence to Hungary, Qatar, and Turkey" — the post-mayor income story; (3) "Even Reed Hastings Gave Up" — the structural rejection by his own historical donor base. Strongest version probably combines #1 and #3 into a single arc: the candidate who broke his promise, didn't pay a political cost from his historical funders because they had already left. Same tier of editorial sharpness as Class Traitor.

**Earliest plausible slot:** Race Map June 1 as one of the cross-candidate threads, OR a standalone in early June if the primary results suggest a runoff scenario where Villaraigosa is consequential.

## C-007 — Hilton's Sacramento Policy Nonprofit (Golden Together)
**Surfaced:** 2026-05-04 from Hilton's Form 700 Schedule C (filed 2026-03-06)

**Thesis:** Steve Hilton is running for governor while serving as **Board President / Chief Executive Officer of Golden Together**, a public-policy nonprofit at **455 Capitol Mall, Suite 600, Sacramento, CA 95814**. The address is in the heart of Sacramento's lobbying corridor, directly opposite the Capitol. The Schedule C entry reports no income from the role. The candidate's own Form 700 puts him in the position of running an issue-advocacy organization while running for the office that would act on those issues.

**Open questions for the editorial:** What does Golden Together advocate for? Who funds it (the IRS Form 990 disclosure)? Do its 2026 policy priorities track Hilton's campaign platform? Does it engage in any activity that could plausibly cross campaign-coordination lines? Is the registered address shared with any of his other LLCs (CR Productions, SHCR Productions, both at Atherton home address per Schedule A-2)?

**Receipts:** Hilton candidate Form 700 Schedule C (page 24 of the filing); the FPPC Form 700 search portal at form700search.fppc.ca.gov ("Steve Hilton" filer search). Form 990 retrieval via ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer or the IRS direct EIN lookup is the next step.

**Why interesting:** The structural fact that a candidate runs a Sacramento policy organization while running to govern Sacramento is editorially distinct from the Sierra/AI conflict. Different shape from the Hilton AI beat: the Sierra finding is "personal financial exposure," this one is "parallel policy infrastructure." A candidate-aligned 501(c) policy shop with a Capitol Mall address that the candidate himself runs is uncommon. Editorial story shape: "Two campaigns under one roof."

**Earliest plausible slot:** Standalone Hilton follow-up if Form 990 retrieval reveals donor disclosures worth editorializing. Or as a paragraph within a future Hilton-runoff piece if he advances past June 2.

**Status:** parked. Needs Form 990 retrieval before editorial work can start.

## C-008 — Hilton's Personal Fossil-Fuel and Pharma Holdings (climate / health-policy conflict)
**Surfaced:** 2026-05-04 from Hilton's Form 700 Schedule A-1 (filed 2026-03-06)

**Thesis:** Beyond the Sierra spine, Hilton's Schedule A-1 lists direct stock holdings in five of the six largest publicly-traded oil majors (Chevron, BP, Exxon Mobil, Shell, TotalEnergies) plus NextEra Energy. He also lists thirteen pharmaceutical and healthcare equity positions including AstraZeneca, Pfizer, Merck, Novartis, Sanofi, Roche, Novo Nordisk, Medtronic, Thermo Fisher. Each sector is regulated by California state government in distinct ways. Aggregate exposure ranges from low six figures to multiple millions per sector based on bracket midpoints.

**Receipts:** Hilton candidate Form 700 Schedule A-1 (pages 8-20 of the filing). All entries quoted directly on `/holdings-hilton-2026`.

**Why interesting:** Same shape as the Sierra/AI conflict, broadened: a California governor's signing pen and agency appointments directly affect Chevron (California-headquartered, regulated by ARB and State Water Board), the major pharmaceutical companies (drug-pricing legislation, Medi-Cal pharmacy reimbursement, Office of Health Care Affordability rate-setting), and the utility sector. This isn't disqualifying on its own — many candidates hold diversified portfolios — but the concentration in fossil fuels specifically (five oil majors, no Tesla, no major renewable other than NextEra) is editorially shapeable into a "this is how he'd govern" piece if a contradiction emerges between his campaign rhetoric on climate or healthcare and the holdings.

**Earliest plausible slot:** Standalone follow-up to /holdings-hilton-2026 if Hilton makes specific climate or healthcare commitments during the campaign that contradict the holdings. Or merged into a Race Map cross-cutting piece comparing every candidate's Form 700 disclosure portfolio.

**Status:** parked. The disclosure portrait is on `/holdings-hilton-2026`; the editorial frame waits for either a campaign contradiction event or a primary-eve cross-candidate comparison piece.
