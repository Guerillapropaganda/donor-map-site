## BEAT 1: COACHELLA DATA CENTER / STRONGHOLD POWER ⭐ STRONGEST FRESH BEAT

### Status: PUBLICATION-READY. Hard deadline May 11 (Monday town hall).

### The deal
- 240→450 acres agricultural land near Avenue 52 & Fillmore, Coachella
- Three data center buildings (~1M sq ft each), 270MW capacity (could expand to 600MW per DataCenterDynamics)
- Public-private partnership called "Coachella Municipal Utility"
- "In development since 2019" per Stronghold

### The vote
- **Feb 11, 2026 — Coachella City Council approved the Municipal Utility Development agreement** establishing public-private partnership with Stronghold Power Systems
- Both Stronghold and DMK Project Solutions Services partnering on the data center campus

### The donor → recipient chain
- **Stronghold Power Systems → former Mayor Steven Hernandez: $4,900**
- Hernandez presided over the Feb 11 vote AS MAYOR
- **Hernandez was indicted Oct 30, 2025 on 9 counts** by Riverside County criminal grand jury:
  - 1 felony, Government Code §1090 (conflict of interest in govt contracts)
  - 4 felony counts, Penal Code §118 (perjury — false Form 700 disclosures)
  - 4 misdemeanor counts, Government Code §87100 (conflict of interest in governmental decisions)
- **Hernandez PLEADED GUILTY March 2026** to one felony. DA dropped 8 charges in plea deal. Sentenced to 2 years probation + 200 hours community service. Resigned after 12 years as mayor.
- **Indictment scope explicitly covered "votes and advocacy related to downtown Coachella development projects"** — broader than just the CVAG Housing First contract that triggered the case

### The developer's federal record
**Stronghold Engineering, Inc. (Bailey-owned parent / sister of Stronghold Power Systems) paid $2.5 MILLION to settle a federal False Claims Act case in April 2021.** DOJ Central District of California alleged that from August 2007 to October 2013, the firm illegally obtained federal contracts by using a Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business as a false front (the SDVOSB program). Scott + Beverly Bailey personally signatories to the settlement.

### Bailey leadership
- **Scott Bailey** — Founder of Stronghold Power Systems, COO of Stronghold Engineering
- **Beverly Bailey** — President/CEO of Stronghold Engineering, $1M pledge to UCR School of Business; involved in 40+ Inland Empire / Coachella Valley nonprofits

### DMK Project Solutions Services
- HQ in Dulles, Virginia (out of state owner's-rep firm)
- Partners with Stronghold on the data center campus

### The opposition
- **April 22, 2026:** 100+ residents at Veterans Park demanding moratorium
- Across April: 250+ public commenters at Coachella Council
- **Frank Figueroa (new mayor, appointed April 22, 2026)** publicly called for moratorium: "If I'm appointed mayor, first thing I want to do is call for a moratorium." Said he has "his own reservations" about water/electricity costs.
- City Council current sitting members (post-Hernandez): Frank Figueroa (mayor), Stephanie Virgen, Denise Delgado, Yadira Perez. **Yadira Perez specifically asked about moratorium options.**
- Grand jury heard testimony from Mayor Pro Tem Denise Delgado, former councilmember Megan Beaman Jacinto, CVAG leadership, DA investigators
- **City Manager search ongoing in closed session as of May 5, 2026** — governance in flux

### The town hall
- **May 11, 2026 — community town hall** at Boys & Girls Club of Coachella Valley, 6-8pm
- City posted defensive disclaimer: "project not approved" + "city is not a co-applicant" — *itself* news (suggests pressure pushing them to back-pedal Feb 11)

### Cross-connection to Beat #3 (Calvert)
The Bailey family also donated to Ken Calvert federally and to Lincoln Club of Riverside County (see top-level cross-connection note).

## BEAT 2: INLAND EMPIRE HEALTH PLAN $320M MEDI-CAL FRAUD

### Status: STRUCTURAL DOCUMENTARY — beat-grade

### The fraud claim
- **DOJ filed September 2025** under False Claims Act
- **IEHP held back $320 MILLION** in federal Medi-Cal expansion money it should have returned
- IEHP serves 1.5-1.6M residents = 35% of combined Riverside + San Bernardino population
- Received nearly $3.5 BILLION under ACA Medi-Cal expansion
- Returned $30M; case alleges $320M short

### The smoking-gun email
**Bradley P. Gilbert** (IEHP CEO 2008-2019), per DOJ complaint, in an internal email:

> "The funds have to flow through the two county hospitals from IEHP so I can get credit for them as medical costs or the dollars go back to the state."

### Routing pattern
- IEHP → county hospitals → providers → **Huron Consulting Group** (instead of paying Huron directly with the federal funds)
- Used $$ as a "bargaining chip to negotiate better care rates" with providers
- Created bonus programs for regional health systems using the expansion money

### The revolving door
**Bradley Gilbert: IEHP CEO (2008-2019, 23 years at IEHP) → Newsom appointed him Director of California Department of Health Care Services** — the very state agency that oversees Medi-Cal. He oversaw the program he allegedly defrauded.

### IEHP Governing Board (4 supervisors + 3 public appointees, JPA structure)

| Member | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Curt Hagman** | Chair (current — Feb 2025+) | San Bernardino Co Supervisor (D4); also CUSM Board of Trustees; prior chair 2018-2020 |
| **Karen Spiegel** | Prior chair (2021–Feb 2025) | Riverside Co Supervisor (D2); ALSO sits on RUHS Medical Center Governing Board |
| **Yxstian Gutierrez** | Member (since Jan 2023) | Riverside Co Supervisor (D5); also WRCOG, RTA, RCTC, RUHS, Flood Control |
| **Jesse Armendarez** | Member (since Jan 2025) | San Bernardino Co Supervisor (D2) |
| **Dawn Rowe** | Member | San Bernardino Co Supervisor (D3) |
| **Andrew Williams** | Public appointee (since 1995) | 30 years on the board |
| **Dan Anderson** | Public appointee (since 2008) | Nonprofit sector 35+ years |

### Cross-board compounded structure
**Hagman governs both sides:** He chairs IEHP's board AND sits on CUSM (California University of Science and Medicine) Board of Trustees. **IEHP committed $1.2M annually to fund medical school debt relief at CUSM** for students serving needy areas — money flowing from the institution he chairs to the institution he sits on.

### The framing
- Sitting Riverside + San Bernardino supervisors had fiduciary duty over IEHP during the alleged-fraud period
- Spiegel was board chair 2021-Feb 2025 — covering the bulk of the alleged fraud window
- Hagman is the current chair receiving the case as it unfolds

### Beat shape
Governance accountability piece. "When the Medi-Cal money got stuck, here are the elected officials who had fiduciary duty over it." Tier 1 (DOJ filings, public board minutes). Low defamation surface — DOJ allegations are public.

### What's still gapped

- Bradley Gilbert current employer / status
- Spiegel + Gutierrez + Hagman supervisor campaign donor lists (NetFile public.netfile.com/pub2/?aid=CTRIV redirects to netfile.com/public/CTRIV/campaign — needs deeper crawl)
