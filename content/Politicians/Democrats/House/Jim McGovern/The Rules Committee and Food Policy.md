### The Rules Committee Ranking Member

Jim McGovern serves as ranking member on the House Rules Committee — the committee that controls which bills reach the floor and under what terms. The Rules Committee is the most powerful procedural body in the House: it determines amendment rules, debate time, and bill structure. Control of Rules effectively means control of the House floor.

McGovern represents Massachusetts's 2nd District (Worcester and the Pioneer Valley). His committee assignment has no particular relationship to his district's economy — the Rules Committee is a leadership-appointed body that serves the Speaker's agenda. McGovern's ranking member position makes him the Democratic counterpart to the Republican chairman, giving him procedural authority to shape minority party strategy on floor votes.

### The Hunger Caucus and SNAP Defense

McGovern's signature policy issue — separate from his procedural role — is hunger and food security. He co-chairs the Congressional Hunger Caucus and has been the leading Democratic voice defending SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) from Republican cuts. McGovern's hunger advocacy is genuine personal commitment (he has spoken extensively about visiting food banks and witnessing food insecurity) and politically safe (opposing hunger is universally popular; SNAP cuts poll poorly even among Republicans).

> [!money]
> McGovern's dual role — Rules Committee procedural warrior and hunger policy advocate — illustrates how committee assignments and policy interests can operate on entirely separate tracks. His Rules position serves the Democratic leadership's procedural needs; his hunger advocacy serves his personal policy commitment and his constituency. The Rules Committee generates no specific fundraising pipeline (it's a leadership-appointed body, not an interest-driven committee), making McGovern's political economy cleaner than most: he raises money on his hunger advocacy and his Massachusetts base, not on committee jurisdiction.
