### The Foreign Affairs Ranking Member

Gregory Meeks serves as ranking member of the House Foreign Affairs Committee and previously chaired it (2021-2023). He represents New York's 5th District (southeastern Queens, JFK Airport). Meeks is notable for having the highest financial services industry donor concentration of any member of the Congressional Black Caucus — his proximity to Wall Street and his committee assignments on both Foreign Affairs and Financial Services create a dual fundraising pipeline from finance and foreign policy-aligned donors.

Meeks' financial services donor base is unusual for a CBC member: Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan Chase, and securities industry PACs are consistently among his top contributors. AIPAC and pro-Israel donors also feature prominently, reflecting the Foreign Affairs Committee's jurisdiction over Israel-related policy.

---

### The Trade Policy Alignment

Meeks is one of the most pro-trade Democrats in the House — supporting TPP, USMCA, and expanded trade agreements. His trade positions align with Wall Street's preference for open markets and capital mobility. Meeks frames trade as benefiting his diverse district (which includes JFK Airport and trade-dependent communities); the material beneficiaries of the trade policies he supports are the financial institutions that profit from international capital flows.

> [!contradiction]
> Meeks represents one of the most economically diverse districts in New York — working-class Caribbean, South Asian, and African American communities in southeastern Queens. His top donors are Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan Chase. His trade positions benefit Wall Street; his constituents' primary economic concerns are housing costs, healthcare access, and wage stagnation. The donor-constituency gap is one of the widest in the Congressional Black Caucus.
