### New Hampshire's Defense-Diplomacy Senator

Jeanne Shaheen serves on Foreign Relations, Appropriations, and Armed Services — a committee portfolio that gives her jurisdiction over the full pipeline of defense and foreign policy: authorization, funding, and diplomatic oversight. She is the only woman to have served as both a governor and a US senator.

New Hampshire's defense economy centers on BAE Systems (Electronic Systems in Nashua), Raytheon (missile systems), and the Portsmouth Naval Shipyard (submarine maintenance). Shaheen's committee assignments align precisely with the industries that sustain her state's economy and fund her campaigns.

---

### The Moderate Appropriator

Shaheen's legislative brand is bipartisan pragmatism — working across the aisle on sanctions policy, NATO support, and defense appropriations. This moderate positioning is both genuine temperament and structural incentive: New Hampshire is a swing state where bipartisan credentials are electoral necessities, and the defense industry's PAC contributions flow to members of both parties who sit on relevant committees.

> Shaheen's triple-committee portfolio — Foreign Relations, Appropriations, Armed Services — gives her influence over authorization, funding, and diplomatic context for defense spending. The committee-jurisdiction-to-fundraising pipeline operates as designed.
