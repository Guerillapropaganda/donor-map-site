## Who They Are

Lisa Blunt Rochester. Democrat, Delaware. US Senator since January 2025, elected November 2024 to succeed retiring Senator Tom Carper. Previously Delaware's at-large House member (2017-2025, 4 terms), the first Black woman elected to Congress from Delaware. Born February 1962 in Philadelphia, PA. University of Delaware and Georgetown. Delaware Secretary of Health and Social Services and State Personnel Director under Governor Carper before Congress. Committee assignments not yet in pipeline. 2,014 roll-call votes tracked (117th-119th Congress, House and Senate). Voting record: 98.4% party-line loyalty across 1,948 substantive votes, 30 deviations. Delaware is a safe blue state; total outside support was $17K and total opposition was zero.

## The Central Thesis

Blunt Rochester is the Delaware senator whose donor map is shaped by a geographic accident of corporate law: Delaware is where more than 60% of Fortune 500 companies are incorporated, making Delaware senators the recipients of access money from virtually every major corporation in America regardless of whether those corporations have Delaware operations. Apollo Global Management ($14K), BlackRock, Blackstone, Fidelity, Goldman Sachs, Oracle, Apple, Amazon, Boeing, Lockheed Martin, and PhRMA all appear in her donor list. None of them have dominant Delaware constituency operations. All of them have Delaware charters. Coinbase, Nvidia, OpenAI, and Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) are the AI and crypto industry's investment in a new Democratic senator whose committee positions will affect their regulatory environment. ## Class Analysis

Blunt Rochester's $2.6M lifetime direct PAC record spans an eight-year House career and a Senate transition. The record is thin by Senate standards ($2.6M lifetime) because Delaware Senate races do not require large fundraising and her House races in a safely blue state were uncontested in practical terms. The $17K in total FOR super-PAC support is one of the lowest in the Democrat Senate cohort alongside Peter Welch. Delaware and Vermont are the two states safe enough that Democratic outside spending is unnecessary.

The corporate access list in her frontmatter is the longest and most economically diverse in the Democrat Senate cohort of profiles in this vault. The Delaware chartering dynamic explains most of it: companies incorporated in Delaware have a structural incentive to maintain relationships with Delaware senators because Delaware corporate law governs their internal governance, M&A activity, and shareholder rights. The Court of Chancery's rulings apply to their operations globally. A new Delaware senator is worth a relationship investment from every major corporation in America, regardless of sector.

The AI and crypto cluster (Coinbase, Nvidia, OpenAI, a16z, TikTok) is the non-chartering component of the frontmatter list. These are technology companies making regulatory bets: which Democratic senators will be sympathetic or at least open to crypto-asset legislation, AI regulation frameworks, and data governance questions. As a new Senate member from a small state with no dominant tech industry, Blunt Rochester's positioning on these issues is not yet defined by vote or committee work. The S. 1582 deviations in May 2025 (Yea when Democrats voted Nay, across two consecutive votes on the same bill) are the first signal. S. 1582's subject is not confirmed in the current pipeline, but the bipartisan crypto-stablecoin legislation active in 2025 (GENIUS Act and related) is the most likely context given the timing and her donor profile. This is flagged as a known gap requiring bill-level verification.

The 30 deviations at 98.4% loyalty in 1,948 substantive votes reflect a senator transitioning from House to Senate. ## Analytical Patterns

**Pilot Program (Delaware chartering senator).** Blunt Rochester is the first Black senator from Delaware and the first senator from the state since Carper and Biden's long tenures ended. She represents the test of whether a progressive House member can hold a Delaware seat that has historically been held by institutionalist Democrats (Biden, Carper) rather than progressives. The progressive coalition (SEIU, Planned Parenthood, Everytown) coexists with the corporate access coalition because Delaware's size means the seat is not competitive enough to force a choice.
