### The National Labs Senator

Martin Heinrich serves on Energy and Natural Resources, Armed Services, and the Intelligence Committee. New Mexico's economy is dominated by two national laboratories — Los Alamos and Sandia — plus Kirtland Air Force Base, White Sands Missile Range, and a growing renewable energy sector. Heinrich's committee portfolio covers every major revenue stream in his state's economy.

Los Alamos National Laboratory ($4.1 billion annual budget, managed by Triad National Security — a Battelle/University of California/Bechtel joint venture) and Sandia National Laboratories ($4.3 billion, managed by National Technology and Engineering Solutions of Sandia, a Honeywell subsidiary) together employ over 25,000 people. These labs are New Mexico's economic engine, and their budgets flow through the committees Heinrich sits on.

### The Clean Energy Transition — Nuclear Included

Heinrich advocates for clean energy transition while supporting nuclear weapons modernization — a position that is internally consistent for New Mexico, where "nuclear" means both weapons and energy. The NNSA's $25+ billion annual budget for nuclear weapons maintenance and modernization flows directly through New Mexico's labs. Heinrich frames nuclear energy as part of the clean energy portfolio, bridging the gap between defense hawks and climate progressives.
