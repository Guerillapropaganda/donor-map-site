### The McConnell Succession and the Majority Leader Fundraising Machine

> [!money]
> [[_John Thune Master Profile|John Thune]]'s election as Senate Majority Leader (November 13, 2024) was the culmination of a McConnell-designed succession. McConnell announced his step-down in February 2024 after a record 18 years as GOP leader. The contest: Thune (McConnell's protégé) vs. John Cornyn (McConnell ally who'd raised $414M+ over his Senate tenure, $33M for GOP candidates this cycle) vs. [[_Rick Scott Master Profile|Rick Scott]] (backed by [[Elon Musk|Elon Musk]], Tucker Carlson, [[_Marjorie Taylor Greene Master Profile|Marjorie Taylor Greene]] — the MAGA insurgent). First ballot eliminated Scott. Second ballot: Thune 29, Cornyn 24. The secret ballot was the key: it allowed establishment-aligned senators to vote for Thune without public MAGA backlash. Trump privately backed Thune (per Sen. Steve Daines) despite having threatened to primary him in 2022. The $4 million single transfer to NRSC — largest ever by a Senate Republican — was the financial proof of Thune's value to the caucus.

### The Secret Ballot Dynamic

The Senate Republican leadership election uses a secret ballot, meaning individual senators' votes are not disclosed. This mechanism is critical to understanding how Thune won:

- MAGA-aligned senators could publicly support [[_Rick Scott Master Profile|Rick Scott]] while privately voting for Thune
- Establishment senators could avoid the political cost of opposing the Trump-endorsed candidate
- The secret ballot protects the donor-class preference (institutional continuity) from the populist preference (MAGA disruption)
- Trump's private support for Thune (communicated through Daines) gave MAGA senators permission to defect without public accountability

> [!contradiction]
> The "anti-establishment" party selected its leader through the most establishment mechanism available — a secret ballot that shields donors' preferred outcome from populist pressure. [[_Rick Scott Master Profile|Rick Scott]], backed by Musk and the MAGA base, lost because the donor class wanted continuity. The secret ballot is the institutional design that makes donor capture durable: it separates public populist posturing from private institutional preference.
