### Alaska's Oil Senator

Lisa Murkowski served as chair and ranking member of the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee, the committee with jurisdiction over energy policy, public lands, and resource extraction. Alaska's economy is fundamentally dependent on oil: the state generates approximately 85% of its operating revenue from petroleum extraction, and every Alaskan receives an annual Permanent Fund Dividend (PFD) from oil wealth investment.

This makes Murkowski structurally inseparable from the oil industry, not because she is personally corrupt, but because her state literally cannot function without oil revenue. Every vote Murkowski casts on energy policy is simultaneously a constituent service vote (protecting Alaskan jobs and PFD payments) and a donor service vote (protecting oil industry profitability).

### The Willow Project

Murkowski was the leading Senate champion of ConocoPhillips' Willow Project, a massive $8 billion oil development on Alaska's North Slope expected to produce 180,000 barrels per day. Murkowski framed Willow as an economic necessity for Alaska's Native communities and state budget. ConocoPhillips is among her top donors. The project was approved by the Biden administration in 2023 after intense Murkowski lobbying.

### The Moderate Brand

Murkowski's moderate Republican brand, she voted to convict Trump in his second impeachment, supports abortion rights, and works across the aisle, provides the political cover that allows her oil industry service to avoid scrutiny. The moderate brand generates national media coverage about her independence; the oil industry alignment generates the donor revenue that sustains her campaigns. The two functions are complementary: the independence brand makes the oil industry service invisible.
