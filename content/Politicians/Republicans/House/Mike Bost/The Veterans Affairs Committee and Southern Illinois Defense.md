### The Veterans Affairs Chairman

Mike Bost chairs the House Veterans' Affairs Committee, overseeing the VA's $300+ billion budget. Bost represents Illinois's 12th District — Southern Illinois, including Scott Air Force Base (US Transportation Command) and a large veteran population. Bost is a Marine Corps veteran, and his personal military service anchors his political identity.

Bost's Veterans Committee chairmanship has focused on VA accountability, opposing what he frames as VA bureaucratic dysfunction, and supporting the MISSION Act's community care provisions — which allow veterans to seek care from private providers when VA wait times exceed standards. The community care expansion is the privatization-by-increment strategy: each expansion of private care options redirects VA funding to private healthcare companies.

### Scott Air Force Base and TRANSCOM

Scott Air Force Base hosts US Transportation Command (TRANSCOM) — the unified combatant command responsible for all military logistics. TRANSCOM manages the military's global transportation network, including airlift, sealift, and ground transportation. The base employs approximately 13,000 military and civilian personnel, making it the largest employer in Southern Illinois.

> [!money]
> Bost's dual role — Veterans Affairs chairman and Scott AFB representative — creates a compound defense spending interest. VA's $300+ billion budget and TRANSCOM's logistics operations both flow through his jurisdiction and district. Defense contractors contributing to Bost are investing in access to both spending pipelines simultaneously. The VA community care expansion redirects federal healthcare dollars to private providers — creating a new revenue stream for healthcare companies while appearing to improve veteran access.
