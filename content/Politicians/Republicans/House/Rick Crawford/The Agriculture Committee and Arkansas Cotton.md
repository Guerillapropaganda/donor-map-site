### The Agriculture-Intelligence Dual Committee

Rick Crawford serves on Agriculture and Intelligence — an unusual committee combination that reflects his district's economy (agriculture) and his military background (Army bomb disposal technician). Crawford represents Arkansas's 1st District, which encompasses the agricultural Delta region — rice, cotton, soybeans, and catfish farming. His Agriculture Committee seat gives him direct jurisdiction over the commodity programs and crop insurance that sustain Delta agriculture.

Crawford's Intelligence Committee seat provides a secondary power base: access to classified information and oversight of the intelligence community creates relationships with defense and intelligence contractors that supplement his agricultural donor base.

---

### The Commodity Subsidy Architecture

Arkansas's 1st District depends on federal agricultural subsidies: rice production receives the highest per-acre subsidy rates in the commodity program, cotton subsidies have repeatedly been challenged by Brazil at the WTO, and crop insurance subsidies reduce the financial risk of farming in the flood-prone Delta. Crawford's committee position ensures these subsidies continue — and his donor base reflects the beneficiaries: large-scale farm operations, agricultural cooperatives, and crop insurance companies.

> [!money]
> The agricultural commodity subsidy system — which Crawford's committee administers — distributes approximately $15 billion annually. The subsidies are structurally regressive: the top 10% of farm operations receive 70%+ of subsidy payments. Crawford's Delta district contains both large-scale commodity operations (which capture subsidy payments) and impoverished rural communities (which capture almost nothing). The committee jurisdiction serves the landed agricultural class, not the rural working class.
