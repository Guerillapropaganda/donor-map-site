### Purpose of This Note

Maps the fossil fuel donor network that funded the most comprehensive environmental deregulation in American history. 40 administration appointees have direct ties to oil, gas, and coal. Three EPA administrators were drawn from the regulated industry's own legal and lobbying infrastructure. The donor returns include the elimination of the legal foundation for climate regulation, record federal land lease sales, and $18 billion in new fossil fuel tax incentives.

---

### The Koch Network. $500 Million Per Cycle

> [!money]
> The **[[Koch Network - Charles Koch|Koch network]]** spends $500 million or more per political cycle across Americans for Prosperity, Freedom Partners, Donors Trust, and dozens of affiliated organizations. The network funds the policy infrastructure (Heritage Foundation, Cato Institute, Competitive Enterprise Institute) that produces the deregulatory framework, the political campaigns that elect the officials who implement it, and the judicial pipeline (through Leo network overlap) that upholds it when challenged. The Koch network's environmental policy returns across both Trump terms include Paris Agreement withdrawal (twice), Clean Power Plan repeal, Chevron deference overturning, methane rule rollbacks, ANWR opening, and the endangerment finding revocation. Each of these eliminates a constraint on fossil fuel extraction or emissions.

### Fossil Fuel Trade Associations

**[[American Petroleum Institute]] (API).** The oil and gas industry's primary trade association. $10 million or more in annual lobbying. API's policy priorities map directly to the administration's actions. Methane rule rollbacks, WOTUS narrowing, Clean Power Plan repeal, offshore drilling expansion, ANWR opening.

**National Mining Association.** Lobbied for coal industry protections. Andrew Wheeler lobbied for Murray Energy ($300K to $3.3M in fees) before becoming EPA Administrator. The revolving door between the coal lobby and EPA leadership is documented.

**Western Energy Alliance.** Represents oil and gas producers on federal lands in the West. Lobbied for expanded BLM lease sales and reduced royalty rates.
