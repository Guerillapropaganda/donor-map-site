### The Adelson Pipeline. Embassy, Abraham Accords, and Iran

> [!money]
> [[_Donald Trump Master Profile|Donald Trump]]'s Israel policy was the most direct donor to policy pipeline in modern presidential history. Sheldon Adelson donated $273 million to Republican causes over his lifetime and $25 million directly to Trump's 2016 inaugural. His widow [[Miriam Adelson]] donated $215 million through Preserve America PAC for the 2024 cycle. The policy deliverables were specific and sequential. Jerusalem embassy recognition (Adelson sat front row at the opening ceremony). Golan Heights sovereignty recognition. JCPOA withdrawal. The Abraham Accords. And in 2026 the Iran war Adelson had publicly advocated for years. [[AIPAC - American Israel Public Affairs Committee|AIPAC]] spent $126.9 million in the 2024 cycle across its affiliated PACs. [[United Democracy Project - UDP|United Democracy Project]], [[AIPAC - American Israel Public Affairs Committee|AIPAC]]'s super PAC, became the largest outside spender in multiple congressional primaries targeting candidates critical of Israel. The Kushner family's $2 billion Affinity Partners deal with the Saudi Public Investment Fund closed the loop between Middle East foreign policy and personal financial enrichment. The donors did not just influence the policy. They specified it. And the policy delivered returns measured in territorial recognition, military contracts, and personal wealth.

### The Adelson Donations and the Policy Deliverables

Sheldon Adelson was the largest individual political donor in American history at the time of his death in January 2021. His lifetime giving to Republican causes exceeded $273 million. He donated $25 million to Trump's 2016 inaugural committee and over $75 million to Trump aligned super PACs across 2016 and 2020.

Miriam Adelson continued the operation after his death. Her $215 million through Preserve America PAC in the 2024 cycle made her the single largest donor to the presidential race. Reports indicated she sought Israeli sovereignty over the West Bank as the next policy deliverable.

The policy sequence maps directly to Adelson's publicly stated priorities. Jerusalem embassy recognition. Golan Heights sovereignty. Iran confrontation. These were not policies that emerged from interagency deliberation or national security assessment. They were items on a donor's wish list, delivered in sequence.

> [!note] Analytical Pattern. Two Audience Problem
> For the evangelical base the story was biblical prophecy and the fulfillment of God's covenant with Israel. For the donor class the story was territorial and strategic. $273 million in donations produced two territorial recognitions (Jerusalem and Golan Heights), one nuclear deal withdrawal, four normalization agreements, and eventually a war. The two audiences heard different justifications for identical policies. The evangelical audience provided the political cover. The donor class provided the funding and specified the deliverables.

### The Abraham Accords. Normalization as Trade Deal

The Abraham Accords produced diplomatic normalization between Israel and four countries. UAE (August 2020), Bahrain (September 2020), Sudan (October 2020), and Morocco (December 2020). Each agreement involved a side deal.

**UAE.** Received F 35 stealth fighter sale commitment in exchange for normalization.

**Sudan.** Removed from state sponsor of terrorism list in exchange for normalization. Had been seeking removal for years.

**Morocco.** Received U.S. recognition of sovereignty over disputed Western Sahara in exchange for normalization.

None of these countries had been in active conflict with Israel. The accords formalized economic relationships that already existed informally. The countries that would have represented genuine breakthroughs (Saudi Arabia, which was the strategic prize) did not join.

The accords were a genuine diplomatic achievement with structural limits. They normalized what was already happening. They did not resolve the Palestinian conflict. And they came with territorial and military concessions that set precedents with long term consequences.

### The Kushner Loop. Foreign Policy as Family Business

Jared Kushner led Middle East policy as senior advisor. After leaving the White House he founded Affinity Partners, a private equity firm. The Saudi Public Investment Fund committed $2 billion to Affinity Partners despite concerns from the fund's own screening committee about the firm's lack of track record.

The $110 billion Saudi arms deal signed in May 2017 was the largest single arms sale in U.S. history. Kushner's relationship with Mohammed bin Salman was central to both the arms deal and the broader Saudi relationship. When Khashoggi was murdered in the Saudi consulate in October 2018, Trump defended the Saudi government with "maybe he did, maybe he didn't." The arms deal and the relationship were preserved.

> [!money]
> The Kushner timeline is the clearest case of the revolving door between foreign policy and personal enrichment. Senior advisor shapes Middle East policy including $110 billion arms deal. Defends Saudi government after journalist murder. Leaves office. Receives $2 billion investment from Saudi sovereign wealth fund. The policy and the payday are connected by a direct line. The two year gap between leaving office and closing the deal is the only thing that separates it from an explicit transaction.

### Iran. The War Adelson Asked For

Sheldon Adelson publicly stated in 2013 that the United States should detonate a nuclear weapon in the Iranian desert as a warning. The policy trajectory under Trump moved consistently toward confrontation with Iran. JCPOA withdrawal in May 2018 eliminated the diplomatic framework. The Soleimani strike in January 2020 brought the two countries to the brink. By February 2026 the war had begun.

As of March 2026 the Iran war was in its fourth week. $12 billion had been spent. A $200 billion supplemental budget request was pending before Congress. Lockheed Martin was scaling Patriot missile production from 600 to 2,000 units per year. Defense contractor stocks were at all time highs.

> [!contradiction]
> The donor who spent $273 million on Republican politics publicly advocated for military confrontation with Iran. The president he funded withdrew from the deal that constrained Iran's nuclear program. Killed Iran's top military commander. And ultimately went to war. At each stage the policy was framed as national security. At each stage the policy matched the donor's publicly stated preference. The $273 million was not spent to influence policy. It was spent to purchase it. The war is the final deliverable.

### AIPAC. The Congressional Enforcement Layer

While the Adelson donations shaped presidential policy, AIPAC and its affiliated PACs enforced discipline at the congressional level. The 2024 cycle spending of $126.9 million made AIPAC's network the largest single issue outside spender in American politics.

[[United Democracy Project - UDP|United Democracy Project]], AIPAC's super PAC, spent heavily in Democratic primaries targeting candidates who had been critical of Israeli government policies or supportive of Palestinian rights. The message to Democratic candidates was explicit. Criticize Israel and face millions in opposition spending in your next primary.

The spending created a structural veto over Middle East policy debate in Congress. Members who might otherwise question arms sales, settlements, or military operations understood that dissent carried a measurable financial cost.
