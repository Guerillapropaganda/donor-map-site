### The [[Koch Network - Charles Koch|Koch Network]] Pipeline and Climate Denial as Policy

> [!money]
> [[_Chris Wright Master Profile|Chris Wright]]'s path to Energy Secretary ran through the Koch donor network. $100,000 to [[Americans for Prosperity|Americans for Prosperity]] Action (2024). $15,000 to Freedom Partners Action Fund (2014). He and wife Elizabeth wrote "we proudly stand with the Kochs." The Koch network — which spent $178 million backing Republicans in 2024 — provided the ideological and financial infrastructure that connected Wright to Trump's inner circle. Wright's climate denial isn't personal eccentricity. ### "Energy Sobriety" and Climate Denial

Wright calls himself a "climate realist" — a rebranding of denial that acknowledges warming while opposing all mitigation:

- **January 2023 LinkedIn video:** "There is no climate crisis and we're not in the midst of an energy transition either"
- **Union of Concerned Scientists response:** Wright "deliberately misrepresents climate data and research"
- **"Bettering Human Lives" (2024):** Liberty Energy's 180-page document arguing fossil fuels are morally essential for human development
- **Bjorn Lomborg friendship:** Wright describes the prominent climate crisis minimizer as "a friend"

The rhetoric serves a specific function: create intellectual permission for the policies the donor class wants. If there's no crisis, there's no need for regulation. If fossil fuels are humanitarian, opposing them is inhumane. The language produces the policy outcome.

> [!contradiction]
> Wright says "there is no climate crisis" while running the department that maintains the country's climate research infrastructure — including 17 national labs, NOAA partnerships, and the DOE's own climate modeling programs. His proposed 70% cut to renewable energy research doesn't just reflect his beliefs. It eliminates the capacity to produce evidence that contradicts them. The denier isn't just denying the science. He's defunding it.
