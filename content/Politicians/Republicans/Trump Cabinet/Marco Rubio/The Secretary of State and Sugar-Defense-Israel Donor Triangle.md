### The Secretary of State Appointment

Rubio was appointed Secretary of State by Donald Trump in January 2025, vacating his Florida Senate seat. The appointment elevated Rubio from a donor-service senator to the nation's chief diplomat — a position where his donor relationships take on new significance. His hawkish positions on Cuba, Venezuela, China, and Iran — developed during his Senate career and shaped by his Miami Cuban-American constituency — now determine US foreign policy.

Rubio's foreign policy positions align with his three primary donor blocs: pro-Israel organizations (AIPAC, which invested in Rubio's political career), defense contractors (who benefit from hawkish foreign policy that increases military spending), and the Florida sugar industry (the Fanjul family, whose sugar empire depends on the US sugar subsidy program and trade protections).

---

### The Fanjul Sugar Connection

The Fanjul family — Alfonso and Jose "Pepe" Fanjul — controls Florida Crystals and Domino Sugar, the largest sugarcane operation in the United States. The Fanjul family's political contributions ($5+ million per cycle across both parties) protect the US sugar program — a system of import quotas, price supports, and loans that costs American consumers an estimated $3.5 billion annually in higher sugar prices. Rubio has been a consistent defender of the sugar program — unsurprising given the Fanjul family's status as one of his largest donor networks.

> [!money]
> Rubio's donor triangle — sugar (Fanjul family), defense (contractor PACs), and Israel (AIPAC) — has been elevated from Senate committee influence to Cabinet-level foreign policy authority. As Secretary of State, Rubio's hawkish positions on Cuba, Venezuela, and Iran now shape actual diplomatic outcomes rather than just Senate votes. The donor relationships that funded his Senate career now benefit from his authority to direct US foreign policy. The Revolving Door has evolved: instead of moving from government to lobbying, Rubio moved from donor-funded senator to foreign policy authority — carrying his donor relationships into the most powerful diplomatic position in the world.
