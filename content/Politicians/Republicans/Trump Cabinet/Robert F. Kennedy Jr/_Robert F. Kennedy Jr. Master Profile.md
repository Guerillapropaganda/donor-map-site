### Who He Is.

Robert F. Kennedy Jr. is Trump's Secretary of Health and Human Services. He is a former environmental attorney, anti-vaccine activist, and third-party presidential candidate who suspended his 2024 campaign and endorsed Trump in exchange for a health policy portfolio. He is the son of Robert F. Kennedy and nephew of President John F. Kennedy. His appointment to HHS — the agency overseeing the FDA, CDC, NIH, and CMS — placed the nation's pharmaceutical regulatory apparatus under the control of the most prominent vaccine skeptic in American public life.

### The Central Thesis.

Kennedy's appointment represents a donor-class realignment within health policy. His "Make America Healthy Again" platform attracted a coalition of anti-pharma populists, wellness industry donors, and supplement company executives who stand to benefit from reduced FDA regulatory authority. The same pharmaceutical industry that RFK Jr. publicly attacks has financial interests that are paradoxically served by regulatory chaos — weakened FDA oversight benefits both the companies avoiding scrutiny and the alternative health industry selling unregulated products. Follow the money on both sides of the "health freedom" movement and the same class interests emerge: deregulation as product, marketed as populism.
