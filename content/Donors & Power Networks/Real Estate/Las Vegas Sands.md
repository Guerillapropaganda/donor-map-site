### Who They Are

Las Vegas Sands Corporation is one of the world's largest casino and hospitality companies, founded by Sheldon Adelson in 1989 and generating approximately $16 billion in annual revenue before his death in January 2021. Adelson was one of the wealthiest men in America with a net worth exceeding $35 billion, making him the principal political benefactor of the Republican Party throughout his lifetime. Since his death, his widow Miriam Adelson has assumed control of the company and inherited his role as mega-donor.

### What They Want

Las Vegas Sands' political interests center on favorable tax treatment of casino operators, light regulation of gambling operations, opposition to labor unions in hospitality, and protection of the company's profitable international operations (particularly in Macau and Singapore). Sheldon Adelson personally prioritized Israeli government interests and staunchly pro-Israel U.S. foreign policy, making the company's political spending heavily weighted toward pro-Israel causes alongside casino industry lobbying.

### Who They Fund

Sheldon Adelson and Las Vegas Sands donated over $500 million to Republican causes, Super PACs, and pro-Israel organizations throughout his lifetime, making the Adelson family the largest single-family donor to Republican politics in American history. Major recipients included Mitt Romney (2012 and 2016 presidential campaigns), Newt Gingrich, Donald Trump (both 2016 and 2020), and numerous Republican congressional candidates. Adelson funded major pro-Israel advocacy organizations including the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC) and various legal defense organizations supporting Israeli settlement expansion.

### What They've Gotten

Las Vegas Sands secured favorable tax treatment for casino operators through Republican administrations and bipartisan congressional allies. The company maintained minimal labor unionization in Las Vegas and internationally through Adelson's political spending, which helped elect union-opposition politicians. Adelson's massive donations to pro-Israel causes shaped Republican Middle East policy to be unambiguously pro-Israel, creating a constraint on Republican foreign policy debate. Sheldon Adelson achieved effective control over Republican presidential primary politics in 2012 and 2016 through his ability to fund candidates single-handedly.

### Class Analysis

Sheldon Adelson exemplifies the mega-donor as foreign policy architect: he used American political donations to shape U.S. Middle East policy to serve Israeli government interests (not even necessarily Israeli citizens' interests, but specifically right-wing Israeli government policy). The fact that Republican presidential candidates competed for Adelson's approval reveals how concentrated wealth can transfer control of foreign policy to individual billionaires. His opposition to labor unions demonstrates how political spending allows corporations to circumvent workers' collective bargaining power. Since his death, his widow has inherited his political role entirely — a dynastic transfer of political power mediated entirely through private wealth, with no democratic legitimacy or constituency.
