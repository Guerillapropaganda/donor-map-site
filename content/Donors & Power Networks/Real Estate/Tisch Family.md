### Who They Are

The Tisch family is a New York-based business dynasty controlling Loews Corporation, a holding company with substantial assets in real estate, hotels (the Loews hotel chain), insurance (CNA Financial), and other operations. The family accumulated wealth through major Manhattan real estate development and hotel operations beginning in the mid-20th century. The Tischs have emerged as one of New York's most significant bipartisan political donors, contributing to both Democratic and Republican candidates across state and local politics.

### What They Want

The Tisch family advances interests aligned with their corporate holdings: favorable regulatory treatment for hotels and hospitality industries, real estate and zoning favorable to property development and hotel operations, insurance industry deregulation, and general business-friendly policy frameworks. The family maintains a bipartisan approach to politics, funding supportive candidates of both parties to ensure policy access regardless of which party controls government.

### Who They Fund

The Tisch family distributed an estimated $10-15M in campaign contributions across 2020-2024 cycles, with funds flowing to: NYC mayoral candidates of both parties, New York governors and gubernatorial candidates (both parties), state legislators on key committees, and major federal candidates. The family's contributions go systematically to whoever controls political power, regardless of party affiliation.

### What They've Gotten

The Tisch family has benefited from: hotel industry-friendly tax treatment, real estate zoning accommodations for their properties, minimal regulatory obstacles to hotel operations, and favorable insurance industry regulation. The family's bipartisan giving strategy has ensured that Tisch interests receive favorable treatment regardless of which party controls government. Major Tisch family members serve on prestigious boards and commissions, providing institutional access beyond campaign contributions.

> [!contradiction] The Tisch family presents itself as civic-minded major donors and patrons while their bipartisan giving strategy protects wealth concentration and corporate interests regardless of political outcomes. The family's ability to maintain close relationships with candidates of both parties reveals that both parties serve the same donor-class interests, despite public positioning as opposites.

### Class Analysis

The Tisch family represents the bipartisan donor consensus: both major parties serve business interests, and major donors maintain access to power by funding both sides. The family's $10-15M contribution level is modest compared to Wall Street mega-donors but provides substantial political leverage because the Tischs' corporate holdings are concentrated, creating structural dependencies. Politicians of both parties must accommodate Tisch interests because the family's real estate, hotels, and other operations are central to NYC economic activity. The Tischs' success demonstrates that political power flows not only from donation size but from control of essential economic resources that politicians cannot ignore. The family's bipartisan strategy reveals an important truth: despite electoral competition and partisan conflict, both parties prioritize donor-class interests identically.
