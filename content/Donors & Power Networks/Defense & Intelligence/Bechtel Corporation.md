### Who They Are

Bechtel Corporation is the largest private construction company in the United States, founded in 1898 and operating as a closely held family corporation. Bechtel's primary revenue streams flow from: major defense and military infrastructure contracts (bases, weapons facilities), civilian infrastructure projects (dams, nuclear facilities, airports), and international development contracts. The company operates substantial revolving-door relationships with the Pentagon and State Department, with executives regularly rotating between Bechtel and government positions.

### What They Want

Bechtel advances defense industry interests including: sustained high levels of military spending (particularly for infrastructure and base construction), government contracting bias favoring large private contractors over in-house government operations, minimal regulatory constraints on construction and environmental standards, and support for military interventions that generate infrastructure contracts (Iraq War generated $20B+ in Bechtel/KBR operations).

### Who They Fund

Bechtel distributed an estimated $5-8M in campaign contributions and lobbying spending across 2020-2024 cycles, with funds flowing to: defense committee members in Congress (both parties), Defense Department oversight legislators, members supporting military spending, and defense-friendly gubernatorial candidates. Bechtel also operates substantial revolving-door partnerships, regularly placing executives in Pentagon and State Department positions and hiring government officials for corporate roles.

### What They've Gotten

Bechtel secured major contracts including: Iraq War infrastructure and base construction (estimated $10B+), Afghanistan reconstruction projects, Pentagon base construction and maintenance contracts, and international development infrastructure contracts. The company's revolving-door relationships have provided sustained Pentagon access and favorable contracting treatment. Bechtel's profit margins on defense contracts remain substantially higher than comparable civilian construction, revealing that defense industry access provides significant financial advantages.

> [!money] Bechtel's Iraq War revenue demonstrates how military interventions generate corporate contracting opportunity. The company's $10B+ Iraq revenue flow directly from U.S. military presence, revealing that corporate profit incentives align with continued military intervention regardless of public interest or strategic necessity.

### Class Analysis

Bechtel exemplifies military-industrial complex dynamics: a major defense contractor with sustained lobbying influence over military spending, revolving-door access to Pentagon decision-making, and direct financial interest in military intervention. The company's business model depends on high military spending and infrastructure projects—profit incentives align with sustained militarism and military presence. Bechtel's revolving-door relationships reveal structural alignment between corporate interests and military leadership, making clear separation between military strategy and corporate profit impossible. The company's sustained success in securing contracts despite questionable performance records and cost overruns demonstrates that defense contractors operate with minimal accountability—once inside the military-industrial system, performance metrics matter less than relationships and access.
