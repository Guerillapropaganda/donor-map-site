### Who They Are

Bechtel Corporation is the largest private construction company in the United States, founded in 1898 and operating as a closely held family corporation. Bechtel's primary revenue streams flow from: major defense and military infrastructure contracts (bases, weapons facilities), civilian infrastructure projects (dams, nuclear facilities, airports), and international development contracts. The company operates substantial revolving-door relationships with the Pentagon and State Department, with executives regularly rotating between Bechtel and government positions.

### What They Want

Bechtel advances defense industry interests including: sustained high levels of military spending (particularly for infrastructure and base construction), government contracting bias favoring large private contractors over in-house government operations, minimal regulatory constraints on construction and environmental standards, and support for military interventions that generate infrastructure contracts (Iraq War generated $20B+ in Bechtel/KBR operations).

Bechtel distributed an estimated $5-8M in campaign contributions and lobbying spending across 2020-2024 cycles, with funds flowing to: defense committee members in Congress (both parties), Defense Department oversight legislators, members supporting military spending, and defense-friendly gubernatorial candidates. Bechtel also operates substantial revolving-door partnerships, regularly placing executives in Pentagon and State Department positions and hiring government officials for corporate roles.
