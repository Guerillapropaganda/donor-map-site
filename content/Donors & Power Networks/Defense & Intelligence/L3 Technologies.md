### Who They Are

L3 Technologies (merged into L3Harris Technologies in 2019) is a major U.S. defense contractor specializing in surveillance technology, electronic warfare systems, and military communications equipment. Operating as a prime contractor to the Department of Defense, National Security Agency, and Intelligence Community, L3 provides critical surveillance infrastructure and weapons systems. The company's primary revenue comes from government defense and intelligence contracts, making L3 a core component of the military-industrial complex.

### What They Want

L3 Technologies advances defense industry and intelligence community interests including: sustained high levels of defense/intelligence spending, continued investment in surveillance technology infrastructure, government contracting advantages for large prime contractors, minimal regulatory oversight of weapons systems development, and support for military and intelligence operations requiring L3 technology.

### Who They Fund

L3 Technologies distributed an estimated $3-5M in campaign contributions and lobbying spending across 2020-2024 cycles, with funding flowing to: defense committee members in Congress (both parties), Intelligence Committee members, members supporting military spending, and defense-friendly gubernatorial candidates. The company also maintains revolving-door relationships with Pentagon and intelligence community positions.

### What They've Gotten

L3 Technologies secured: continued government contracts for surveillance and communications systems worth billions annually, sustained Department of Defense and NSA funding, no meaningful regulatory oversight of weapons systems, and revolving-door access to Pentagon decision-making. The company's surveillance technology infrastructure powers U.S. intelligence operations, making L3's business success central to intelligence community operations.

> [!money] L3's surveillance technology contracts generate billions in recurring revenue as military forces require constant surveillance equipment updates. This structural dependency means Pentagon leadership has ongoing incentives to maintain L3 relationships and contract awards regardless of performance or cost overruns.

### Class Analysis

L3 Technologies exemplifies how defense contractors operate within military-industrial complex dynamics: sustained government spending guarantees corporate revenue, political influence ensures contract awards, and revolving-door relationships eliminate separation between corporate interests and military strategy. The company's surveillance technology operations reveal that the military-industrial complex includes electronic surveillance as core infrastructure. L3's success in maintaining contracts despite technological obsolescence (surveillance systems require constant upgrade) demonstrates that military-industrial complex dynamics eliminate market accountability—performance doesn't determine contract awards, relationships do.
