### Who They Are

The American Israel Public Affairs Committee. The most powerful foreign policy lobbying organization in American politics. AIPAC operates through three financial channels: AIPAC itself (a 501(c)(4) lobbying organization, does not make direct contributions), AIPAC PAC (a federal political action committee, makes direct contributions to candidates), and the [[United Democracy Project - UDP|United Democracy Project]] (UDP, a super PAC that spends unlimited amounts on independent expenditures for and against candidates).

The combined operation spent approximately **$126.9 million in the 2024 cycle**, making it one of the single largest political spending operations in American politics, comparable to the Koch network and the crypto industry's [[Fairshake PAC|Fairshake PAC]]. [Source: [Read Sludge: All the money AIPAC spent on 2024 elections](https://readsludge.com/2025/01/24/here-is-all-the-money-aipac-spent-on-the-2024-elections/) (Tier 2) (VERIFIED)]

### The Class Analysis

AIPAC spent $126.9 million in the 2024 cycle. That money comes from a small number of extremely wealthy individuals, not from a grassroots base. The United Democracy Project's top donors include Jan Koum ($5 million, WhatsApp co-founder), Jonathon Jacobson ($4.6 million, Highfields Capital hedge fund), Bernard Marcus ($3 million, Home Depot co-founder), Haim Saban (seven figures, media), and Paul Singer (seven figures, Elliott Management hedge fund). The concentration in venture capital, hedge funds, and tech billionaires is the class composition in plain sight.

The mechanism is the primary election. AIPAC does not need every member of Congress to support Israel. It needs every member of Congress to fear the cost of criticizing Israel. That cost is quantified: $14.9 million against Jamaal Bowman in NY-16, the most expensive House primary in American history. $8.6 million against Cori Bush in MO-1. Both lost. The message reaches every other member without a dollar being spent on them. When 356 House members and 40 senators received pro-Israel PAC money in 2024, the coverage is not about electing allies. It is about maintaining a floor of compliance across the entire institution.

The cross-party donor pipeline reveals the class function most clearly. According to Politico analysis, 46% of donors who gave to Democratic candidates via AIPAC in 2024 had also given to Republicans since 2020, compared to just 2% of ActBlue donors generally. Republican hedge fund managers and venture capitalists fund attacks on progressive Democrats through AIPAC's bundling conduit. In the Bowman-Latimer race, approximately 40% of Latimer's AIPAC-channeled donors had previously given to Republicans. AIPAC is the pipe through which donor-class capital crosses party lines to discipline elected officials on a single issue. The ads never mention Israel. They attack candidates on policing, on fire alarms, on domestic issues. The donor motivation is Israel policy. The advertisement content is whatever polls best in the district. The gap between funding source and ad content is the structural tell.

The return on investment is concrete. Since October 2023, Congress has approved $26.4 billion in emergency military aid to Israel on top of the standing $3.8 billion annual commitment. No conditions on weapons transfers. Near-universal congressional support for Israeli military operations. The two most prominent congressional critics of Israel removed through primary challenges. AIPAC spent $126.9 million and secured $30+ billion in policy outcomes. The lobbying infrastructure ($3.3 million in registered expenditures, 65-70% of all pro-Israel sector lobbying) and the AIEF travel program ($10 million in congressional luxury trips to Israel since 2012, nearly half of all current House members) reinforce the electoral spending. The system operates on fear, access, and financial discipline simultaneously.

### The Bundling Architecture (Pre-2022)

Before AIPAC created its own PAC in 2022, the organization operated through a coordinated but legally separate network of bundlers and informal PACs:
