### Who They Are

Ballard Partners is the third-largest federal lobbying firm in the United States, founded and led by Brian Ballard, a veteran Republican lobbyist with deep ties to Donald Trump and the Republican establishment. The firm specializes in foreign and domestic lobbying, with a particularly lucrative practice area in unlawful clemency petitions and corporate legal work. As of 2023-2024, Ballard Partners reported over $88 million in foreign lobbying revenue alone, making it one of the most successful foreign agent firms operating in Washington.

### What They Want

Ballard Partners operates on a client-service model: it wants sustained relationships with high-net-worth individuals, foreign governments, and corporations requiring access to federal power. The firm's core interest is maintaining revolving-door access to the Trump administration (and other GOP power centers) while converting that access into billable hours. Their clemency practice specifically targets wealthy clients seeking presidential pardons and commutations — a market that became extremely lucrative during Trump's first term.

### Who They Fund

Brian Ballard personally has donated to Republican causes, Trump's campaigns, and various GOP political action committees. The firm itself maintains corporate PAC operations and bundling capacity. Key recipients include Marco Rubio (Ballard's former employer and ongoing client), Republican Senate candidates, and Trump's legal defense funds. The firm's funding flows through traditional Republican channels while maintaining relationships across the Trump ecosystem.

### What They've Gotten

Ballard Partners achieved peak influence during Trump's first term (2017-2021), when the firm's clemency practice generated an estimated $5.2 million in lobbying revenue as wealthy clients paid to secure presidential pardons. Notable successes include securing clemency for Paul Manafort (Trump's campaign chairman) and high-profile financial crimes defendants. The firm's foreign lobbying clients have included Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates, and various pharmaceutical companies seeking FDA action. Under Trump's second term, Ballard Partners has positioned itself as the premier access point for clemency petitions and regulatory relief.

### Class Analysis

Ballard Partners is a pure distribution mechanism for elite legal and political access: it exists to convert client wealth into government favor. The firm's clemency practice exemplifies the two-tiered justice system — wealthy defendants with millions to spend on lobbying can purchase their way out of federal prison, while ordinary defendants face mandatory minimums. The fact that clemency has become a commodified service, with fixed price points and billable hour structures, reveals the extent to which presidential power has been privatized. The firm's Trump alignment is not ideological but purely transactional: Trump grants pardons, Ballard Partners captures the market, and both parties profit from the sale of executive clemency.
