### Who They Are

Craft Ventures is a venture capital firm co-founded by David Sacks (formerly of PayPal, Zenefits, and SlideShare) in 2017. The firm manages approximately $2.5 billion in assets and focuses on investments in crypto, AI, fintech, and enterprise software. Sacks personally has invested in major cryptocurrency platforms and AI companies, positioning himself at the intersection of Silicon Valley wealth and cryptocurrency industry interests. Craft Ventures has become a major player in cryptocurrency venture funding, backing projects across the blockchain ecosystem.

### What They Want

Craft Ventures and David Sacks want regulatory clarity favorable to cryptocurrency and AI development, prevention of strict SEC enforcement against cryptocurrency investments, favorable tax treatment of venture capital gains, and deregulation of AI development to permit rapid scaling without extensive government oversight. Sacks personally advocates for libertarian cryptocurrency ideology and opposes government financial regulation. The firm wants to preserve the startup ecosystem's favorable treatment and prevent regulatory restrictions on venture capital investment in emerging technologies.

### Who They Fund

David Sacks has donated to pro-crypto Republican candidates and funds cryptocurrency policy advocacy organizations. As Trump's AI/Crypto czar (appointed in Trump's second term), Sacks has direct influence over federal AI and cryptocurrency policy. Craft Ventures itself maintains an active corporate political operation, though the firm operates more through industry advocacy and policy influence than direct campaign contributions. Sacks has been influential in Silicon Valley political circles and has participated in major fundraising events for Republican candidates.

### What They've Gotten

David Sacks' appointment as Trump's AI/Crypto czar in 2025 represents the most direct capture of regulatory authority by a venture capitalist with massive financial interests in the industries he now oversees. This constitutes an unprecedented conflict of interest: Sacks' personal wealth is substantially dependent on cryptocurrency price appreciation and favorable crypto regulation, while his government position allows him to shape the regulatory environment that affects those investments. Craft Ventures' cryptocurrency investments have benefited from regulatory clarity that Sacks himself has influenced through government positions. The firm has secured favorable treatment for its portfolio companies in areas ranging from AI safety regulation to cryptocurrency exchange oversight.

### Class Analysis

David Sacks exemplifies the revolving door in its purest form: a venture capitalist with billions in crypto investments who directly controls the federal government's crypto policy. The fact that Trump appointed someone with direct financial interests in cryptocurrency to oversee cryptocurrency regulation reveals the complete capture of regulatory authority by oligarchic interests. Sacks' AI czar position is equally problematic — he controls federal AI policy while his venture capital fund invests in AI companies that would benefit from light-touch regulation. This is not simply corruption; it's the normalization of regulatory authority as a profitable asset for venture capitalists. The "both-sides" rhetoric of venture capitalists obscures the fundamental conflict: they support "deregulation" not from ideological principle but from direct financial interest.
