### Who They Are

National Nurses United (NNU) is the largest nurses union in the United States, representing approximately 225,000 registered nurses across multiple healthcare systems and settings. Founded in 2009 through the merger of several regional nurses unions, NNU has emerged as one of America's most militant and progressive labor organizations. The union is headquartered in Oakland, California and has positioned itself as a leading voice for both workers' rights and patient advocacy, arguing that nurse working conditions and patient safety are fundamentally connected.

### What They Want

NNU's core political interests are winning Medicare for All as the foundation for healthcare in the United States, protecting and expanding nurse staffing ratios (legally mandated nurse-to-patient ratios), combating healthcare privatization, and strengthening labor organizing rights for healthcare workers. The union wants to transform healthcare from a profit-driven market system into a public system where medical decisions are based on patient need rather than corporate profit. NNU has consistently opposed healthcare privatization schemes including public-private partnerships and "managed competition."

### Who They Fund

National Nurses United contributes substantially to Medicare for All advocates regardless of party, but focuses primarily on Democrats aligned with progressive healthcare policy. The union funds progressive primary challengers against corporate-friendly incumbent Democrats, has supported candidates including Bernie Sanders and Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, and mobilizes significant member volunteer activity beyond direct contributions. NNU's funding is supplemented by massive member mobilization — the union regularly organizes nurse strikes and political demonstrations that function as a form of political power beyond traditional donations.

### What They've Gotten

NNU has been the most consistent institutional voice for Medicare for All in American politics, maintaining this position even as other unions compromised or abandoned the position. The union has secured modest improvements in nurse-to-patient ratio laws in several states through direct action and political organizing. NNU has prevented healthcare corporations from completely controlling the Democratic Party's healthcare policy position, maintaining a space for genuine healthcare reform advocacy within Democratic politics. The union has built one of America's most militant labor organizations, conducting successful strikes against major healthcare corporations and hospital systems.

### Class Analysis

National Nurses United represents class analysis embedded within labor organizing: the union consistently connects healthcare worker exploitation to patient harm, arguing that understaffing that maximizes corporate profit directly harms patients. This analysis rejects the false choice between worker interests and patient interests, instead arguing that they are aligned against corporate management. NNU's aggressive pursuit of Medicare for All represents a fundamental challenge to healthcare corporations' profit extraction: universal healthcare would eliminate the ability of hospital corporations to charge whatever the market will bear. The union's militancy — its willingness to strike and disrupt healthcare operations — demonstrates that fundamental healthcare reform requires confrontation with corporate power, not compromise with it.
