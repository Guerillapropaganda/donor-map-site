### Who They Are

SV&B PAC is a sports betting industry political action committee representing DraftKings, FanDuel, and other major sports betting operators. The PAC operates to advance sports betting industry interests at state and federal levels, specifically opposing taxes on gambling operations and regulatory frameworks limiting industry profitability. SV&B functions as the consolidated political arm of the sports betting industry, which has grown dramatically following legalization in most U.S. states.

### What They Want

SV&B PAC advances sports betting industry interests including: opposition to gambling taxes, opposition to regulations limiting betting access or frequency, support for favorable regulatory frameworks, prevention of state monopoly betting operations, and expansion of legal sports betting into additional states and betting types. The PAC frames gambling expansion as "consumer choice" while actually protecting industry profit maximization against taxation and regulation.

### Who They Fund

SV&B PAC distributed an estimated $5-8M in campaign contributions across 2020-2024 cycles, with funding flowing to: state legislators opposing gambling taxes and favoring industry-friendly regulation, ballot measure campaigns opposing gambling tax initiatives, gubernatorial candidates supporting industry expansion, and federal candidates opposing sports gambling regulation. The PAC concentrates spending in states with recent legalization decisions or active tax/regulation debates.

### What They've Gotten

SV&B PAC has secured: defeat of gambling tax proposals in multiple states, regulatory frameworks favorable to operator profitability, ability to expand betting products and advertising, and continued growth of sports betting market without meaningful taxation. The PAC's successful tax opposition has allowed sports betting operators to generate billions in revenue while paying minimal taxes, exemplifying how industry lobbying can prevent taxation of profitable industries.

> [!money] SV&B PAC's opposition to gambling taxes demonstrates that sports betting industry can operate profitably in legalized states while avoiding meaningful taxation. The industry's $40B+ annual revenue base generates relatively minimal state tax revenue, revealing successful industry lobbying preventing taxation of profitable gambling operations.

### Class Analysis

SV&B PAC exemplifies how emerging industries can prevent taxation through concentrated political spending. Sports betting operators, facing new regulatory environments in states legalizing gambling, deployed substantial spending to shape favorable regulatory and tax frameworks. The PAC's success in blocking gambling taxes reveals that industry can prevent taxation when industry spending exceeds tax-revenue-seeking political pressure. SV&B demonstrates that sports betting operators have translated legalization into substantial political power, ensuring continued profitability despite generating minimal state revenue.
