### Who They Are

One Nation is a 501(c)(4) "dark money" organization registered as a tax-exempt social welfare group that operates as a de facto independent Super PAC aligned with Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell and the Republican Senate establishment. Established in 2013, One Nation has become one of the largest and most successful dark money vehicles in American politics, spending over $50 million per election cycle on Senate races while maintaining complete donor secrecy. The organization's political operatives are primarily drawn from McConnell's Senate leadership operation and affiliated Republican consulting firms.

### What They Want

One Nation's core interest is electing and reelecting Republican senators, particularly those aligned with McConnell's Senate leadership and favoring corporate/financial interests over populist/Trump-aligned candidates. The organization functions as McConnell's electoral operation, deploying massive funding toward Senate races where McConnell's preferences diverge from grassroots Republican preferences. One Nation wants to maintain Republican Senate control and prevent populist or Trump-aligned Republicans from displacing traditional Republican moderates.

### Who They Fund

One Nation spends heavily in Senate races, targeting Republican candidates aligned with McConnell's leadership and attacking candidates McConnell opposes (whether Democratic or Republican primary challengers). In recent cycles, One Nation has supported candidates including:
- Rob Portman (Ohio) - Corporate-aligned Republican
- Pat Toomey (Pennsylvania) - Moderate Republican
- Susan Collins (Maine) - Moderate Republican
The organization also spends significantly against populist/Trump-aligned candidates in Republican primaries, particularly where Trump's preferred candidates threaten to displace McConnell-aligned incumbents.

### What They've Gotten

One Nation's massive spending has successfully protected McConnell-aligned Republican senators from primary challenges and general election defeats. The organization spent approximately $85 million in 2020 and $50+ million in 2022 and 2024, making it one of the most powerful political forces in Senate elections. One Nation's dark money status (as a 501c4 rather than a Super PAC) allows it to maintain complete donor secrecy, preventing voters from knowing which wealthy interests fund the organization. The organization's influence has allowed McConnell to maintain control of Senate Republican strategy despite Trump's increasing dominance over the Republican Party base.

### Class Analysis

One Nation exemplifies how dark money enables wealthy donors to exert political power while remaining completely anonymous: the organization spends tens of millions of dollars annually without disclosing a single donor's identity. The 501(c)(4) structure — ostensibly for "social welfare" — is entirely a legal fiction; One Nation operates as a Super PAC but avoids the donor disclosure requirements that Super PACs must follow. The organization's function is to prevent the Democratic process from working as intended: Republican voters prefer Trump and populist candidates, but One Nation's massive, anonymous spending allows billionaire donors to overrule grassroots preference and install their preferred moderate Republicans. One Nation's existence and success reveals that the restriction on campaign spending is not a financial limit but a donor anonymity structure.
