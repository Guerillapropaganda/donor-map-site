### Who They Are

Illinois Future PAC. JB Pritzker's personal political operation — a Super PAC funded almost entirely by the billionaire governor himself. Pritzker has contributed $300+ million of his own fortune to Illinois Future PAC and related committees since 2018, making it one of the largest self-funded political operations in American history. The PAC funds Pritzker's own campaigns, allied legislative candidates, and ballot initiatives (including the 2020 graduated income tax amendment).

The structural function: Illinois Future PAC transforms Pritzker's personal wealth into political infrastructure that bypasses the traditional donor ecosystem. Pritzker doesn't need to court donors — he is the donor. This creates the appearance of political independence while consolidating power in a single billionaire's operation. The PAC's spending dwarfs all other Illinois political committees combined, effectively making Pritzker the state's political party apparatus.

---

### The Self-Funding Paradox

Illinois Future PAC exemplifies the Self-Funding as Independence pattern: Pritzker frames his self-funding as proof of incorruptibility — "I can't be bought because I'm spending my own money." The reality: self-funding doesn't eliminate donor influence, it concentrates it in a single individual. Pritzker's policy agenda aligns with his class interests (Hyatt Hotels, private equity, real estate holdings). The difference between a self-funding billionaire and a donor-funded politician is not the absence of moneyed influence — it's the consolidation of that influence in one person instead of a coalition.

> [!money]
> Illinois Future PAC is the Pritzker family checkbook with a legal designation. $300+ million in self-contributions creates a political operation that no challenger can match — Pritzker outspent all opponents combined in both the 2018 primary, 2018 general, and 2022 general elections. The PAC's dominance means Illinois Democratic politics runs through one billionaire's strategic priorities: the party apparatus, legislative candidates, and ballot initiatives all depend on Pritzker funding. Self-funding doesn't eliminate the donor-policy pipeline — it makes one person the entire pipeline.
