Every politician tells two stories. One to their voters, one to their donors. The gap between those stories is the most important fact about them.

This site is built around that gap.

---

### Why we flag contradictions

A **contradiction** is a case where the stated position and the material position don't match. When a senator runs on breaking up big banks and then votes for a bill written by Goldman Sachs lobbyists, that's a contradiction. When a populist railing against corporate power takes money from the Koch Network, that's a contradiction. When two senators publicly feud while sharing the same donors, that's the Both-Sides Illusion.

We name these patterns. They repeat across parties, across decades, across issues, because they're not glitches. They're the system working as designed.

Flagging contradictions is the part of this project that couldn't be automated if we wanted to. It requires reading the rhetoric, reading the record, and saying "these don't match." That's editorial judgment. We make it. We cite it. We own it.

---

### Why we publish our standards

Most investigative outlets don't tell you how they work. We do.

Our standards define what counts as a verified source, what promotes a profile from draft to published, how we handle broken links, how we cite source IDs, how we flag our own uncertainty.

We publish this because the alternative, "trust me," is not enough. In an era of AI-generated content, unnamed sources, and partisan framing, the only currency that matters is traceable evidence. A claim without a source is worth less than no claim at all.

If we violate our own standards, you can point it out. If a profile doesn't meet them, it doesn't get promoted to the public site. We'd rather say "we don't know yet" than tell you something we can't back up.

### Why we use AI (and why we're telling you)

Every independent investigative outlet operating at this scale is using AI. Most of them don't tell you.

We do. Here's what we use it for:

- **Drafting** — first-pass profile writing from verified source material
- **Aggregating** — pulling together sourced findings across multiple filings
- **Pattern detection** — flagging potential contradictions for human review
- **Verification** — running structured checks against the source-tier rules

Here's what humans do:

- **Editorial judgment** — deciding what matters, what doesn't, what's a pattern vs. noise
- **Contradiction detection** — naming the gap between rhetoric and record
- **Source verification** — checking every URL, every filing ID, every tier assignment
- **Voice** — making sure what gets published is something a person would actually say

This combination is what makes the project possible for one editor. It's also what makes it vulnerable if done wrong. Our standards exist to keep the human layer honest. We'd rather use tools transparently than pretend we built everything by hand.

**AI translates. Humans decide.** No claim on this site is asserted by AI alone. Every factual claim traces to a source record a human can verify.
