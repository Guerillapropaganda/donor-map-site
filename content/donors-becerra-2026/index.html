Becerra for Governor 2026 · the top donors.

Companion to /three-becerras. The full top-donor universe so the reader can audit the healthcare-industry concentration directly. I am not cherry-picking. The list below is the top of a 5,061-donor pool that raised approximately $5.7 million.

The headline numbers. Xavier Becerra's gubernatorial committee, FPPC #1480025, has raised approximately $5.7 million from 5,061 donors as of the most recent semi-annual filing. Average contribution: about $1,135. Top contribution: the $39,200 California legal maximum for an individual donor to a 2026 gubernatorial candidate.

The list below is the verified top fifteen for the 2026 cycle, augmented with the six healthcare-industry max-or-near-max donors profiled on the main investigation (some of which fall below the top fifteen by raw total but are editorially relevant). Class tags identify the donor's industry sector and any HHS-policy hook. The reader can read the full list to audit whether the healthcare-industry concentration on the main investigation is selected or representative. The healthcare-industry concentration is representative.

Industry concentration · top fifteen

Reading the top fifteen by class tag:

  • Healthcare (HHS-regulated): five entries in the top fifteen totaling $207,250 (Molina + Ayala household + AltaMed + Schaeffer + Border Health Federal PAC). The full healthcare slice profiled on the main investigation extends to additional donors below the top fifteen (Cantu personally + Prime Healthcare), bringing the total verified healthcare-industry concentration to roughly $266,450 across seven entities.
  • Construction labor (LIUNA): two entries, $101,700 combined. State infrastructure spending, prevailing-wage enforcement.
  • Tribal gaming: two entries, $86,500 combined. Standard California institutional Democrat donor.
  • Entertainment / Spanish-language media: one entry (Alexandra Seros, married to Walter Ulloa, Entravision CEO), $42,200. Identity / community giving, no HHS hook.
  • Equipment leasing / Hispanic-philanthropy: one entry (Jules Buenabenta, founder of Jules and Associates Inc.; USC presidential commission Hispanic-equity advocacy), $41,900. Verified not healthcare-aligned.
  • Life-sciences IP litigation: one entry (Morgan Chu, Partner & Chair of Litigation Practice at Irell & Manella), $39,200. Pharma / biotech IP work, FDA-adjacent but not directly HHS-regulated.
  • Environmental philanthropy: one entry (Cindy Horn, NRDC board chair 2018-2020, Disney-studios household), $39,200. No HHS hook.
  • Plaintiff bar: one entry (Tigran Martinian), $45,600. Founder of Martinian Lawyers Inc., a Los Angeles personal-injury, wrongful-death, and criminal-defense firm. California State Bar #247638. Cal-Access ZIP+4 (90068-2106) matches the State Bar registered firm address (2801 Cahuenga Blvd W, Los Angeles).

Healthcare is the single largest sector by dollar concentration in the verified top fifteen. Construction labor is second. Together those two sectors account for more than half of the top-fifteen dollars. The healthcare concentration is real, primary-source-verified, and not selection-bias.

What this list does not show

The 5,046 donors below the top fifteen contributed approximately $3.0 million combined, average about $590. That distribution is consistent with a campaign that has both an institutional max-donor base (the rows above) and a small-dollar long tail (the donors below). The page does not detail the long tail because each donor below the max-donor tier contributes a small share of the total. Readers who want the long tail can pull the full RCPT_CD records from the Cal-Access bulk export.
