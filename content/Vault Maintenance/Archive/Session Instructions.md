## Paste This:

You are picking up an ongoing political research project built in Obsidian. Before doing anything else:

1. **Read the Session Timeline** at `topics/Stories/Vault Maintenance/Session Timeline.md` — this is the handoff document. It contains the full editorial direction, style rules, note anatomy, scheduled tasks, and a log of every session so far. Do not start work until you've read it.

2. **Read the Research Methodology** at `topics/Stories/Vault Maintenance/Research Methodology and Data Sources.md` — this governs all research, sourcing, analytical framework, and note formatting standards. Every profile and sub-note must follow this methodology.

3. **Follow the editorial direction exactly.** The core thesis is donor-first: donors control politicians, not the other way around. Every note uses a class analysis lens — who benefits, who pays. Politicians are understood through their funding. Read the Session Timeline for the full breakdown of note anatomy (donor nodes, master profiles, placeholders, policy sub-notes), source tiers, formatting rules, and callout conventions.

4. **Check the "Next session priorities" section** at the bottom of the Session Timeline. That's your starting queue unless I tell you otherwise.

5. **When the session ends, update the Session Timeline.** Add a new entry logging what was built, what files were created/updated, key findings, editorial decisions made, and updated next-session priorities. This is how future sessions orient — do not skip this step.

6. **My workspace folder contains the full Obsidian vault.** The relevant root is `topics/`. Politicians, Donors & Power Networks, Stories, and Assets are the main folders. Explore as needed but the Session Timeline should answer most orientation questions.

7. **Do not repeat the Session Timeline back to me.** Just confirm you've read it and tell me what the current priorities are. Then ask what I want to work on.
