### Think Tank Policy Kill Map — Deep Research Prompts

**Purpose:** 5 targeted deep research queries to document how donor money flows through think tanks to produce intellectual ammunition that blocks structural economic reform. Each query targets a specific think tank and asks for the policies donors pay them to fight against, the research they produced, and the legislative/regulatory outcomes.

**Analytical framework:** For each think tank, we need: (1) specific policies opposed, (2) specific research/reports produced against those policies, (3) who funded that research, (4) what was the policy outcome, (5) timeline showing donation → research → policy kill sequence.

**Target policies across all queries:** Medicare for All / single payer, public banking / postal banking, nationalization of utilities or energy, Green New Deal / climate regulation / carbon pricing, wealth taxes / financial transaction taxes, union organizing / PRO Act / labor protections, antitrust enforcement / corporate breakups, pharmaceutical price controls / drug importation, Social Security expansion (vs. privatization), minimum wage increases, student debt cancellation, public housing / rent control.

### Prompt 1: Mercatus Center — Koch Deregulation Pipeline

```
Comprehensive investigation: What specific government regulations and progressive policies has the Mercatus Center at George Mason University produced research against, and what were the policy outcomes?

I need documented evidence for each of these areas:

1. LABOR DEREGULATION: What specific Mercatus research has argued against minimum wage increases, union organizing rights (PRO Act, card check), OSHA workplace safety regulations, overtime protections, or gig worker classification rules? Which Koch Industries facilities or Koch network companies directly benefit from these positions?

2. HEALTHCARE DEREGULATION: What specific Mercatus research has argued against Medicare for All, ACA expansion, pharmaceutical price controls, certificate-of-need laws, or single-payer proposals? Include the 2018 Charles Blahous study that estimated Medicare for All costs — what was the funding source and how was the study used politically?

3. ENVIRONMENTAL/CLIMATE DEREGULATION: What specific Mercatus research has argued against EPA regulations (Clean Power Plan, methane rules, CAFE standards, chemical safety rules)? Which Koch Industries operations (Flint Hills Resources refineries, Koch Pipeline, Georgia-Pacific, Koch Minerals) directly benefit from weakened environmental enforcement?

4. FINANCIAL DEREGULATION: What specific Mercatus research supported rolling back Dodd-Frank provisions, weakening CFPB authority, or opposing financial transaction taxes?

5. DOGE PARTNERSHIP (2025-2026): What specific Mercatus Center resources, personnel, or research are being used by the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE)? What is mercatus.org/doge? Which specific deregulatory recommendations flow from Mercatus to DOGE? Has Veronique de Rugy or any other Mercatus scholar criticized DOGE's approach?

6. REGULATORY BUDGET CONCEPT: Trace the full pipeline from Mercatus's RegData project to Executive Order 13771 ("10-for-1" deregulation) — who were the key Mercatus personnel involved, what specific regulations were eliminated under this framework, and what was the estimated cost savings to regulated industries?

7. CHARLES KOCH FOUNDATION FUNDING: What is the current (2020-2026) level of Koch foundation funding to Mercatus? Has the academic capture arrangement (Koch representatives on faculty selection committees) continued after the 2018 FOIA revelations? What other major donors fund Mercatus besides Koch?

For each area, provide: specific report titles and dates, author names, funding sources where documented, and the specific policy outcome (regulation blocked, weakened, or eliminated). Cite FEC records, IRS 990 filings, FOIA documents, congressional testimony, and major investigative journalism. All claims must be sourced to identifiable primary or secondary sources.
```

### Prompt 2: Cato Institute — Libertarian Policy Opposition and Hidden Donors

```
Comprehensive investigation: What specific progressive policies has the Cato Institute produced research against, who funded that research, and what were the policy outcomes? Special focus on Cato's post-2012 donor opacity.

I need documented evidence for each area:

1. SOCIAL SECURITY PRIVATIZATION: Trace Cato's 30+ year campaign to privatize Social Security. Who were the Wall Street firms (Citicorp, Chase, Salomon Brothers, Bank of America documented as early co-financiers) that funded Cato's Project on Social Security Choice/Privatization? What was the total funding? How did Cato's research specifically influence the Bush 2005 privatization push? What specific legislative proposals did Cato draft or support? Why did the privatization effort fail and what is Cato's current position?

2. HEALTHCARE: What specific Cato research has argued against Medicare for All, single-payer healthcare, ACA/Obamacare, Medicaid expansion, pharmaceutical price controls, or public option proposals? Who are the healthcare industry donors to Cato (pharmaceutical companies, insurance companies, hospital chains)? Michael Cannon's role specifically — what has he published and what policy impact has it had?

3. CLIMATE AND ENERGY: Document Cato's history with climate denial research, particularly the Patrick Michaels era. What specific EPA regulations, carbon pricing proposals, or Green New Deal elements has Cato produced research against? How much ExxonMobil, Koch, or other fossil fuel funding has Cato received historically and currently? What happened after Cato reportedly stopped climate denial work — have they continued opposing climate regulation through different framing?

4. LABOR AND UNIONS: What specific Cato research opposes union organizing rights, minimum wage increases, PRO Act, prevailing wage laws, or occupational licensing reform? What business interests fund this research?

5. FINANCIAL REGULATION: What specific Cato research opposes wealth taxes, financial transaction taxes, capital gains tax increases, Dodd-Frank, CFPB, or antitrust enforcement against tech companies?

6. DONOR TRANSPARENCY (CRITICAL): Cato scores 0/5 on transparency and publishes no donor list. What is known about Cato's current (2020-2026) funding sources? After the 2012 Koch takeover attempt and subsequent settlement, how did Cato's donor base change? What do IRS 990 filings reveal about revenue sources, largest grants, and donor concentration? Has any investigative reporting, FOIA request, or leaked document revealed current Cato donors? What is the current Koch network relationship (Charles Koch Foundation, DonorsTrust, Stand Together)?

7. KOCH-CATO-TRUMP FRACTURE: Document the May 2025 fracture where Cato president Peter Goettler criticized Trump on tariffs and due process. Has this affected Koch/Stand Together funding? What is the current relationship?

For each area: specific report titles and dates, author names, funding sources, policy outcomes. Cite IRS 990 filings, congressional testimony, investigative journalism, and any available donor records.

5. SOCIAL SECURITY AND ENTITLEMENTS: Andrew Biggs and other AEI scholars have produced research on Social Security reform/privatization. What specific proposals has AEI advocated? Who funds this research? How does it connect to Wall Street interests in managing privatized retirement accounts?

6. DARK MONEY PIPELINE: DonorsTrust provided $86.7M to AEI (2003-2010) — the LARGEST documented dark money transfer to any single think tank. For each area: specific report titles, author names, funding documentation, legislative/regulatory outcomes. Cite IRS 990 filings, FOIA documents, congressional testimony, OpenSecrets data, investigative journalism.

### Prompt 4: Brookings Institution — Wall Street's Intellectual Cover on the Democratic Side

```
Comprehensive investigation: What specific progressive economic policies has the Brookings Institution — particularly its Hamilton Project — produced research against or helped moderate/weaken, and how does this connect to Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan Chase, and other Wall Street funding?

I need documented evidence for each area:

1. HAMILTON PROJECT AND WALL STREET: The Hamilton Project was co-founded by Robert Rubin (Goldman Sachs CEO → Clinton Treasury Secretary) in 2006. What specific Hamilton Project research has argued against financial regulation expansion, Glass-Steagall restoration, breaking up big banks, financial transaction taxes, or wealth taxes? What specific Hamilton Project research has supported positions favorable to Wall Street (capital gains tax treatment, carried interest preservation, derivatives market structure)? Who are the current Hamilton Project funders and advisory council members?

2. HEALTHCARE: Has Brookings produced research opposing Medicare for All, single-payer, or public option proposals? What specific Brookings research supported the ACA's market-based approach over single-payer alternatives? Who funded this research? What is the connection between Brookings healthcare funders (pharmaceutical companies, insurance companies, hospital systems) and Brookings healthcare policy positions?

3. HOUSING AND REAL ESTATE: Has Brookings produced research opposing rent control, public housing expansion, or institutional landlord regulation? What specific Brookings research has supported market-based housing solutions that benefit real estate industry donors? JPMorgan Chase gave $10M for the Global Cities Initiative — what policy positions came out of that program?

4. LABOR AND WAGES: Has Brookings produced research opposing or moderating minimum wage proposals, union organizing rights, or wealth redistribution? What specific Brookings research has argued for "market-based" solutions to inequality rather than structural reforms (wealth taxes, union power, corporate governance changes)?

5. TRADE AND GLOBALIZATION: What specific Brookings research supported NAFTA, TPP, or other free trade agreements opposed by labor unions? How does this connect to Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, and other financial sector donors who benefit from trade liberalization?

6. FOREIGN GOVERNMENT FUNDING AND POLICY: Qatar gave $14.8M (2011-2013) for the Brookings Doha Center. What specific Brookings research on Middle East policy, energy policy, or Israel-Palestine tracked with Qatar's interests? What other foreign governments fund Brookings and what policy positions correlate?

7. CORPORATE DONOR GOVERNANCE: Goldman Sachs ($2.5M+ from John Thornton), JPMorgan Chase ($10M+), Microsoft, Google, and other tech firms are major Brookings donors. What governance mechanisms exist? Do corporate donors have any documented influence on research agendas or personnel decisions? Has any Brookings scholar reported pressure to avoid topics that would harm major donors?

8. BROOKINGS vs. PROGRESSIVE ALTERNATIVES: When progressive think tanks (EPI, Roosevelt Institute, Center on Budget) propose wealth taxes, corporate breakups, or public investment, does Brookings produce competing "moderate" alternatives that preserve existing power structures? Document specific cases where Brookings research was positioned as the "responsible" centrist alternative to progressive proposals.

For each area: specific report titles and dates, author names, funding documentation, policy outcomes. Cite IRS 990 filings, ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer, investigative journalism (NYT 2014 foreign influence investigation), congressional testimony.
