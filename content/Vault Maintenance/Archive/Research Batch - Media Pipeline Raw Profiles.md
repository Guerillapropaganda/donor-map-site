## BATCH 3: Left Media — Independent Outlets & Their Funding Models
*These are outlets that claim independence from corporate/billionaire funding. The question is whether that's true and what capture dynamics exist.*

```
Deep background on these left-leaning media outlets and their funding structures. For EACH one, I need:

1. FOUNDING STORY: Who started it, when, with what money?
2. FUNDING MODEL: Subscriptions, donations, foundation grants, institutional backing? Break down revenue sources.
3. INSTITUTIONAL FUNDERS: Any foundation grants, major donors, or institutional money? Name them.
4. KEY PERSONNEL: Founders, editors, prominent writers — and where they came from.
5. EDITORIAL INDEPENDENCE: Any documented cases where funding influenced coverage? Any topics they avoid?
6. FINANCIAL SUSTAINABILITY: Are they profitable? Growing? Struggling?

Subjects:

A) DROP SITE NEWS — Founded by Ryan Grim and Jeremy Scahill after leaving The Intercept. Focus on: launch funding (self-funded or investor-backed?), subscriber numbers if reported, how it differs editorially from The Intercept, whether any institutional funders backed the launch, comparison to Omidyar-funded Intercept model.

B) JACOBIN — Socialist magazine. Focus on: Bhaskar Sunkara's ownership, revenue model, foundation grants if any, relationship to DSA, Catalyst journal, book publishing arm (Verso connection?), any corporate or institutional funding despite socialist positioning.

C) THE GRAYZONE — Max Blumenthal's outlet. Focus on: funding sources (this is heavily debated), allegations of Russian/Syrian government ties, revenue model, institutional connections if any, editorial line on foreign policy and whether it maps to any state interests.

D) NOVARA MEDIA — UK left media. Focus on: funding model (Patreon/subscriptions?), any institutional backers, key personnel, editorial line, audience size, any documented funding controversies.

E) CROOKED MEDIA — Pod Save America parent company. Focus on: founding by Obama staffers, venture capital funding (who invested?), revenue from advertising and live tours, corporate sponsors, relationship to Democratic Party operations, whether "independent progressive media" framing is accurate given founder backgrounds and corporate ad revenue.

F) HEATHER COX RICHARDSON — Substack historian. Focus on: subscriber numbers and revenue (she's reportedly one of Substack's top earners), funding model (pure subscriptions?), any institutional ties, speaking fees, political connections, how her historical framing serves specific political narratives.

G) RYAN GRIM — Focus on: career arc (HuffPost → Intercept → Drop Site), any political operative connections, FEC contributions if any, relationship to progressive political organizations.

H) JEREMY SCAHILL — Focus on: career arc (Democracy Now → Nation → Intercept → Drop Site), Dirty Wars documentary funding, any institutional ties, book deal economics, Blackwater reporting and its political impact.

I) MAX BLUMENTHAL — Focus on: The Grayzone founding and funding, father's (Sidney Blumenthal) political connections, foreign travel and reporting that generated controversy, specific allegations about funding sources with evidence for/against.
```
