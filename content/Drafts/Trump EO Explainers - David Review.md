## 1. Ensuring Citizenship Verification and Integrity in Federal Elections
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Draft explainer:** *Adds new citizenship documentation requirements to federal voter rolls. Fraud-prevention framing; in practice, naturalized citizens and low-income voters face the heaviest new paperwork burden.*

## 2. Addressing DEI Discrimination by Federal Contractors
**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Draft explainer:** *Kills DEI hiring and promotion requirements for federal contractors. Contractors are no longer required to demonstrate efforts to hire or promote non-white, non-male, or disabled workers.*

## 3. Further Continuance of the Federal Emergency Management Agency Review Council
**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Draft explainer:** *Extends a Trump-appointed council reviewing FEMA. The direction of travel is to shrink federal disaster response and push more cost onto states.*

## 4. Preserving America's Game
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Draft explainer:** *Restricts transgender athletes from women's sports categories in federally-funded programs. Culture-war signal; administrative burden lands on schools and leagues.*

## 5. Establishing the Task Force To Eliminate Fraud
**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Draft explainer:** *Creates a federal task force targeting "fraud" in benefit programs (Medicare, SNAP, unemployment). Similar past enforcement pushes have reduced benefits for working-class recipients more than they've caught fraud.*

## 6. Removing Regulatory Barriers to Affordable Home Construction
**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Draft explainer:** *Strips environmental review (NEPA), labor standards, and fair-housing compliance from federally-supported housing projects. Developers win. Union wage protections and environmental review lose.*

## 7. Promoting Access to Mortgage Credit
**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Draft explainer:** *Loosens mortgage underwriting standards and expands federal mortgage backing. Banks and private-equity housing funds benefit. The loosened standards mirror what preceded the 2008 crash.*

## 8. Ensuring Truthful Advertising of Products Claiming To Be Made in America
**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Draft explainer:** *Tightens enforcement of "Made in USA" labels. Populist framing, narrow practical impact — most Trump-aligned large donors are already compliant or exempt.*

## 9. Adjusting Certain Delegations Under the Defense Production Act
**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Draft explainer:** *Expands presidential authority to direct private industry to produce defense-related goods, bypassing normal competitive bidding. Benefits flow to politically-selected defense contractors.*

## 10. Combating Cybercrime, Fraud, and Predatory Schemes Against American Citizens
**Date:** 2026-03-11
**Draft explainer:** *Creates a DOJ+FBI task force on online fraud. Popular framing; actual enforcement uncertain given parallel cuts to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.*

## 11. Ending Certain Tariff Actions
**Date:** 2026-02-25
**Draft explainer:** *Removes specific tariffs (mostly on allied nations) while maintaining others. Importers with Trump administration access benefit; domestic producers previously sheltered by the tariff lose.*

## 12. Continuing the Suspension of Duty-Free De Minimis Treatment for All Countries
**Date:** 2026-02-25
**Draft explainer:** *Extends the ban on duty-free shipments under $800 (targeting Shein, Temu, low-cost Chinese imports). Domestic retailers win. Working-class consumers who buy cheap goods online pay more.*

## 13. Promoting the National Defense by Ensuring an Adequate Supply of Elemental Phosphorus and Glyphosate-Based Herbicides
**Date:** 2026-02-23
**Draft explainer:** *Federally subsidizes Monsanto/Bayer-line herbicides under a "national security" framing. Direct agribusiness donor payoff dressed up as defense policy.*

## 14. Strengthening United States National Defense With America's Beautiful Clean Coal Power Generation Fleet
**Date:** 2026-02-17
**Draft explainer:** *Coal industry subsidies framed as defense. "Clean coal" is industry marketing, not a technical category. Climate policy rollback disguised as national security.*

## 15. Protecting the National Security and Welfare of the United States and Its Citizens From Criminal Actors and Other Public Safety Threats
**Date:** 2026-02-11
**Draft explainer:** *Expands immigration enforcement powers and detention funding. Private-prison donors (CoreCivic, GEO Group) are the primary financial beneficiaries.*

## 16. Modifying Duties To Address Threats to the United States by the Government of the Russian Federation
**Date:** 2026-02-11
**Draft explainer:** *New Russia-related tariff adjustments. Specifics lean on energy markets — the net effect benefits US fossil fuel producers.*

## 17. Establishing an America First Arms Transfer Strategy
**Date:** 2026-02-11
**Draft explainer:** *Loosens arms-export restrictions. Lockheed, Raytheon, Northrop, General Dynamics all benefit. Expands US weapons flows to Saudi Arabia, Israel, the Philippines, and others.*

## 18. Addressing Threats to the United States by the Government of Iran
**Date:** 2026-02-11
**Draft explainer:** *Intensifies Iran sanctions and military posture. The Adelson family and defense contractor donors benefit. Oil prices tick up.*

## 19. Celebrating American Greatness With American Motor Racing
**Date:** 2026-02-04
**Draft explainer:** *Ceremonial. Minor federal support for motor racing events. Culture-war branding; no substantive economic impact.*

## 20. Addressing Threats to the United States by the Government of Cuba
**Date:** 2026-02-03
**Draft explainer:** *Expands Cuba sanctions. Cuban-American Florida donors benefit politically. Cuban families receiving remittances take the pain.*

## 21. Addressing Addiction Through the Great American Recovery Initiative
**Date:** 2026-02-03
**Draft explainer:** *Federal addiction program. Treatment-over-enforcement framing. Funding level uncertain. Pharmaceutical companies producing detox and maintenance drugs indirectly benefit.*

## 22. Continuance of the Federal Emergency Management Agency Review Council
**Date:** 2026-01-29
**Draft explainer:** *Original establishment of the FEMA review council (EO #3 above extends it). Signals intent to restructure federal disaster response away from federal coordination toward state-level.*

## 23. Addressing State and Local Failures To Rebuild Los Angeles After Wildfire Disasters
**Date:** 2026-01-29
**Draft explainer:** *Federalizes response to California wildfires, overriding local and state permitting. Preferred developers benefit from fast-track reconstruction contracts. Targeted political attack on Newsom.*

## 24. Stopping Wall Street From Competing With Main Street Homebuyers
**Date:** 2026-01-23
**Draft explainer:** *Restricts institutional investors from buying single-family homes. Populist framing. Actual impact is narrow — many workarounds remain open for large funds.*

## 25. Designating the Board of Peace as a Public International Organization Entitled To Enjoy Certain Privileges, Exemptions, and Immunities
**Date:** 2026-01-22
**Draft explainer:** *Creates a "Board of Peace" with international-organization privileges. Specifics minimal at time of signing. Appears primarily symbolic / branding.*
