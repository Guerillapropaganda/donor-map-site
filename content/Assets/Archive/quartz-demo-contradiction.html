Goldman Sachs Funds Both Sides of Financial Regulation

Contradiction Deep Dive #01 — When the same donor funds opposing politicians, the conflict is theater.
STORY READY CONTRADICTION
#goldman-sachs #both-sides #wall-street #revolving-door #class-analysis

The Performed Opposition

The vault's core thesis: when two politicians funded by the same donor stage public conflict over that donor's core interests, the conflict is theater — a mechanism to maintain plausible deniability while guaranteeing the donor wins either way.

Goldman Sachs funds both sides of financial regulation fights. This section documents the specific quotes and floor moments where politicians funded by Goldman perform opposition to each other on issues Goldman shaped.

Trump's "Drain the Swamp" vs. Goldman Recruitment

The Campaign
Donald Trump
Attacked Clinton in 2016 for Goldman ties. Signature pledge: "drain the swamp." Positioned as anti-Wall Street crusade.
The Reality
The Goldman Administration
Appointed 4 Goldman alums: Cohn (NEC), Mnuchin (Treasury), Powell (NSA), Bannon (Strategist). Signed tax bill they wrote.

"Goldman Sachs has total control over Clinton... I want to make our government honest again."

— Trump, 2016 campaign speech
// The Counter

Trump signed the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (Dec 2017), authored largely by Cohn and Mnuchin. The top six banks, including Goldman, saved $32 billion in taxes in two years while cutting jobs and increasing shareholder payouts. Goldman's estimated share: $4–6 billion.
