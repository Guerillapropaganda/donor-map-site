### What Was Found and Added

### CCPOA Contributions to [[_Gavin Newsom Master Profile|Gavin Newsom]]

**Total since he became governor: $2.9 million** — representing 31% of all CCPOA political spending since 2001. [CalMatters, July 2024]

Breakdown by cycle:
- **2018 gubernatorial campaign:** ~$1.5 million — this figure was already in the vault; widely reported but the specific FPPC source was not directly accessed this session
- **2021 anti-recall campaign:** $1.75 million — the **single largest contribution** to the anti-recall effort from any California state employee union [CalMatters / LAist, July 2024]
- **2024 Proposition 1 (mental health/housing bond):** $1 million [CalMatters, July 2024]

These figures were not broken out in the vault previously. The note previously said "Ongoing contributions throughout his tenure" with no dollar amounts for 2021 or 2024. Now updated.

### Contract Terms Received in Exchange

### 2023 CCPOA Contract — $1 billion+ over three years:
- 3% raises in 2023 and 2024
- $10,000 retention bonuses at hard-to-fill facilities (Salinas Valley, CSP-Sacramento, R.J. Donovan)
- Night/weekend premium pay raised from $1.50 to $2.50/hour
- Bilingual pay raised from $100 to $200/month
[CalMatters / CapRadio, August 2023]

### 2025 CCPOA Contract — $600 million over four years:
- Two 3% raises (immediate and July 2027)
- Personal leave program (furloughs) worth ~$132M in short-term savings
- Net first-year state savings of only ~$3.9M
- Additional retention incentives at specific prisons
- First union to reach a deal after Newsom's budget deficit announcement
[CalMatters, June 2025]

### Per-Prisoner Cost

**$132,860 per inmate annually** as of early 2024 — a record high, up 161% since 2010-11.
- Cost drivers: +$28K/prisoner in security staff compensation; +$27.4K/prisoner in healthcare
- California ranks among highest-cost states in the nation
[CalMatters, January 2024]

The vault note previously said "approximately $15 billion annually" for the CDCR budget. The LAO 2025-26 figure is ~$13.9 billion (General Fund). The discrepancy likely reflects total vs. General Fund accounting or older data. Updated the vault to use the LAO figure with attribution.

### Overtime Data

**~$491 million in CDCR overtime in 2019** — the most recent systemwide annual total located.
- Paid to adult and juvenile justice system employees
- Represents nearly as much as total base salaries in that year
[CalMatters, 2020; original data: California State Controller]

**2014 individual data (illustrative):** Top earner Lt. Jeffrey Nixon — $162,048 overtime on $105,204 base. 8,000+ guards earned $100K+ total wages.
[Prison Legal News, December 2016; original: Transparent California]

**2022–2024 totals:** Not confirmed this session. Both publicpay.ca.gov (State Controller database) and transparentcalifornia.com were blocked. These figures are likely higher than $491M due to prison closures reducing headcount while mandatory staffing ratios force remaining guards into extensive overtime.
