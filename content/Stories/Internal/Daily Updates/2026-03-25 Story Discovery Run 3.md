### [GOLD] Fairshake's $193M CLARITY Act Pipeline. Crypto Industry Writes Its Own Regulatory Framework Through Senate Committee Donors

Documented FEC data showing $265,500 in direct contributions from crypto-affiliated individuals to the 7 senators who control the CLARITY Act, alongside $48M from Ripple Labs alone to the industry's primary PAC. The vault tracks Fairshake but hasn't mapped the specific Senate committee donation-to-legislation pipeline or the stablecoin yield fight.

The Digital Asset Market Clarity Act of 2025 passed the House on July 17, 2025 with a 294-134 bipartisan vote. It is now stalled in the Senate, where seven of the forty-six senators on the two committees with direct control over the bill received $265,500 in crypto-affiliated contributions during the 2025-2026 cycle. ### [SILVER] The People's Pledge as Counter-Architecture. Lander-Goldman NY-10 Race Tests Anti-Super PAC Framework

**Rating: Silver**. A significant structural development in the anti-dark-money movement, but no money has been spent yet. Worth documenting because it represents an emerging counter-strategy to the AIPAC/Fairshake model the vault tracks extensively.

Brad Lander, the former New York City comptroller challenging incumbent Rep. Dan Goldman in NY-10, has challenged Goldman to sign the People's Pledge, a campaign finance agreement where candidates agree to donate campaign funds to charity equal to 50% of PAC spending or 75% of dark-money super PAC spending against them. The pledge was developed by Larry Cohen, former president of the Communications Workers of America and longtime DNC official.

**Why this matters for the vault:** The People's Pledge is the only tested structural mechanism for blunting super PAC influence in individual races. It has been around for over a decade but has never been deployed against AIPAC-scale spending. If Goldman signs, it functionally neutralizes AIPAC's United Democracy Project in NY-10. If he refuses, it becomes a public record of which candidates benefit from and protect the super PAC architecture.

**Context:** The American Prospect published a major analysis on March 25, 2026, today, arguing that the People's Pledge is the most viable near-term mechanism for addressing the super PAC problem. This connects directly to the vault's tracking of AIPAC's Illinois shell PAC architecture (Story Discovery Run 1) and Fairshake's New York targeting.

**Analytical patterns:** **Dark Money Symmetry** (Goldman, a Democrat, benefits from the same super PAC architecture Democrats claim to oppose), **Two-Audience Problem** (Goldman must appeal to progressive voters while relying on AIPAC/donor-class support).
