### GOLD — Tariff Exemptions as Donor Payback: Politically Connected Firms Get Carveouts While Small Business Pays

**Classification:** Gold
**Status:** NOT in vault. No existing tariff-donor connection analysis found.

### The story:
ProPublica investigation reveals that politically connected firms are disproportionately receiving tariff exemptions from the Trump administration, while the exemption process itself has become opaque — with no formal application process in the second term. A Lehigh University study of Trump's first-term tariff exemptions quantified the pattern: a one standard deviation increase in Republican campaign contributions raised the probability of receiving a tariff exemption by 3.94 percentage points, while the same increase in Democratic contributions *decreased* approval odds by 3.4 percentage points. Public Citizen's analysis found that pharmaceutical companies (PhRMA spent $387M on lobbying in 2024 alone), semiconductor manufacturers, and energy companies all received blanket exemptions in the February 2026 proclamation — exemptions that mirror the industries' lobbying and donation profiles.

Specific example: Reyes Holdings, a Coca-Cola bottler owned by brothers who donated millions to Republican causes, hired a lobbying firm with direct White House ties to secure tariff relief. The February 20, 2026 Proclamation exempted agricultural products, copper, pharmaceuticals, semiconductors, lumber, critical minerals, and energy products — over 90% of which mirror exemptions from previous rounds, suggesting an entrenched carve-out system for politically connected industries.

Meanwhile, small businesses without lobbyist access face the full tariff burden. The structural function is a two-tier trade policy: tariffs for the unconnected, exemptions for the donor class.

**Why Gold:** Peer-reviewed academic study quantifying the donation-to-exemption pipeline (Lehigh), investigative journalism documenting specific cases (ProPublica), and the $387M pharma lobbying figure provides industry-level receipts. The mechanism is systematic: donate → lobby → get exempted from the policy your donations helped enact. Multiple analytical patterns: **Donor-Class Override** (tariffs hit everyone except donor-connected firms), **Two-Audience Problem** (Trump campaigns on "tariffs for all" while exempting donors).
