### The story:
The Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2026, signed February 3, 2026, included the PBM Reform Act of 2025 (HR 4317) provisions. The law restructures pharmacy benefit manager compensation in Medicare Part D, delinking PBM fees from drug prices, requiring flat-dollar service fees (effective 2028), mandating any-willing-pharmacy networks (effective 2029), and requiring transparency reporting to employer plans.

What it does not touch: pharmaceutical manufacturers' list prices, rebate structures, or underlying pricing power.
