### Key Findings

### 1. AIPAC Illinois Shell PAC Funding Revealed (FEC Filings. March 21)
March 21 FEC filings confirmed that AIPAC's [[United Democracy Project - UDP|United Democracy Project]] (UDP) contributed **$5.3 million** of the **$14.1 million** raised by two anonymous shell groups. Affordable Chicago Now and Elect Chicago Women, that spent over $14M to influence Illinois Democratic primaries on March 17. This is the clearest documentation yet of the UDP → shell PAC → candidate pipeline.

AIPAC-backed candidates went **2-for-4** in Chicago-area House races: [[_Donna Miller Master Profile|Donna Miller]] (IL-2) and [[_Melissa Bean Master Profile|Melissa Bean]] (IL-8) won; candidates in IL-4 and IL-9 lost. Total AIPAC spending across Illinois: ~$22M. Combined with crypto (Fairshake/Protect Progress $8.6M) and AI (Think Big $2.5M+), total outside spending exceeded $34M in Illinois alone.

AIPAC's cumulative 2025-2026 cycle direct contributions now total **$28 million** to congressional campaigns, more than 3x the next-largest PAC. War chest: $96M.

*Updated:* [[AIPAC - American Israel Public Affairs Committee]]

### 2. [[MAGA Inc|MAGA Inc]] War Chest and New Mega-Donors
MAGA Inc holds **$294-304 million** cash on hand for 2026 midterms. Trump-aligned PACs collectively hold **$400M+**, unmatched by Democrats. New mega-donors with zero prior political history include:

- Foris Dax (Crypto.com parent): $30M, crypto regulatory interest
- Greg Brockman (OpenAI co-founder): $25M. AI deregulation
- Energy Transfer + CEO [[Kelcy Warren|Kelcy Warren]]: $25M combined, oil/gas deregulation
- [[Stephen Schwarzman|Stephen Schwarzman]] (Blackstone): $5M, private equity
- Juul Labs: $1M, vaping regulation
- 96% of all MAGA Inc money comes from $1M+ donors

*Updated:* [[MAGA Inc]]

### 3. [[Leonard Leo|Leonard Leo]]'s Lexington Fund → First Principles PAC Pipeline
The Lexington Fund poured $2M into First Principles PAC (Tennessee-registered entities led by former Leo deputy). First Principles Wisconsin spent $600K+ on the Wisconsin Supreme Court race, the most expensive judicial election in US history. Despite this spending (plus [[Elon Musk|Elon Musk]]'s $20M), the Democratic-endorsed Susan Crawford won decisively. The Judicial Crisis Network Fund now operates as a trade name within the Lexington Fund, consolidating Leo's dark money infrastructure.

*Updated:* [[Leonard Leo]]

### 4. Fairshake Targeting New York House Races
Fairshake confirmed targeting NY congressional races in 2026. Key targets: Democratic Reps. Laura Gillen, Dan Goldman, and Ritchie Torres, all broke with party to vote for the CLARITY Act (Digital Asset Market Clarity Act). All three face progressive primary challenges. Fairshake spent $5.3M on 6 NY races in 2024 (all wins). War chest: $191M+.

*Updated:* [[Fairshake PAC]]

### 5. Record Super PAC Spending Milestone
Overall outside spending has surpassed **$225 million**, the most ever at this point in any election cycle, and $75M+ more than the record pace set in 2024. The WaPo/PoliticalWire reporting highlighted that some groups deliberately hide their cause through neutral-sounding names (e.g. "Affordable Chicago Now" for AIPAC, "Protect Progress" for crypto).

### 6. NAR (National Association of Realtors) PAC. Texas Focus
NAR PAC spending reached ~$10M ahead of 2026, with Texas receiving the most funding. NAR-backed $216K in digital advertising for John Cornyn (R-TX). Smaller targeted spending across Texas House races including Tony Gonzales (R), Dan Crenshaw (R), Julie Johnson (D), and [[_Henry Cuellar Master Profile|Henry Cuellar]] (D). Not significant enough to warrant vault note updates but confirms NAR as a bipartisan real estate spending vehicle.

### 7. California Governor Race. Fundraising Update
Republican Steve Hilton leads overall fundraising (CalMatters). [[_Tom Steyer Master Profile|Tom Steyer]] has self-funded $28M+ (Mercury News/SF Examiner), outspending the field 2:1 but polling at ~8%. [[_Katie Porter Master Profile|Katie Porter]] raised $6.1M from ~12,000 mostly small-dollar donors. Vault notes already current on these figures.

### 8. FPPC New Portal
California's Fair Political Practices Commission launched a new Public Official Financial Disclosure Portal (March 12), streamlined Form 700 (Statements of Economic Interests) access. Relevant for future CA governor race donor research.

### 9. Texas Senate Democratic Primary. Sixteen Thirty Fund Dark Money Trail
The March 3 Texas Democratic Senate primary produced a fully documented Arabella/Sixteen Thirty Fund dark money cascade: **Sixteen Thirty Fund → Government that Works PAC ($4M) → Lone Star Rising PAC ($3.75M of $6.14M total)** → advertising supporting state Rep. James Talarico over Rep. Jasmine Crockett. Talarico won 53.1%–45.6%.

Separately identifiable contribution within the same race: **[[Reid Hoffman|Reid Hoffman]] donated $500,000** to Lone Star Rising PAC. This is FEC-disclosed and sits alongside the anonymous Sixteen Thirty money, the named-donor + anonymous-money hybrid that characterizes elite Democratic spending.

On the Republican side: Cornyn and Paxton advance to a May 26 runoff. Cornyn has $14M+ cash vs. Paxton's $4M. Cornyn's super PAC received $2.9M from Houston businessman John Nau. Pro-Paxton dark money top donor: Douglas Scharbauer ($250K, Midland oil).

*Updated:* [[Sixteen Thirty Fund]], [[Reid Hoffman]]

### 10. Arabella Advisors → Sunflower Services Rebranding + Chorus Successor
The House Oversight Committee (Comer, R-KY) continued its investigation into the Sixteen Thirty Fund's "Chorus" influencer program, which paid social media creators to promote Democratic messaging without required political ad disclaimers. Following the inquiry, Sixteen Thirty severed ties with Chorus. A new nonprofit **Creator Collective** was filed November 21, 2025, appears to be the operational successor. Arabella Advisors itself rebranded as **Sunflower Services** before the midterm cycle.

*Updated:* [[Arabella Advisors]]

### 11. FEC March Monthly Reports Due March 20
March Monthly FEC reports were due March 20, 2026 for PACs on monthly filing schedules. Key items flagged from the new filings cycle. Illinois shell PAC data (covered in Finding #1 above) and Texas dark money cascade (Finding #9) both emerged from this reporting deadline. Q1 quarterly reports (January–March 2026) are due April 15, 2026, expect the next major disclosure window then.
