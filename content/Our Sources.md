Every claim on this site traces back to a record. No "sources say." No "according to reports." No unnamed officials. If we say Koch Industries donated $2.3M to a politician, you can click the FEC filing that proves it.

This is the tier system that tells you how strong the evidence is.

---

### Tier 1 — The government record

The original paperwork. FEC filings. State campaign finance databases. Congressional voting records. IRS 990s. Court documents. USASpending.gov contracts. STOCK Act financial disclosures.

Tier 1 is the strongest evidence we can cite. It's the primary document the politician or donor filed themselves. If they lied on it, that's a crime. If we misread it, you can point to the original and correct us.

Most profile claims on this site resolve to Tier 1 data.

---

### Tier 2 — Investigative journalism with editorial standards

ProPublica, The Intercept, The Washington Post investigations desk, CREW, Brennan Center, Yale Climate Connections, academic research with peer review.

Tier 2 is strong secondary evidence. A named reporter put their byline on it. An editor reviewed it. A publication stands behind it. When the original records are locked behind paywalls, sealed, or not yet digitized, Tier 2 is often the best available path to the claim.

---

### Tier 3 — Secondary reporting

Ballotpedia, Wikipedia, mainstream wire copy, trade press. Useful for context and background. Weaker for claims about money or power. Used when the harder tiers aren't available and the claim isn't load-bearing for an argument.

---

### Tier 4 — Partisan, single-sourced, or unverified

If all we have is a partisan outlet or an unverified claim, we flag it. You'll see tags on the source itself. We'd rather tell you we're unsure than tell you something we can't back up.

---

### Where the data comes from

Every profile is built from a combination of:

- **Government bulk data** — FEC donor-by-donor filings, USASpending contract awards, EPA emissions data, IRS 990 nonprofit filings, Congressional voting records, committee assignments, STOCK Act stock trades
- **Open-source news feeds** — RSS from investigative publications, parsed and matched to entities daily
- **Editorial review** — every verified profile gets a human read-through

If a claim can't be sourced, it doesn't ship. That's the rule.
