One shop. Three IEs. The Newsom-orbit machine.

Bearstar Strategies has been on three independent expenditures in the same 2026 California governor's primary. Pro-Swalwell. Anti-Steyer. Pro-Becerra. Polaris Campaigns took $13.78 million from the anti-Steyer side. The senior architect of their consulting hub was indicted on federal fraud charges four months before the launch. The Cal-Access expenditure files tell the story the candidate webpages don't.

Bearstar Strategies · three independent expenditures · same 2026 California governor primary

Bearstar at the center. Three IE arms reaching to three candidates in the same primary. Polaris Campaigns as the $13.78 million paid vendor on the anti-Steyer side. Williamson under federal indictment for billing Becerra's dormant campaign account, four months before Bearstar launches the pro-Becerra IE.

The protagonist of this beat is not a candidate. It is a political-consulting firm. Bearstar Strategies, Inc., the San Francisco shop built around Ace Smith, Sean Clegg, Juan Rodriguez, and Newsom's former chief of staff Jim DeBoo, has been on three different independent expenditure committees in the same 2026 California governor's primary. Pro-Eric Swalwell in early March. Anti-Tom Steyer in April. Pro-Xavier Becerra in May. The same firm. The same Newsom-orbit address book. The same architecture, applied to whichever candidate the donor class is investing in that month.

The single largest paid vendor on the anti-Steyer side, Polaris Campaigns, took $13,782,095 from committee 1489677 across five Cal-Access EXPN_CD line items in March and April 2026. The same firm took $50,870,203 from Newsom's Yes-on-Proposition-50 redistricting committee in 2025: the largest single payee on that campaign.

And the senior architect of the Democratic public-affairs hub these consultants belong to, "The Collaborative" co-architect Dana Williamson, was indicted on 23 federal counts in November 2025 for billing Xavier Becerra's dormant state campaign account for fabricated consulting services. KCRA-TV reported on May 1, 2026 that Bearstar's launch of the pro-Becerra IE was notable specifically because, as KCRA put it, "some of the same consultants now connected with Bearstar were previously linked with a group tied with that case."

This beat sits alongside two other 2026 California Governor primary investigations. $31 million to bury a class traitor documents the donor coalition spending against Tom Steyer that this consulting firm runs. Three Becerras documents the candidate the same firm now backs through an IE on the other side of the same race. This page documents the firm itself, in the structure that connects them.

Three IEs in one primary

Bearstar Strategies, Inc. (CA SOS document number 1998426, registered January 15, 1997, principal address 131 Stillman Street, San Francisco, formerly SCN Public Relations and SCRB Strategies) is the political-consulting firm most closely associated with Gavin Newsom's political career. Its named partners are Averell "Ace" Smith (CEO and Registered Agent), Juan V. Rodriguez (CFO and Director), and Sean Clegg (Secretary). Jim DeBoo, Newsom's former chief of staff, is closely associated through The Collaborative consulting hub but operates under his own entity, DeBoo Communications.

Across the 2026 California Democratic gubernatorial primary, Bearstar has been on three independent expenditures in sequence:

The pivot from pro-Swalwell to anti-Steyer and pro-Becerra was operational, not ideological. Politico California Playbook reported on April 17, 2026 that "Bearstar was behind an outside pro-Swalwell super PAC that launched in early March with TV ads." The same Politico Playbook noted in the same dispatch that Bearstar was "involved in a related super PAC created to oppose rival candidate Tom Steyer, an effort led by Jim DeBoo." When Swalwell exited the race after sexual-misconduct allegations, the pro-Swalwell IE dissolved and refunded donors. The anti-Steyer committee continued. Bearstar then launched the pro-Becerra IE on May 1.

Polaris Campaigns: the $13.78 million paid vendor

The single largest paid vendor on the anti-Steyer committee's expenditure file is not Bearstar. It is Polaris Campaigns, Inc., the Washington-DC-based media firm founded by Tom Pino. Polaris took $13,782,095 from committee 1489677 across five Cal-Access EXPN_CD line items, every one filed under the description "Digital Media/Oppose/Tom Steyer/Governor/Statewide":

Polaris's other major 2026 California work was the Yes-on-Proposition-50 redistricting committee (FPPC #1380675, sponsored by Governor Newsom), where Polaris received $50,870,203: the largest single payee on the campaign that passed Prop 50 with 64 percent statewide support. Bearstar served as Creative and Production affiliate on the same Prop 50 campaign while Polaris handled media buying. Per Campaigns & Elections' 2026 Reed Awards listing, this was a confirmed coordinated relationship between the two firms on the Newsom-orbit redistricting effort.

Jim DeBoo and the Newsom-orbit network

Jim DeBoo, who served as Governor Gavin Newsom's chief of staff, is identified across multiple named publications as the lead on the anti-Steyer committee effort:

  • Politico California Playbook, April 17, 2026: DeBoo "led" the anti-Steyer PAC effort.
  • Sacramento Bee, April 20-21, 2026 (Lia Russell): DeBoo described as "leading the California Is Not For Sale anti-Steyer super PAC."
  • LA Times, April 17, 2026: confirms Bearstar provides "media consulting to a committee running attack advertisements against environmentalist Tom Steyer" and that DeBoo "is helping on the anti-Steyer committee."

DeBoo does not appear on Bearstar's CA Secretary of State officer filings; he operates under DeBoo Communications. Under the No-on-33 ballot-measure coalition (sponsored by the California Apartment Association in 2024), DeBoo Communications received $225,000 while Bearstar Strategies received $598,019.89, per public Yes-on-33 documentation.

Other Bearstar partners on the 2025-2026 California work include Juan Rodriguez, who carried a separate documented related-party conflict: Consumer Watchdog reported on January 13, 2026 that Rodriguez held the media and consulting contract for Uber's anti-contingency-fee ballot initiative while engaged to Ramona Prieto, Uber's Head of Public Policy and Communications for the Western Region. Consumer Watchdog asked Uber's CEO and board to flag the related-party SEC disclosure obligation.

"Business owners and concerned citizens" was placeholder

The anti-Steyer committee originally registered on March 27, 2026 under a different name: "Californians for the People, sponsored by business owners and concerned citizens." Three days later, on March 30, an amended Form 410 changed the name to "California is Not for Sale, No on Steyer for Governor 2026, a Coalition of Housing Advocates, Labor and Small Business." Same FPPC ID. Same treasurer. Same Sacramento address.

Capitol Weekly reported on April 28-29, 2026 that the committee's only listed phone number routes to its treasurer firm, Deane & Company, which declined to comment on the committee. Deane & Company's president is Shawnda Deane, who previously served as treasurer for Tom Steyer's own Need to Impeach PAC, per Capital Research Center 2019 reporting. The Form 410 treasurer-of-record is Laiza Negrete, VP of Political Affairs for the California Association of Realtors. Negrete previously worked at Deane & Company, creating an organizational link between the two entities. CAR is the committee's $5 million direct funder.

The committee's public spokesperson is Amelia Matier, a Bay Area Democratic strategist who has worked on multiple California ballot measure and candidate campaigns including as spokesperson for the 2024 Yes-on-Proposition-5 affordable housing bond and the 2022 No-on-Proposition-30 campaign.

The "business owners and concerned citizens" language was generic Form 410 placeholder, common in California practice when a committee is registered before its principal funder coalition is finalized. The actual organizational structure (DeBoo organizing, Bearstar consulting, Matier as spokesperson, Deane & Company on treasury, Negrete as the Form 410 signatory bridging CAR and the treasurer firm) is consistent across the named-publication record. The "Coalition of Housing Advocates, Labor and Small Business" disclaimer language, like the original placeholder, is itself a soft-focus description: the committee's actual top funders are a utility giant (PG&E), the state's largest real-estate trade association (CAR), the California Chamber's IE arm (JOBSPAC), the homebuilders (CBIA), and a prison-guard union (CCPOA).

The Williamson indictment, four months earlier

In November 2025, four months before the anti-Steyer committee was registered, the LA Times (November 13, 2025) reported a federal indictment against Dana Williamson, Newsom's former chief of staff and the architect of the political-consulting hub known as "The Collaborative." Williamson was charged on 23 federal counts including bank fraud, tax fraud, and conspiracy, with allegations centered on billing Xavier Becerra's dormant state campaign account for fabricated consulting services and claiming luxury goods as business expenses. The fraud allegations against Williamson, taken at face value as the indictment alleges them, represent a documented pattern of the Newsom-orbit consulting infrastructure extracting money from a candidate's campaign account through fabricated billing.

Lobbyist Greg Campbell was named as a co-conspirator in the Williamson indictment. DeBoo, Clegg, and other Bearstar partners have not been charged. The LA Times reported that DeBoo and Clegg "have not been accused of any wrongdoing." Consumer Watchdog reported on January 13, 2026 that FBI surveillance notices had reportedly been received by "many associates and clients" of The Collaborative.

Bearstar's pro-Becerra IE launched on May 1, 2026. Becerra is the candidate whose dormant state campaign account Williamson was allegedly billing for fabricated consulting services in the indictment. The same consulting infrastructure that allegedly defrauded Becerra in 2024 is now operating an independent expenditure to elect him in 2026.

That last paragraph is the structural fact that KCRA-TV's May 1, 2026 reporting flagged. KCRA wrote that Bearstar's pro-Becerra launch was notable because "some of the same consultants now connected with Bearstar were previously linked with a group tied with that case." Whether Becerra knew the consulting infrastructure now supporting his IE is the same network that had allegedly defrauded him is a question the named-publication record cannot yet answer. Whether the FBI investigation extends beyond Williamson and Campbell to other Collaborative principals is also open.

The Collaborative

"The Collaborative" is the name used in named-publication coverage (originally Politico in September 2020, cited in the LA Times November 13, 2025 reporting) for the integrated Democratic public-affairs hub that includes:

  • Jim DeBoo as managing director
  • Sean Clegg (Bearstar Strategies partner) as a partner
  • Dana Williamson as architect (later Newsom Chief of Staff, indicted November 2025)
  • Greg Campbell as lobbyist (named co-conspirator in Williamson indictment)
  • Shannon Murphy of M Strategic Communications

The Collaborative is the institutional answer to a question California political journalism has been asking for years: where is the Newsom-orbit consulting infrastructure, and what does it do when Newsom is not personally on the ballot? The 2026 California gubernatorial primary is the answer in real time. Newsom is term-limited. The infrastructure stayed.

Why this is the structure of the race

Newsom is not on the 2026 California governor ballot. He is term-limited. But the political-consulting infrastructure that built and defended his career, including Bearstar Strategies, Polaris Campaigns, Jim DeBoo personally, Dana Williamson's Collaborative architecture, and Deane & Company on treasury, is the operational backbone of the 2026 race anyway.

They worked for Eric Swalwell while he was the Newsom-orbit pick. When Swalwell collapsed, they pivoted to opposing Tom Steyer (the donor-class threat) and supporting Xavier Becerra (the donor-class-acceptable alternative). The simultaneous involvement in three IEs across one primary is a structural fact about California political consulting under one-party-dominant conditions: there is one major Democratic consulting infrastructure, and it operates wherever the influence opportunities are.

The Williamson indictment is the load-bearing structural detail. It is not that Bearstar is criminally implicated. It is that the consulting infrastructure operating the 2026 race is the same network whose senior architect is currently under federal indictment for billing a candidate's dormant campaign account for fabricated consulting. The pattern is documented and ongoing. KCRA noticed. Consumer Watchdog noticed. Putting the full architecture into one piece is what no other named publication has yet done.

The candidates are interchangeable. Swalwell exits, Becerra rises, the operation continues. The protagonist is the firm.
