Steve Hilton's stock portfolio. The disclosed positions.

Companion page to /hilton. Hilton's March 2026 Form 700 lists about 130 named companies on Schedule A-1 plus three entities he owns more than 10% of on Schedule A-2. The portfolio is wide, public, and reveals what regulated industries the next California governor would have direct personal financial exposure to.

The point of a Form 700 is to make a candidate's financial exposure public before voters decide. Hilton's Schedule A-1 puts roughly 130 individual stock positions on the record, plus three entities he controls. Five concentrations in that list matter for the question of who he would govern for. Sierra is the headline; that's the main investigation. The other four concentrations are below.

Every entry on this page is taken directly from Hilton's candidate Form 700 filed March 6, 2026. Fair-market-value brackets follow the disclosure form's own ranges. The candidate signed the form under penalty of perjury. The list is what he himself disclosed.

What is not on the list

A complete disclosure portrait names what is absent as much as what is present. Hilton's Schedule A-1 does not list:

  • No direct cryptocurrency holdings. No Coinbase, Ripple, Circle, or any specific crypto exchange or token-holding entity. Hilton's campaign donors include cryptocurrency executives (Chris Larsen of Ripple at the legal max), but his own personal portfolio does not.
  • No other privately-held AI startups. No OpenAI direct stake, no Anthropic, no xAI. The AI-conflict-of-interest claim collapses entirely to Sierra. There is no second AI-startup holding to broaden the conflict.
  • No California cannabis-industry positions. The state's regulated cannabis industry has multiple publicly-traded operators (Cresco Labs, Canopy Growth, others) whose share prices move on California licensing decisions. None appear.
  • No California utility holdings beyond NextEra. No PG&E Corp, no Edison International, no Sempra. California's three big regulated utilities are absent from the list.
  • No tobacco. Philip Morris, Altria, BAT do not appear.

The absence list narrows the conflict-of-interest map. Hilton has structural exposure to fossil fuels, pharmaceuticals, defense, and global financial services. He has one privately-held AI position (Sierra) that sits in his top disclosure bracket. He has zero direct cryptocurrency, no other private-AI, and no in-state utility positions. The candidate's policy-by-policy posture should be read against this map, not against general "billionaire" or "tech bro" framings.
