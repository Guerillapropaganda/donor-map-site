What the May 1 California governor poll actually tells you.

Three forces are tipping the scales in the most expensive non-presidential primary in California history. The polling that just landed was paid for by one of them.

The poll

Mike Madrid published a Gudelunas Strategies poll on April 30: 800 likely voters, mixed-mode English/Spanish, 4/23-27. Becerra 24%, Hilton 23%, Steyer 15%, Bianco 13%, Porter 10%, Mahan 6%. Becerra moved nine points in two weeks. Steyer didn't move. Bianco lost a point. Porter dropped three.

Madrid disclosed the poll's commissioner in his thread: "the anti-Tom Steyer committee California Is Not For Sale." That's FPPC committee 1489677, registered 4/1/2026 as "Californians for the People, sponsored by Business Owners and Concerned Citizens." Same compliance shop (Nielsen Merksamer LLP, San Rafael) as the other major anti-Steyer committee, 1490270, which has received $9.975 million from Pacific Gas & Electric.

Force #2 — Silicon Valley billionaires, $8M+ for Matt Mahan

Mahan's own gubernatorial campaign committee has raised $0 from voters. The pro-Mahan IE PAC (FPPC 1487425, "California Back to Basics Supporting Matt Mahan") has raised at least $8 million from a tight cluster of Silicon Valley billionaires:

  • Michael Moritz (Sequoia Capital chairman): $2,000,000
  • Ashley Merrill (Lunya): $1,000,000
  • Michael Seibel (Y Combinator partner): $1,000,000
  • Patrick Collison (Stripe CEO): $990,000
  • John Pritzker (Pritzker family heir): $500,000
  • Brian Armstrong (Coinbase CEO): $500,000
  • Neil Mehta (Greenoaks Capital): $500,000
  • Brian Singerman (Founders Fund — Peter Thiel's firm): $250,000
  • Rick Caruso (LA real-estate developer): $250,000

Mahan polls at 6%. The deployed-vehicle model has a ceiling.

The Ripple hedge

Crypto-industry money runs through this race in three vehicles:

  • Christian "Chris" Larsen, executive chairman of Ripple, gave $39,200 to Porter (D) and $39,200 to Hilton (R) — same person, opposite parties, same race.
  • Brian Armstrong, Coinbase CEO, gave $500,000 to Mahan's IE PAC — Coinbase also funds Fairshake PAC, which spent $10M against Porter's 2024 Senate campaign.
  • The crypto industry isn't picking sides. It's owning all three lanes.

The Steyer self-fund context

Tom Steyer has personally contributed $133,776,122.76 to his own gubernatorial committee across 41 contribution events. He's outspending his identified opposition roughly 13 to 1. The polling shows him flat at 15% — the opposition is winning the narrative even though Steyer is winning the dollar count.

Where the race actually is

Two candidates are consolidating into the top-two primary advance: Becerra (D) and Hilton (R). Three candidates with substantial money apparatus are losing ground: Steyer (under negative blitz), Bianco (losing R lane to Hilton), Porter (squeezed). Mahan's $8M billionaire bloc isn't translating to votes.

And the Madrid caveat — "this poll was conducted before most of Steyer's multimillion-dollar negative hits started" — means the next poll likely shows Steyer dropping further. The Camp Fire utility's $10 million is buying the narrative even as Steyer's $134 million keeps buying TV time.
