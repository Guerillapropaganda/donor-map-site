What the May 1 California governor poll actually tells you.

A California business establishment coalition has put $21 million into a single committee to defeat Tom Steyer. The members: utility, Realtors, Chamber of Commerce, Building Industry, prison guards. Plus a coalition of Silicon Valley billionaires funding Matt Mahan. Plus the crypto industry hedging across all three lanes.

The poll

Mike Madrid published a Gudelunas Strategies poll on April 30: 800 likely voters, mixed-mode English/Spanish, fielded April 23-27. Becerra 24%, Hilton 23%, Steyer 15%, Bianco 13%, Porter 10%, Mahan 6%. Becerra moved nine points in two weeks. Steyer didn't move. Bianco lost a point. Porter dropped three.

Madrid's caveat: "this poll was conducted before most of Steyer's multi-million dollar negative hits started." The next public poll will probably show Steyer dropping further.

Force #2 · A second anti-Steyer committee paid for opposition polling

Separately, FPPC committee 1489677 ("Californians for the People, sponsored by Business Owners and Concerned Citizens" / alternate registered name "STEYER FOR GOVERNOR 2026 ... CALIFORNIA IS NOT FOR SALE, NO ON") paid David Binder Research, Inc. (San Francisco) $30,000 on April 2, 2026, with the filing description: "Survey/Oppose/Tom Steyer/Governor/Statewide."

The two anti-Steyer committees use TWO different compliance firms in a tiered structure: Nielsen Merksamer (San Rafael) files for 1490270 (the PG&E funnel) and both pro-Mahan IE PACs (1487425, 1488176). Deane & Company (Sacramento) files for 1489677 (the actual anti-Steyer spending committee) and CAR-adjacent vehicles like FAIRPAC. The compliance work is divided: NM handles utility/employer-coalition adjacencies; D&C handles Realtor / anti-Steyer-spending adjacencies. The same shop is filing for utility-aligned anti-Steyer money and tech-billionaire-aligned pro-Mahan money. That's not necessarily collusion; California's campaign-finance compliance ecosystem is centralized in a small number of firms. But it's a structural observation worth surfacing: the legal infrastructure for ostensibly opposing political projects runs through the same hands.

The Steyer self-fund context

Tom Steyer has personally contributed $133,776,122.76 to his own gubernatorial committee across 41 contribution events. He's outspending his identified opposition roughly 13 to 1. The polling shows him flat at 15%. The opposition is winning the narrative even though Steyer is winning the dollar count.

Where the race actually is, May 1, 2026

Two candidates are consolidating into the top-two primary advance: Becerra (D) and Hilton (R). Three candidates with substantial money apparatus are losing ground: Steyer (under negative blitz from PG&E + opposition polling), Bianco (losing R lane to Hilton's Fox-network reach), Porter (squeezed). Mahan's $8M billionaire bloc isn't translating to votes.

The Camp Fire utility's $10 million is buying the narrative even as Steyer's $134 million keeps buying TV time. The next poll will almost certainly show Steyer dropping further. Watch for whether Becerra holds the surge or whether the institutional-Democratic consolidation is itself fragile.
