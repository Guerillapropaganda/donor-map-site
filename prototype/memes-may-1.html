What the May 1 California governor poll actually tells you.

Three forces are tipping the scales in the most expensive non-presidential primary in California history. The Camp Fire utility is one of them. So is a coalition of Silicon Valley billionaires. And the crypto industry is hedging across all three lanes.

The poll

Mike Madrid published a Gudelunas Strategies poll on April 30: 800 likely voters, mixed-mode English/Spanish, fielded April 23-27. Becerra 24%, Hilton 23%, Steyer 15%, Bianco 13%, Porter 10%, Mahan 6%. Becerra moved nine points in two weeks. Steyer didn't move. Bianco lost a point. Porter dropped three.

Madrid's caveat: "this poll was conducted before most of Steyer's multi-million dollar negative hits started." The next public poll will probably show Steyer dropping further.

All contributions flowed into FPPC committee 1490270 ("Californians for Resilient and Affordable Energy, NO ON Steyer for Governor 2026") — the FPPC's required disclosure for committees primarily formed against a candidate. PG&E pleaded guilty to 84 counts of involuntary manslaughter for the Camp Fire in 2020. The same utility is now spending $10 million to defeat the only major candidate running on utility-and-climate accountability.

Force #2 — A second anti-Steyer committee paid for opposition polling

Separately, FPPC committee 1489677 ("Californians for the People, sponsored by Business Owners and Concerned Citizens" / alternate registered name "STEYER FOR GOVERNOR 2026 ... CALIFORNIA IS NOT FOR SALE, NO ON") paid David Binder Research, Inc. (San Francisco) $30,000 on April 2, 2026, with the filing description: "Survey/Oppose/Tom Steyer/Governor/Statewide."

Both anti-Steyer committees use the same compliance firm — Nielsen Merksamer LLP, San Rafael — which is also the operator behind both pro-Mahan IE PACs. The same shop is filing for utility-aligned anti-Steyer money and tech-billionaire-aligned pro-Mahan money. That's not necessarily collusion; California's campaign-finance compliance ecosystem is centralized in a small number of firms. But it's a structural observation worth surfacing: the legal infrastructure for ostensibly opposing political projects runs through the same hands.

Note on attribution. The polling that's driving the public narrative — Gudelunas Strategies for Mike Madrid — has a separate commissioner that has not been confirmed from primary FPPC records. The David Binder Research $30K is verified anti-Steyer polling. Don't conflate the two.

Mahan polls at 6%. The deployed-vehicle model has a ceiling.

The Ripple hedge — crypto industry across all three lanes

Crypto-industry money runs through this race in three vehicles:

  • Christian "Chris" Larsen, executive chairman of Ripple, gave $39,200 to Porter (D) and $39,200 to Hilton (R) — same person, opposite parties, same race.
  • Brian Armstrong, Coinbase CEO, gave $500,000 to Mahan's IE PAC. Coinbase also funds Fairshake PAC, which spent ~$10M against Porter's 2024 Senate campaign.
  • The crypto industry isn't picking sides. It's owning all three lanes simultaneously.

The Steyer self-fund context

Tom Steyer has personally contributed $133,776,122.76 to his own gubernatorial committee across 41 contribution events. He's outspending his identified opposition roughly 13 to 1. The polling shows him flat at 15% — the opposition is winning the narrative even though Steyer is winning the dollar count.

Where the race actually is, May 1, 2026

Two candidates are consolidating into the top-two primary advance: Becerra (D) and Hilton (R). Three candidates with substantial money apparatus are losing ground: Steyer (under negative blitz from PG&E + opposition polling), Bianco (losing R lane to Hilton's Fox-network reach), Porter (squeezed). Mahan's $8M billionaire bloc isn't translating to votes.

The Camp Fire utility's $10 million is buying the narrative even as Steyer's $134 million keeps buying TV time. The next poll will almost certainly show Steyer dropping further. Watch for whether Becerra holds the surge or whether the institutional-Democratic consolidation is itself fragile.
